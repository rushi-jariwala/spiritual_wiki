The Hingori Sutras

Five Flatmates You Cannot Evict

No matter how many lifetimes you try.
They don't pay rent. They don't do the dishes. They have been in my head — not just in this lifetime, but apparently in every single one that came before it. I have died and been reborn, and somehow, every single time, they found the new address.
01 / 05
Avi
Avidya  ·  Misperception
"I know exactly who I am. I'm Senior Manager, Finance, Andheri West."
Introduces himself at every party by his job title, his car, and his three strongly-held opinions. On a difficult day, he introduces himself as his mood. Ask him to describe himself without any external reference — no job, no status, no achievements — and he goes very quiet. He genuinely does not know. He has never been asked. He has never asked himself. The fundamental confusion of the packaging for the contents; the non-aatma mistaken for the aatma.
Primary flatmate. The others build their bedrooms on his foundation.
02 / 05
Asmi
Asmita  ·  Egoism
"I had a feeling the meeting would go well."
Heard an idea at dinner, repeated it with minor rephrasing three days later, and by the end of that week had fully convinced herself she invented it. "I told you so" is not just how she responds to bad news — it is her love language. She gets quietly wounded when you discover a film before she recommended it. Keeps a careful mental record of everything she said that turned out to be right. A glass of river water that has convinced itself it is not river water.
The rest of the river has no name on it. She finds this very suspicious.
03 / 05
Rag
Raga  ·  Attachment & Indulgence
"I'm not possessive. I just need things to stay exactly as they are."
Still listens to the album from a relationship that ended years ago. Has a jacket that hasn't fit in three years — not since a summer that was particularly good — and cannot throw it away. Holds a certain old grudge with something close to warmth, because at least it is his grudge, and it is familiar. Says yes to everything. The food delivery at 1am. The extra helping. The karma incurred is expensive to pay.
He will address it next life. He is quite sure of it.
04 / 05
Dev
Dvesha  ·  Aversion
"I have my reasons. They don't need revisiting."
Strong aversions to: mornings, that one colleague, the neighbour's dog, a specific type of weather, and the font choice in a presentation from 2021. None of these dislikes have been reviewed since the original incident. Has walked out of rooms that contained the answer. Has dismissed teachers because of their shoes. He and Rag together ensure the mind is permanently tilted — one tilts toward, the other tilts away.
Together with Rag, they have never once met in the middle.
05 / 05
Abhi
Abhinivesha  ·  Fear & Clinging to Life
"I have forty-seven insurance policies and I am still not comfortable."
Has Googled his symptoms eleven times today. Current diagnoses: four terminal conditions and one vitamin deficiency he has decided is the worst one. Will not board a plane. Has died many times — as a fish, a king, a corrupt administrator, possibly a frog — and has learned absolutely nothing reassuring from any of it. The fear has survived every single death, which is, philosophically, quite an achievement for something that was supposed to be irrational.
Tenure: multiple lifetimes. Departure date: unknown.

The CCTV Method — The Only Thing That Works
You cannot evict them. This has been attempted across civilisations and multiple philosophical traditions. It has not worked. What works is an imaginary CCTV camera.
"I myself found out noticing my interactions with the kleshas from outside was the best approach. It was like putting up a CCTV camera outside me to note what kleshas were afflicting me, when and how. Whenever this imaginary camera was on, I became aware of being a victim of one or a combination of kleshas. I would laugh at myself and the strength of the klesha would fade." — Hingori, Karma Sutra, Ch.7, p.114
Watch them. Do not engage them. When you catch Rag ordering the third delivery at 1am, simply note: "Ah. That's Rag." When Asmi quietly takes credit for how the evening went, let her claim it. The klesha wanted your 'I'. You did not provide it. The strength of it fades.

You still live with them. But you have stopped feeding them. That is where the work begins. The lease runs indefinitely. The camera, at least, is yours.
