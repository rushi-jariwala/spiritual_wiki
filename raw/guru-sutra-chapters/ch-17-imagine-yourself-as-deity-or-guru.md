        CHAPTER 17

    Imagine Yourself as
    a Deity or Guru




I have discovered that the best way of evaluating people, their
situations and issues, is by putting yourself in their shoes.

How would it feel to be worshipped if you were, the ‘Father who
art in heaven’ or god’s voice (in the burning bush) that Moses
heard on Mount Horeb, or the divinity that Guru Nanak Dev
dealt with underwater, or Krishna, the divine exemplar of The
Mahabharat?

As a deity, how would you like to be revered?

• Would you like people to visit your ashrams and offer
    flowers and sweets, money and jewels?

• Would you like people to sing hymns in your praise,
    perhaps the same one for 500 years? Would you like to
    hear the same prayer repeatedly recited by millions?

• Would you like loudspeakers outside your place of worship,
    blaring religious sermons in an out-of-tune, perhaps highly
    disturbing hoarse voice?

• Would you like to hear voices of adulation from your
    disciples?

Or

• Would you prefer that your disciples perform acts of
    kindness in order to please you or justify the inspiration
    they received from you?

• Would you like to see such people dedicate themselves to
    evolving their gunas and attitudes?

• Would you rather observe an effort towards living the
    philosophy you inspired in them?

A very significant question is: ‘What would YOU really like?’

Do consider it.

I would welcome a disciple who is determined to succeed
spiritually, and yet underplays his reverence, while being
sincere and loyal. I would prefer someone who does not spend
his time trying to impress me but follows instructions to
further spiritual practices and seva.

I would probably lock up the man who sings me a song in a
soundproof room; especially one who sings the same song
every day caring nothing for my boredom! (Newcomers with
heightened emotions can be tolerated, until on teaching them,
they learn better. That I can accept.)

Since people who pray with intense fervour seldom sit back
and observe themselves, it is wise to sometimes switch sides
and be the worshipped, only so that you can learn what to do
and how to worship.
