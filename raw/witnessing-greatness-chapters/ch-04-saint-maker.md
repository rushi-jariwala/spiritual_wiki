    Chapter 4
    Saint-maker



The enigma of how Gurudev chose his disciples is a peculiar tale,
as most of his chosen ones, including myself, hardly fit the mould
of traditional spiritual seekers. We were diverse, pulled from
different professions and academic backgrounds, with many
among us, like me, barely grasping the basics of spirituality. We
did not realise then that our outwardly ordinary attributes belied
the underlying connection we shared with the mahaguru.

Decades ago, I had a vision of Gurudev resting on an invisible
platform suspended in the sky with five or six of us in attendance.
We witnessed hundreds of bald monks dressed in cream-
coloured togas descending to earth on an invisible escalator.
I intuitively sensed that they would be part of a joint spiritual
mission of personal development and changing the ethos of
millions on this planet. These beings were born into whatever
wombs were available, aligning with the mahaguru at different
stages in life. Some wombs were designated for a second wave
of disciples, others for future generations. This spiritual relay is
set to unfold over approximately 250 years, with the overarching
goal of guiding individuals to reassess their attitudes and evolve
into saints.

Gurudev gathered the first wave of these scattered disciples in
this lifetime, drawing them back into his spiritual sphere. Some
found their way to him in pursuit of healing for themselves or
loved ones, while others came searching for guidance through
life's tumultuous journey. Upon their arrival, Gurudev welcomed
them with the knowing phrase, "Tu aa gaya, putt! (You have
come, my child)"—a simple greeting that held layers of

recognition mired in ancient bonds. Though they did not know
it then, these words were far from mere pleasantries; they were
an acknowledgement of a reunion centuries in the making.

Ravi ji shares a poignant observation: "The guru shoulders the
duty of guiding his disciples, binding his evolution to theirs.
He cannot progress until they do; they are in his charge, and he
cannot forsake them mid-journey. Gurudev made it clear that he
returned to this life to advance his spiritual family beyond the
point they had reached in their past lives."

Under Gurudev's guidance, the rekindled connections became
channels for spiritual awakening. He nurtured their spiritual
growth and equipped them with the tools and symbols of seva,
empowering them to extend their seva to others.

Puran ji reflects on the deep bonds that tied Gurudev's disciples
to him: "Gurudev often spoke of the 500-year journey to reunite
us in this lifetime. Our destinies were entwined long before."

Gurudev's gathering was a meticulous orchestration, drawing
individuals from across the globe, artfully cloaking destiny as
coincidence. Puran ji himself was privy to revelations about his
past connections with Gurudev—once during a period of seva
in Ujjain and then again in the era of Guru Nanak Dev, over 500
years prior.

Giri Lalwani ji, a disciple, was told by Gurudev, "Our bond
goes beyond a single lifetime and spans across many". He was
made to understand that this connection is not unique to them
but includes other disciples who have accompanied Gurudev
through his previous incarnations as a guru.

convergence of my past with my present, an unfolding of the
spiritual continuity that Gurudev foresaw with clarity.

past, where would be the wonder in that? Recite the Mahaguru
mantra as you drift into sleep and find out for yourself."

"While doing the mantra, I was transported back 150–170 years

Gurudev would visit and reside for months," Ravi ji recounts. "It

He drew the mansion and presented the sketch to Gurudev, who
confirmed it stood in Shahjahanpur, Uttar Pradesh. Fuelled by

the present reality was affirmed, except that the courtyard, once
paved with mud, had since been replaced with cement.

he mentioned that the blessings Santlal ji's mother bestowed
upon her son had brought forward their destined encounter by

Many of us approached the idea of meeting the mahaguru with
scepticism. The thought that a mere man, as he appeared, had
the power to transform our destinies, bring about remarkable
changes in our lives, and heal us and our loved ones seemed to
challenge our rational minds. Persuasion was often required for
us even to consider such a meeting. Yet, once we were in his
presence, we found ourselves drawn into his spiritual influence.

Lal Saheb's initiation into the sthan was marked by a nonchalant
curiosity rather than a quest for solutions, setting his story apart
from the rest. As a self-described average Indian-Canadian, his
life was filled with social festivities until a chance meeting at a
temple piqued his interest in a guru visiting Toronto. Following
an impromptu decision, Lal Saheb was embroiled in a seemingly
endless waiting game that tested his patience over several days.

"I had no pressing issues and no ailments. Merely a whim led
me to Gurudev's doorstep," he recalls. His bemusement with the
concept of a guru would soon take a turn. On the third day of his
persistent visits, he received half a cup of tea, a gesture he initially
thought of as a mistake. Nonetheless, he drank it, and later, when
called upon by Gurudev, he found himself agreeing to undertake
seva, a concept alien to him at the time. Though Lal Saheb was
unaware, the half-consumed tea was the medium through which
Gurudev imparted a part of his spiritual essence.

Months later, a visit to Gurgaon subjected Lal Saheb to a similar
pattern of wait and brief encounters, culminating in Gurudev's
cryptic instruction to be present when Mataji lit a jyot¹ at the sthan
at dawn on the first day of the new year. These interactions, though
fleeting, marked the beginning of Lal Saheb's transformation
into a devout disciple.

Malhotra ji's past lifetimes, adorned with sainthood, barely
lingered in his conscious memory. The powers he received from
Gurudev were to him as novel as a child's toy, and he approached
them with the same experimental curiosity.

with its horns, overturning it. Though a showcase of Malhotra ji's
emerging powers, this incident was a departure from Gurudev's
teachings, who consistently emphasised using one's spiritual gifts
for the greater good, not personal retribution. In this instance,
Malhotra ji's misuse of his abilities led to a stern reprimand from
the mahaguru.

Gurudev's quest for his predestined disciples was a spiritual
endeavour that demanded much of his attention. Those from
Himachal Pradesh and other North Indian states were more
receptive to his call, quickly blending into his spiritual milieu.
The Mumbaikars, however, presented a greater challenge with
their agnostic leanings and scepticism towards spirituality.

I, a typical Mumbai sceptic, witnessed Gurudev's miraculous
healing powers when he cured me of severe arthritis. Yet, I
could not develop faith. It took another five years and a series of
surreal events to finally shift my perspective. It was during my
time in Africa that the undeniable influence of Gurudev became
apparent. Every time I veered towards forbidden escapades,
an unseen force intervened. With each thwarted attempt at
impropriety, the image of the kada Gurudev had energised and
placed on my wrist years earlier would emerge in my mind's eye.
As peculiar as they were, these events occurred too frequently to
be mere chance. It dawned on me that these were not coincidences
but Gurudev's subtle influence at work. His invisible hand quite
masterfully browbeat me to faith.

The legacy of the mahaguru as a saint-maker endures, with his
teachings continuing to cultivate saints with the same fervour
as during his lifetime. Numerous individuals who have never
encountered him physically but are linked to him from previous
lifetimes are reawakening to the deep connections they share.

was walking among these rows, invisible to everyone but me.
When I touched his feet, he blessed me and said, 'Putt, tera mera

For some, the path is smooth; for others, it is challenging, yet
several others seamlessly blend into the mix. Gurudev's saint-

purpose. United by this communal journey, our gratitude
towards him is not just a fleeting emotion but a perpetual state
of being. We bask not only in his reflected glory but also in the
collective radiance of our transformed selves, forever indebted to
his transformative touch.
