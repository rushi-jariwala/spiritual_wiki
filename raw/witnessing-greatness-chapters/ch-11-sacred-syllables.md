The Mahagayatri mantra, a sacred verse from the Rig Veda,¹
stands as one of the most powerful mantras in existence. Its
composition follows the Gayatri metre, known for its distinctive
arrangement: 24 syllables divided into three lines, each with eight
syllables. This mantra is a potent call to the solar deity Savitur,
seeking illumination of the intellect as one progresses towards
self-realisation.

The mantra's discovery is attributed to Brahmarishi Vishwamitra,
who, through intense and prolonged meditation, was bestowed
