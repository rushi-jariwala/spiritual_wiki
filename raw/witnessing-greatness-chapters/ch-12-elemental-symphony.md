In the late 1980s, we encountered a significant hurdle at the
Khandsa farm: the only available water was saline and unsuitable
for agriculture. Recognising the critical need for a viable water
source, Gurudev confidently guided us to a particular spot on
the farm and instructed us to dig. To our astonishment, we
unearthed an underground reservoir of water directly beneath
that spot. As we dug further, freshwater surfaced, promising
sustainable irrigation for the farm's future.

Years earlier, Gurudev had faced a similar predicament in an arid
region of Renuka, where he and his team established a soil survey
camp. Alongside Bittu ji and a labourer, he ascended a nearby
mountain. On their descent, Gurudev instructed Bittu ji to trace
the path he walked with a stick, outlining where a canal could be
dug. He directed the labourer to follow this line with his digging.
Miraculously, as the labourer dug, water began to surge forth,
forming a canal that flowed directly to their campsite. This newly
sourced water not only sustained them during their stay but also
revitalised the dry terrain into a lush green sanctuary. However,
once they vacated the camp, the water receded to its source, and
the land reverted to its barren state.

come with me."
