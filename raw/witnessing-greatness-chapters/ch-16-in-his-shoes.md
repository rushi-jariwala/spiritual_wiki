    Chapter 16
    In His Shoes





The image that most held of Gurudev was shaped by stories
exchanged in hushed tones and reverent whispers. When devotees
approached him, they brought their problems and a plethora
of spiritual expectations. They sought his aid in healing and
transforming their circumstances by tapping into the reservoir
of his spiritual powers—powers he had painstakingly cultivated
not just over the years but lifetimes.

In the early stages of his journey as a healer, the ailments of those
he healed often left a residual mark on his aura. He would find
himself grappling with the very negativity he sought to alleviate
from others. Moreover, brief encounters at public events posed
another challenge: remembering the sea of faces that sought his
guidance. Most times, he could not meet many devotees since
they would frequent the sthans he established outside Gurgaon.
To connect with them, he either relied on the insights of his
disciples or embarked on astral journeys.

At the peak, Gurudev interacted with lakhs of people monthly.
There were instances where he catered to nearly 50,000 individuals
in a space as tiny as 600 square feet. The management of such
large gatherings was a challenge due to organisational limitations.
Furthermore, he had to navigate the complex interplay of forces,
both benevolent and malevolent. On numerous occasions, he had
to tactfully engage with other powers. The roadblocks came in
many forms, from spirit attacks and black magic to disgruntled
spiritual masters.

Countless beings placed their faith in the mahaguru, hoping
he would answer their prayers. Though expressing these
expectations in words is simple, the weight of such responsibility
was enormous. In addition to his spiritual duties, Gurudev led an
ordinary, middle-class life. He held a regular job, attended camps
for half the year, and maintained a close bond with his family
while appearing to lead an average life.

Those who recognised Gurudev's spiritual powers felt a deep
connection with him. Being in his position is a unique privilege,
yet I often wonder how challenging it must have been. Nitin
Gadekar ji observes, "Gurudev's life exemplified his relentless
commitment to seva. Catering to the varied and sometimes
unreasonable demands of individuals required unparalleled
patience. Some even approached him with trivial requests like
choosing a television! Yet, he remained composed and attentive
to everyone's needs." He continues with a smile, "His regular job
was perhaps a respite, offering a break from these overwhelming
spiritual responsibilities."

Gurudev's boundless generosity was evident in all aspects of his
life. Despite his modest earnings, he gave freely, even when he
knew that not all requests were genuine.

In the early years, his close companions, Dwarkanath ji
and Nagpal ji, witnessed his selflessness first-hand. As his
spiritual family grew, Gurudev generously supported and fed
his disciples. He viewed seva as the highest form of goodwill,
never a transaction or exchange, and therefore never accepted
anything for himself. While he occasionally allowed his disciples
to contribute towards seva, his family took on the responsibility
of covering the personal expenses of many disciples who made
his home their abode.

Gurudev's financial approach might seem imprudent to some,
as he often spent more than he earned. Yet, somehow, he always
managed to make ends meet. Mataji expresses her admiration
for her husband's generosity: "He never hesitated to help those in
need. Whether it was a small or large amount, he gave willingly
and without reservation."

Gurudev viewed money not as a possession to be owned, but
as a tool for improving the lives of others. He treated his wife's
earnings with respect, and at work, he was open-handed with
his salary. Even though it seemed unlikely they could afford to
support a family and the activities of a sthan given their limited
resources, they still found a way to get by. Even when faced
with financial constraints, the mahaguru never faltered. His
unwavering integrity ensured that he always found a way.

Mataji was adept at managing their finances with utmost caution
and discernment. She bore the brunt of financial tightness with
grace and wit. Gurudev's limitless generosity frequently led him
to come home with an empty wallet on payday. Consequently,
it was crucial to ensure that a portion of his monthly salary
reached her, enabling her to manage the household expenses. His
disciples recognised this dynamic and decided to streamline the
process. They ensured a part of Gurudev's salary went directly to
Mataji, allowing her to manage the house while he continued his
philanthropic endeavours.

Though puzzling to many, Gurudev's choice to refuse monetary
offerings and donations earned him deep respect. His story is
a remarkable example of human grit and persistence through
obstacles that could quickly derail one's chosen path. Each
hurdle he faced in this journey was daunting, appearing nearly
insurmountable. Surrounded by a unique circle of ethereal
mentors, allies, deities, and watchful ordinary individuals, he
navigated his path with remarkable resilience.

Initially, he began his seva in a humble manner, much like a
wandering saint, offering seva in people's homes and at the
tea and juice stall beneath his office. As time progressed, he
ingeniously merged his seva with his professional life, doing seva
wherever his work led him.

        •

Gurudev's mission to foster faith added obstacles because he
dressed and spoke like an ordinary person. Convincing others to
trust someone who looked no different from anyone else in the
crowd was inherently challenging. Nevertheless, after just a few
interactions, many were captivated by his authenticity and were
drawn towards him. His way of life and the palpable energy of his
aura swayed their beliefs and led them to become his devotees.

Nikku ji shares an interesting observation: "Whether Gurudev
went to bed at 2 am or 3 am, he was always up by 5 am. Typically,
he would head out for work around 7.30 or 8 am, reaching his
office by 9 or 9.30 am. Each morning, people would gather
outside his home, eagerly awaiting a glimpse of him. To make
it to his office on time, he would sometimes slip out by jumping
over the back wall instead of using the main gate."

Eventually, a sliding door was installed at the back of the house to

One can compare Gurudev's adaptability to a chameleon,
shifting hues rapidly. People approached him bearing a
myriad of emotions. When their prayers bore fruit, they
would exude joy; yet, in trying times, they sought solace in
sharing their sorrows with him. For Gurudev, seamlessly
transitioning between these emotional extremes was a
requisite, as challenging as it might have been.

Another facet of his duty stemmed from heightened
expectations. Many, having heard stories of swift relief
bestowed upon others, anticipated similar expediency for
themselves. Yet, Gurudev, in his wisdom, often took a more
measured approach, beckoning them to the sthan multiple
times, facilitating healing at its natural pace. He employed
various methods, such as leveraging their faith and patience,
when negotiating with advanced deities on their behalf.

be incredibly challenging for me if I were in his position."

Scaling mountain peaks is a formidable challenge, demanding
immense stamina. Each step reflects the climber's unyielding
determination and resilience. Upon conquering the summit,
the climber joins the ranks of an elite few who have triumphed
over the same colossal heights. Though fraught with trials, this
journey culminates in a sense of achievement: the recognition
that every obstacle overcome was a necessary sacrifice for the
majestic reward that lay in wait.

Those privileged to have known Gurudev concur that relentless
trials marked his existence. Yet, despite facing daunting
adversities, his legacy shines as a source of inspiration to many.
