    Shiv



One of the most misunderstood concepts in Indian philosophy
is 'Shiv'. Shiv is often humanised and envisioned as a physical
entity residing in places such as Mount Kailash, Amarnath,
or Kedarnath. However, this widely recognised depiction
corresponds to Shankar, who embodies a form in which Shiv
consciousness is fully realised. Fundamentally, Shiv signifies a
collection of qualities, attributes, and capabilities that emerge
at the pinnacle of spiritual consciousness. Individuals in whom
these traits manifest are considered embodiments of Shiv,
transcending the limitations of a mere physical form.

Guru Vashisht explains that Shiv is the infrastructure for the play
of Shakti, where Shakti is the dynamic force driving creation. Shiv
is a potent, static, formless energy that permeates everything but
remains unnoticed by many. While everyone holds the potential
to uncover this divine essence within themselves, only a select
few achieve this realisation.

Accessing and connecting with Shiv consciousness demands
profound spiritual development and awareness. To reach this
state of being, one must elevate one's kundalini¹ beyond the Ajna
chakra, located at the third eye on the forehead. Advancing the
kundalini past this point allows individuals to tap into subtler
levels of consciousness, a journey open to anyone, regardless of
his or her religious beliefs.

paper and pencil. Let me explain the meaning of the OM and its

utpatti¹ and mirrors the foetal position of a child in the womb."

extends from these semi-circles represents Ganesh, who embodies

Gurudev would tailor his techniques, inspiring and motivating
individuals according to their unique needs and abilities. With
adept skill, he employed practical demonstrations to ignite the
passion of those like Ravi ji, who could be easily motivated.
However, for individuals like me, Santosh ji of Nadaun, Gupta ji
from Parwanoo and his family, and Captain Sharma of Mumbai,
who were less ambitious, it was an automatic transmission.

The mahaguru's extraordinary skill of transferring power
symbols at will is an ability that many find both astonishing and
complex to grasp. He extended this distinctive capability to his
disciples, empowering them to emulate this impressive feat.

FC Sharma ji vividly recalls one of the methods Gurudev used
for transferring these power symbols: "Gurudev would show the
OM in his hand to an individual, promising that the same symbol
would appear on their hand the next day. And it invariably did!"

        235

In certain instances, this power transfer occurred instantaneously.
During a memorable meeting, Gurudev summoned Gupta ji of
Parwanoo and his family, and declared, "I have bestowed the OM
upon each of you". To their surprise, the OM symbol materialised
on everyone's hands, leaving them awestruck.

The mahaguru imparted these symbols so subtly that we only
became aware of the gifts we had received as he led us to uncover
and realise their presence within us.

Gurudev's transfer of his divine powers was contingent upon
his discernment of a disciple's worthiness. After he departed
from the earthly realm, some disciples initially hesitated about
passing on these powers and sacred symbols. Yet, upon deep
contemplation, many disciples chose successors, ensuring the
continuation of these remarkable abilities for future generations.
This act of succession honours Gurudev's legacy and secures the
ongoing journey of spiritual enlightenment and seva.
