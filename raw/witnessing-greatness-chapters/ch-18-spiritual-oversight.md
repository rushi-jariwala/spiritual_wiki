Steering disciples entangled in worldly desires toward self-
realisation was a significant undertaking for Gurudev. It required
his relentless commitment and the disciple's steadfast faith. While
the mahaguru, with his acute insight, had already discerned the
disciple's potential and pitfalls, he still posed tests, not as an
assessment for his knowledge, but as a mirror for the disciple to
introspect and self-reflect.

        •

Gurudev's spiritual presence was so formidable that it could
unnerve men of steel. Yet, his intention was always to uplift and
inspire. He did not merely want his disciples to reach his spiritual
level but to surpass it. This explains his boundless patience and
unyielding dedication to guiding them.

His tests were so subtle that we were often unaware that we were
being evaluated. One day, I observed how he assessed Rajpal ji at
his Curzon Road office. As we sat, a guest arrived, and Gurudev
said, "Take Rajpal's car. Use it and return it later". After the
guest left, Gurudev, with a twinkle in his eye, confided, "I had
previously told Rajpal not to lend his car. Now, after instructing
someone to use it, let us observe his reaction."

I was eager to see how Rajpal ji would fare. He entered Gurudev's
office and promptly placed the car keys on the table, stating,
"Gurudev, here are the keys". When he exited the room, Gurudev

ground on which you stand, and you'll find your voice'. Rajpal ji
complied without hesitation, and his voice was restored."

    realm testifies to their spiritual depth, vast understanding, and
    transformative influence.

In their initial journey, many disciples wrestled with grasping
the complexities of disciplehood, resulting in occasional lapses.
Suresh Kohli ji shares a moving story from those early days: "I
remember an episode with Shambhu ji, who was battling cancer.
One afternoon, Gurudev decided to cure him. Both Shambhu
ji and Nagpal ji were present. Gurudev blessed jal and offered
it to Shambhu ji, urging him to drink it. However, Shambhu ji
hesitated, believing he saw worms in the glass. Despite Gurudev's
reassurances of the water's purity, Shambhu ji declined and did
not drink the jal."

Recognising the fragile nature of human faith, Gurudev remarked
that perhaps Shambhu ji's destiny was not to be healed that day.
He then offered the water to Nagpal ji, who drank it trustingly
without a second thought.

For Shambhu ji, yielding to momentary doubts instead of
maintaining steadfast faith had catastrophic consequences; it
ultimately cost him an extension of life.

Dr Shankarnarayan ji shares an incident involving RC Malhotra
ji, a junior technical assistant in his office: "I held Malhotra ji
in high esteem". This respect was challenged when Gurudev
instructed him to obtain a mantra from Malhotra ji. However,
shortly afterwards, the mahaguru remarked, "Malhotra is a
nobody; there is no need to touch his feet". Such conflicting
guidance left Shankarnarayan ji in an ethical dilemma.

The next day, in an unforeseen turn of events, Malhotra ji
visited Shankarnarayan ji's home. Without much thought,
Shankarnarayan ji, guided by instinct, respectfully touched
Malhotra ji's feet. As he later reflected on his gesture, a realisation
struck him: he had acted contrary to Gurudev's advice. He became
anxious about the mahaguru's possible reaction. Yet, when they
met at the office, Gurudev greeted him with an approving smile.

     "Today, you've won me over", he remarked. At that moment,
     Shankarnarayan ji understood that Gurudev had tested his
     humility and sincerity—a test he had fortunately passed.

     Gurudev had a unique way of guiding his disciples. He often
     employed what appeared as contradictions in his teachings,
     challenging the perceptions and convictions of those he
     mentored. These apparent paradoxes were not whimsical but
     intricately crafted to see who could pierce through the surface
     and grasp the underlying essence. He observed the depth of
     their spiritual understanding by occasionally endorsing their
     superficial tendencies. While many stumbled on this path, it
     gave Gurudev valuable insights, allowing him to fine-tune his
     guidance. Despite these hurdles, his unwavering commitment
     to his disciples' spiritual development underscored his relentless
     pursuit of their advancement.

        •

     Disciplehood is a complex journey. Interacting with a siddh
     guru's subconscious power demands tact and experience. Due to
     the guru's approachable demeanour, there is a risk of losing sight
     of formal boundaries, leading to guru avhelna.

     Bade Jain Saheb learnt this lesson the hard way. He was influenced
     by an energy that appeared before him as a manifestation of Shiv.
     This overwhelming experience disrupted his mental balance,
     leading him to challenge Gurudev. This caused him to go insane
     and publicly fall from grace. Tragically, this event also marked
     the plateau of his spiritual journey. Although Gurudev extended
     his compassion and forgiveness, the incident cast a shadow over
     Bade Jain Saheb's reputation. Some disciples even harboured
     lingering resentment, resulting in diminished respect.

     Bittu ji offers an account of this turn of events. "Once revered,
     Bade Jain Saheb sadly became the subject of ridicule. Some
     younger disciples even resorted to mocking his appearance and

belittling him openly". I was dismayed by the disrespect directed

Jain Saheb in clearing his own karmic slate!

Guru avhelna is more than just upsetting or going against the
guru. It combines disrespect, lying, and other wrongs. Though
they may seem harsh, the consequences help correct and prevent
these mistakes from recurring. Viewed in this light, such
punishment becomes an indirect blessing from the guru—a
sentiment I have come to understand, having experienced this
backhanded act of grace alongside Rajpal ji, Santlal ji, Bade Jain
Saheb, Malhotra ji, and several others.

I was closely associated with my gurubhai Santlal ji. Our spiritual
journeys intersected when we simultaneously attained guru
charan prapti, signalling the activation of our forehead chakra
and an elevation of our conscious awareness.

Santlal ji's initial spiritual journey was tumultuous. Lying to
Gurudev led to his peculiar punishment—a constant itch. This
affliction inadvertently compounded his troubles, especially with
a female colleague at his workplace.

visited the sthan. I had a persistent itch that became a problem.

labelling me uncouth because of my scratching. I desperately
sought Gurudev's help, saying, 'I need your grace'. I explained
my distressing condition and how I would scratch myself with
an iron rod until I bled, staining my shirts and vests. I confided
in him about the lady's accusations and sought his judgment.
Gurudev laughed when he heard my story. He assured me,

The mahaguru continues to test, assess, and guide his
disciples and their downlines. Shyam Gala ji and Roshini ji's

Shyam ji, who has been a devoted follower of the sthan
from his youth and now participates in seva from the
United States, recounts a dream that affirms this ongoing
spiritual oversight: "I stood next to Gurudev, who was
deeply immersed in meditation. Suddenly, a group entered
the room, radiating negative energies. Instinctively, I began
chanting the Mahagayatri mantra to protect us". Shyam ji

Similarly, Roshini ji shares a transformative dream that occurred
on the morning of Guru Purnima, which impacted her spiritual
commitment. "In my dream, I observed Gurudev and Guruji
discussing my spiritual progress. At the time, I had become
negligent about seva," she reveals. She distinctly remembers
Gurudev commenting, "She has become complacent", which her
Guru agreed with.

"Awakening the next morning, I was embarrassed about my
complacency and determined to change my ways," she admits.
"This was a clear indication that Gurudev remains watchful,
though not physically present, evaluating our progress and
steering us towards becoming evolved versions of ourselves."

        •

The journey of disciplehood is akin to navigating a labyrinthine
path towards self-realisation. Throughout this complex journey,
disciples encounter a myriad of tests and challenges. Despite the
diverse forms these trials may take, Gurudev's intention is always
constructive—to teach, inspire, and elevate. He continues to lead
his spiritual family beyond their inherent limitations, guiding
them toward fulfilling their spiritual destinies.
