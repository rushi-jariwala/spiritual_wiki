    Chapter 20
    Sculpting Divinity




A simple lump of clay, at first glance, appears ordinary. Yet, in the
hands of a master sculptor, this malleable substance transforms
into a work of stunning beauty that belies its ordinary beginnings.
With his vision and skill, the sculptor reveals the hidden potential
within the clay. Similarly, Gurudev wielded a transformative
influence over his disciples' minds. Much like a skilled puppeteer
with a masterful grip on the strings, he delicately steered the
course of their thoughts, beliefs, and actions. His guidance was
subtle yet powerful, unlocking their inherent potential and
directing them toward self-realisation.

Kishanmohan ji reflects on his transformation after meeting the
mahaguru, contrasting his earlier materialistic dreams with his
spiritual awakening: "In 1970, I dreamt of accumulating wealth. I
imagined myself driving an Impala and living a life of luxury. But
everything changed when I met Gurudev. Before meeting him, I
had even considered becoming an Aghori, driven by a desire to
punish those who had wronged me. I found an Aghori master
who began teaching me his ways, but midway through, he told
me this path was not mine to follow. He said I would one day
learn from a siddh guru whose arrival in my life was still some
time away.

"When I met Gurudev, I understood that one encounters a
siddh guru only when destined for something beyond worldly
desires. Gurudev took someone like me, a ruffian, and turned
him into a spiritualist. Thanks to him, my life took a 180-degree

        257

      turn. I discovered something more substantial than materialistic
      wealth—the chance to do seva, a calling far surpassing my
      previous ambitions. But this transformation is a journey, a multi-
      lifetime process, and a path of ceaseless spiritual endeavour."

      Gurudev was a visionary, painting our lives with a vibrant shade
      of blue—a colour symbolising Shiv's divine nature in Hinduism.
      He saw each of us as significant players in this vast 'Blue Man
      Show', with the promise of unimpeded growth if we did not resist
      the transformative brushstrokes of his spiritual teachings.

      His guidance was unwavering and precise, advocating for fairness
      and ethical conduct. Moreover, he championed the concept of a
      debt-free lifestyle, urging us to live within our means and be free
      from the shackles of obligation or the need to curry favours. He
      emphasised, "We must be givers, not takers."

      Kishanmohan ji reflects on this further, saying, "He blessed me
      with self-reliance, teaching me to live within my means, even if it
      meant surviving for days on a handful of chickpeas. Over these
      years, I have never felt compelled to ask for personal favours
      from Gurudev. I have only requested his guidance and protection
      for others."

      Those who ask not for themselves but for others benefit from the
      concept of 'not asking'.

      friends—Srikrishna Deolekar, Suresh Prabhu, Pradeep Sethi,
      Shyam Dhumatkar, their wives, and I—began gathering at Khar
      Gymkhana for table tennis. Strangely enough, most of us were
      not even keen on the game. Yet, we found ourselves drawn
      together in the evenings. It was during these gatherings that we
      started sharing stories about Gurudev. Pradeep used to mimic
      him humorously, offering, 'Show me your forehead, and I'll heal

your headache', which always got us laughing. It was all in
jest; none of us took it seriously.

"However, everything changed when Pradeep got engaged.
We all went to his engagement, and that is when we had
this incredible meeting with Gurudev. That was the exact
moment when each of us truly began our journey in this
lifetime with him."

After this encounter, Nitin ji felt an inexplicable pull
towards the mahaguru. "Coming from a family with a
strong preference for non-vegetarian cuisine, where fish or
meat graced nearly every meal, my decision to leave non-
vegetarian food a few months after meeting Gurudev marked
a significant shift. Typically, I am not one to put anyone on a
pedestal. However, Gurudev was an exception. His presence
was magnetic, captivating my attention entirely. Whenever
he was around, it felt as though he was the sole focus of the
room, making it impossible for me to notice anyone else or
think about anything but him.

"Normally, when you visit a temple, you are still aware of your
surroundings. But with Gurudev, it was a completely different
experience. I would forget everything else. Sometimes, I
would not even remember my own identity as Nitin. All I
knew was that I existed and was in the presence of a great
being. This experience was so elevated that it continually
compelled me to return to him."

In the mahaguru's presence, we experienced a nurturing
influence similar to trees flourishing near a sandalwood
tree. His aura was so potent that it impacted the atmosphere
around him, allowing those in his presence to absorb the scent
of his spiritual being. Being near him was like an effortless
immersion in his wisdom and insights. Nitin ji articulates
the mahaguru's impact precisely, "I think Gurudev was a far
greater force than we would like to believe."

     Many of us were fortunate to receive Gurudev's corrective
     guidance, as his reprimands steered us towards the right path.
     On one occasion, when Rajpal ji visited Gurudev, the mahaguru
     blessed him by placing his hand on Rajpal ji's head. As Rajpal ji
     prepared to leave the room, Gurudev remarked, "Raje, you have
     come here after mistreating your factory worker."

     Rajpal ji was puzzled at Gurudev's knowledge of the incident,
     especially in an era without mobile phones or WhatsApp.
     Gurudev enlightened him, "I am your guru. I can tune in to see
     you whenever I feel inclined."

     In an attempt to rationalise his actions, Rajpal ji recounted
     how the factory worker's mistake had financially impacted the
     company. Gurudev responded with a gentle rebuke, "Everyone
     errs, my son. No one is immune to making mistakes or facing
     losses. Remember, this man toils in your factory and contributes
     to your business, yet you spoke to him with undue harshness."

     Rajpal ji offered his defence: "Gurudev, I compensate him for the
     work he does."

     Gurudev offered a deep insight, "The wages he receives are his
     destiny. Your duty is to provide those wages, while his is to earn
     them. He manufactures products in your factory, enabling you to
     reap profits and live a life of luxury. However, your actions today
     placed a debt upon you."

     The mahaguru emphasised through his actions and teachings
     that seva must be recognised as the shared destiny of the server

260

and the served. Our fortunate alignment of stars cast us in the
role of servers, yet it was crucial to understand that we were not
architects of fate but simply channels through which it flowed.
This insight is a constant reminder that destiny works through
us, not because of us, ensuring humility remains a cornerstone
of our actions.

Rajpal ji shares another anecdote that reveals the depth of
Gurudev's insight: "I sought Gurudev's blessings before flying
to Singapore for work. After I had touched his feet, he looked at
me intently and said, 'My son is off to deceive the world.'"

Confused, Rajpal ji responded, "Deceive? I have not misled
anyone." To which Gurudev enigmatically replied, "If not yet,
then you are about to."

Baffled by this cryptic statement, Rajpal ji explained the purpose
of his trip. He was visiting a factory in Singapore to examine a
dye machine capable of crafting sinks, drain boards, and bowls
from a single sheet without welding. It was then that Gurudev
pointed out that Rajpal ji had concealed the true intent of his
visit. Instead of revealing his genuine interest in the technology,
he had misrepresented himself as a potential buyer interested
in purchasing 10,000 sinks.

Rajpal ji, still puzzled, sought clarification from Gurudev, "How
am I deceiving them?" Gurudev clarified, "You are not just
misleading them; you are causing them disappointment. They
are eagerly preparing for your arrival, arranging transportation
from the airport, yet your intentions are not to buy. You are
merely visiting to observe, and they are oblivious."

Rajpal ji acknowledged the truth in the mahaguru's words,
"Gurudev, you are absolutely right. I want to learn the
technology as I do not know how to create a dye that can
produce both the bowl and drain board from the same sheet".
Gurudev responded with assurance, "My son, I will teach you,"
prompting Rajpal ji to cancel his planned visit to Singapore.

Fifteen days later, on Bada Guruvar, Rajpal ji took a short break
from seva. He recalls, "As I lay resting with my eyes closed, the
dye machine lingered in my thoughts. As I drifted to sleep, a
strikingly detailed blueprint of the dye unfolded before me,
complete with dimensions, radii, and curvatures!"

This unexpected vision jolted him back to wakefulness. Upon
opening his eyes, he found himself in a room at the sthan in
Gurgaon. Intriguingly, the precise blueprints reappeared every
time he closed his eyes, vividly etched in his mind's eye.

The next day, filled with inspiration, Rajpal ji hurried to his
factory. He instructed his head foreman, Ratan Singh, to fabricate
the dye following the specs of the blueprint he had envisioned.
However, Ratan Singh expressed concerns, believing the design
was impractical for production and could lead to financial losses.
Despite these objections, Rajpal ji remained adamant. Yielding
to his boss' firm resolve, Ratan Singh manufactured the dye using
the unique design.

Concluding his captivating tale, Rajpal ji shares, "Once we made
the dye, we attached it to the press and initiated a test run. To our
amazement, it worked flawlessly in just one attempt. Can you
believe that Gurudev gave me the complete blueprint of this dye
while I was asleep in Gurgaon?!"

Normally, Gurudev would engineer the float, not the sink.
By this, I mean that he advocated for ethical behaviour in the
face of challenges, encouraging us to rise above rather than
be overwhelmed by circumstances. He dedicated ample time
to guiding us through the process of transcending our lower
impulses. Our inherent divinity was confined within our limited
selves. Gurudev acted as the key, unlocking this inner sanctum
and liberating our spiritual potential.

Sushila Chaudhary's story is a chronicle of faith and her
husband's remarkable transformation. Her husband, a staunch
atheist, firmly disbelieved in religion. He accompanied Sushila
ji to sacred places like Gurudwara Bangla Saheb and Hanuman
temple, but only as a passive observer, always staying outside to
guard her shoes.

Four years following the birth of her daughter Ritu, Sushila ji
learnt from her doctors that future pregnancies were improbable
due to her having just one operational fallopian tube. Working
in the same office as Gurudev allowed her to seek his guidance.
During their first meeting, she openly shared her struggles and
the medical conclusion that another pregnancy was improbable.
Gurudev, however, brushed aside these medical assessments.
"Forget what the doctors say. You will have three sons," he
proclaimed. This statement overwhelmed Sushila ji with joy.
Gurudev's prophecy materialised with the birth of her two
sons, and when he foresaw a third, Sushila ji, content with her
expanded family, respectfully declined the possibility, focusing
only on her existing blessings.

She shares her husband's gradual shift in beliefs: "After Gurudev
assured me of having a child within a year, I started making
trips to the Gurgaon sthan by bus. It was then that my husband,
appalled by the condition of the buses, remarked, 'How can you
travel in these stinking buses? Let us see who this person is for
whom you go so far and in such unpleasant conditions'. That
is when he started driving me to Gurgaon. There, he directly
observed Gurudev's extraordinary healing powers.

"Later, he accompanied me to Odi in Himachal Pradesh, where
Gurudev had established a sthan. He was shocked to find people
arriving on stretchers and leaving on their feet, completely
healed. These experiences deeply impacted him and resulted in
him developing staunch faith. He once said of Gurudev, 'There
is no other guru on Earth with such powers, nor will there ever
be'. In time, his faith in Gurudev grew even stronger than mine."

     blessings and guidance, helping them to achieve their life goals.
     Chaudhary ji's spiritual contributions have transcended his

     Twenty-three-year-old Tony was the quintessential party-goer.
     He revelled in the pleasures of parties, enjoyed his beer, and
     loved meat. His lifestyle choices were a far cry from religious or
     spiritual paths. His curiosity and a hint of scepticism led him to
     accompany his girlfriend to meet Gurudev. His intent was more
     journalistic than spiritual—he wanted to pen a critical article
     on 'guru culture', highlighting it as a facade, and use the money
     earned to fund his indulgent habits.

     "Gurudev noticed me from a distance and burst into laughter,"
     Tony ji recalls their first encounter. "Then, he said, 'Tu aa gaya
     (You've come)', as if he knew me from before. When he placed
     his hand on my head to bless me, it felt like a few thousand
     tonnes of weight had been lifted. At that moment, I assumed he
     was hypnotising me."

     Gurudev's miraculous impact left both Tony ji and me in a state
     of bewilderment. As young men whose preferences leaned more
     towards disco than divinity, we could not logically comprehend

Gurudev's extraordinary capabilities. Hypnosis seemed the
sole plausible explanation, as conventional reasoning failed
to account for what we witnessed. Yet, despite our initial
doubts, we gradually found ourselves drawn into the sphere
of his spiritual influence.

Reflecting on the profound change within him, Tony ji shares,
"Encountering Gurudev initiated an unexpected shift in my
life. My perception of many things I once enjoyed began to
transform. This change unfolded effortlessly, without any
deliberate effort or direct guidance from Gurudev. He did not
ask me to change; the transformation occurred naturally."

Many of us observed Gurudev's profound influence on
people's attitudes, yet we did not fully understand how he
brought about these transformations. In hindsight, these
changes resulted from his ability to channel his energy
towards individuals and maintain a deep connection with
them. His powerful energy subtly influenced the chemistry
of their aura, raising their level of consciousness.

Tony ji honours the mahaguru's impact: "He was cosmic
consciousness. Like a master horticulturist, he cultivated
not plants but future gurus, nurturing the realisation of their
own divine nature within everyone."

Suresh Prabhu ji speaks of Gurudev's enduring presence,
suggesting that physical absence does not equate to a genuine
lack of presence: "I never doubt Gurudev's presence, even in his
physical absence. Sometimes, having someone physically present
can be limiting, as our interactions are constrained to visible
gestures. In contrast, when Gurudev is not physically here,
his presence takes on a fuller, more formidable form. It is not
confined to the physical realm, but manifests in various, more
impactful ways."

He acknowledges Gurudev as a guru who established lofty ideals
that were challenging but inspiring for his disciples and followers
to strive for: "We endeavour to rise to the benchmark he set,
nurturing the hope that, in time, we will fulfil the expectations
he had for us."

When Giri ji arrived in Delhi in the late 1980s, he hailed a taxi to
Gurgaon. The driver, a kind-hearted Sikh man, curiously asked
him about the purpose of his visit. Giri ji shared that he was


    266

going to meet his guru in Gurgaon. Delighted by this, the cabbie
remarked, "A guru is like Paaras. He transforms iron into gold."
        1

Giri ji was deeply moved by the driver's insightful analogy: "I was
touched by his words and decided to share them with Gurudev."

Upon meeting Gurudev, Giri ji eagerly conveyed the cab driver's
comparison. However, the mahaguru's reaction was different
from what Giri ji expected. Instead of appreciating the depth of
the driver's words, Gurudev was gently dismissive. Sensing Giri
ji's confusion, he explained, "My son, a guru's impact is even
more significant than Paaras. Paaras can turn iron into gold, but
it can't convert iron into another Paaras. A guru does more than
improve people or create disciples; he teaches them to be like him
and transforms them into gurus, capable of helping, healing and
enlightening others."

Giri ji reflects, his voice laced with affection, "He consistently
aimed for us to surpass him spiritually. He devoted every moment
to our empowerment, nurturing our growth, and awakening the
essence of the guru within us."

Gurudev was not just a guide; he was a visionary who saw the
latent greatness within everyone, nurturing it with the wisdom
