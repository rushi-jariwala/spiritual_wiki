Gurudev was not a great cricketer, but he was a master of the googly. I recall a time he took a group of us to the famous Badrinath Temple in the Himalayas. As we stood before the entrance, he turned to me with a dead-serious expression and gave me a most unusual instruction: I was to go inside, hug the deity, and say, *"My dear elder brother, I have come to give thee a hug."* In my native Punjabi, he made it sound even more emphatic: *"Bade Bhaiya main twanu jhappi paan ayaan."*

I was terrified. How could "Mr. Ordinary" walk into one of the most powerful temples in India and treat the Lord like a sibling? I ended up hugging a marble statue of Adi Shankaracharya instead, finding it more "tangible and huggable." But when I came out, Gurudev sent me right back in to mentally hug the Lord. It took me decades to understand what he was doing that day. He was shattering my inferiority complex and forcing me to see the equality of one *aatma* with another. This is the foundation of what I call *self-worship*.

*(→ [[stories/hingori-badrinath-brother|Hingori at Badrinath]])*

**The Light and the Two Gates**

To understand self-worship, one must first distinguish between the container and the content. Imagine a bright, eternal light surrounded by two gates. The outer gate is your physical body—it may be aging, imperfect, or unremarkable to the world. The inner gate is your spirit body—it may be clouded by old habits or ignorance. But the light within is the *jivaatma*, a divine spark that is identical in nature to the consciousness supreme. 

It is like the air inside a balloon; it feels separate because of the thin layer of rubber, but it is the same substance as the atmosphere that surrounds it. *(→ [[concepts/jivaatma]])* The gates are ever-changing and dynamic, but they cannot diminish the quality of the light. Self-worship is not the glorification of your social identity or your physical form. It is the reverence of that light. As I wrote in *Aatma Sutra*, *"The 'self' in self-worship we are referring to, is not the self of the gate but the self of the light within."*

**The Atheist’s Candle**

Understanding this is one thing; practicing it is another. You do not even need to believe in a traditional God to access this power. I recall a man named John, a boxing promoter in New York who was diagnosed with blood cancer and given only months to live. He was a blunt atheist who refused to step into a church or temple. I told him: *"Light a candle twice a day and pray to yourself for five minutes. If you can't do that, goodbye."* 

The rudeness worked. John began to pray to his own divinity, recognizing the "That" in the ancient Sanskrit formula *Tat Twam Asi*—That You Are. Twenty years later, John was still alive and kicking in New York. He had benefited from his own inner divinity without needing a theology to name it. *(→ [[stories/john-atheist-self-healing|John — Self-Healing by an Atheist]])*

**Nine Billion Doorways**

Sant Tukaram, the 17th-century Marathi saint, provided the perfect analogy for this practice. He wrote: *"I made an earthen image of Shiva, but the Earth is not Shiva; My worship reaches Shiva, the Earth remains the Earth it was."* He called the image a "conduit pipe"—a medium that allows worship to reach the divine. Your physical self is that same conduit. 

*(→ [[stories/sant-tukaram-worship-poem|Sant Tukaram — Worship Reaches Through Any Image]])*

There are nine billion doorways to the supreme—one for every person alive today. Your own existence is simply the doorway you have the keys to. Using it is not vanity; it is common sense. I eventually had a vision where I stood before Lord Ram, Sita, and Lakshman. When asked what I desired, I found myself offering *them* my good wishes instead of asking for a boon. Sita smiled, and the brothers gave me wry grins. They knew I had finally stopped identifying with my outer layers and started identifying with the part of me that is eternal. *(→ [[stories/hingori-vision-of-ram|Hingori’s Vision of Ram]])*

Stop looking for the divine in distant places. Stand before your mirror today and recognize the light. You are the God to whom you pray.
