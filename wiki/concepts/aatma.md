# Aatma

## Definition

The **aatma** is the essence of an individual that is eternal, unchanging, and indistinguishable from that of the universal consciousness. It is ultimately what we are â€” minus the various bodies and minus the individual ego.

> The *aatma* is commonly defined as "The essence of an individual that is eternal, unchanging and indistinguishable from that of the universal consciousness." In other words, the *aatma* is ultimately us, minus the various bodies and our individual ego.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.I, p.20

## Aatma vs Jivaatma

The aatma and the [[concepts/jivaatma|jivaatma]] are distinct, though related. The jivaatma is the *individualised form* of the consciousness supreme â€” a fragment that has taken on the sense of a separate self, carries the karmic balance sheet, and is subject to rebirth. The aatma is that same consciousness in its pure state: without individuation, without ego, without the karmic baggage.

The jivaatma is like air sealed inside a balloon. The aatma is the atmosphere itself â€” limitless, undivided. The balloon (jivaatma) is a miniscule version of the atmosphere, temporarily enclosed and experienced as separate. When the balloon dissolves, the air rejoins the whole without remainder.

> The *jivaatma* is the individualised form of the consciousness supreme. Just as the air in a balloon is a miniscule version of the atmosphere, the *jivaatma* is a part of the consciousness supreme. The *jivaatma* is nothing but the divine spark at the core of your existence.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.I, p.19

This answers the distinction that *Karma Sutra* left implicit: jivaatma = the divine spark + individuation + karmic load; aatma = the consciousness supreme itself, prior to any individuation.

## The Consciousness Supreme

Hingori's term for what has elsewhere been called God, the Creator, Parmatma, universal consciousness:

> I believe that there is a supreme creative power or energy. And, since energy has a consciousness, it is eponymously and conveniently called "the consciousness supreme". Now, this creative energy has... created the universe (or multiverse, according to the latest scientific hypotheses), and everything in it.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.I, p.19

The aatma *is* this consciousness supreme, viewed from the perspective of the individual. *Tat Twam Asi* â€” "That Thou Art" â€” is the book's core assertion: 'That' = the consciousness supreme; 'Thou' = the body, spirit, and jivaatma; 'Are' = you are already a part of it, whether or not you know it.

## Relation to Moksh

[[concepts/moksha|Moksh]] is the event in which the distinction between aatma and jivaatma dissolves. The karmashaya is fully deleted, the individuation dissolves, and what remains is only the aatma â€” the consciousness supreme, undivided. The purpose of the jivaatma is to reduce its stored samskars until this dissolution becomes possible.

> It is only when the *karmashaya* has managed to delete or cleanse its stored data, can a person get freedom from life and death and attain *moksh*.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.I, p.19
