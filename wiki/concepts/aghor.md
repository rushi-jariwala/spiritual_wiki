# Aghor (The Philosophy of Non-Duality)

## Definition

Aghor is a **state of mind that is free of contradiction and discrimination.** It is not primarily a set of practices but a philosophical stance: the complete dissolution of the categories through which the conditioned mind perceives and reacts to the world.

> Aghor is a state of mind that is free of contradiction and discrimination.
> â€” Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.51

## Aghor Vidya and Its Misunderstanding

Aghor vidya has commonly come to be recognised as lower-level tantric worship. This misunderstanding is compounded by the Aghoris' visible practices:

- Eating dead bodies and excreta
- Maintaining poor hygiene
- Meditating on charnel grounds (burning ghats)

These practices serve a specific philosophical purpose: by immersing themselves in what the conditioned mind finds most repulsive, Aghoris work to achieve states in which stench and other sensory stimuli no longer produce disgust. The goal is not filth for its own sake but the **destruction of the aversion response** â€” the dwesha pole of the raga-dwesha axis.

> Even though their practices are considered dirty, the truth is that the Aghor philosophy is highly evolved beyond the seemingly messy optics.
> â€” Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.51

## Purpose: Breaking Free from Maya

By practising non-duality â€” treating the beautiful and the repulsive, the sacred and the profane, as aspects of the same consciousness â€” Aghoris aspire to **break free from the tantalising tentacles of maya or worldly illusion.**

> [!tip] This aligns Aghor with the broader Hingori Sutras framework: [[concepts/maya]] is sustained by duality. Any practice that systematically dissolves the mind's preference structure attacks maya at its root. The Aghor method is extreme and unusual; its metaphysical target is the same as [[concepts/non-doership]] and [[concepts/citt-vritti-nirodh]].
