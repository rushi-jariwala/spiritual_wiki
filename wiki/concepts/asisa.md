# Asisa

## Definition

**Asisa** is the will of an individual to exist eternally. It is a kind of force that impels a [[concepts/jivaatma|jivaatma]] to exist in continuity. The intent of self-preservation that holds together our individuality is fuelled by some of the [[concepts/kleshas|kleshas]] — asmita, abhinivesha, and avidya.

> This is the will of an individual to exist eternally. It is a kind of force that impels a jivaatma to exist in continuity. The intent of self-preservation that holds together our individuality is fuelled by some of the kleshas (asmita, abhinivesha, avidya).
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.127

## The Klesha Cocktail

Asisa is not a single klesha but a composite force assembled from three:

**Asmita** supplies the desire to preserve the 'I'-sense — the individuality of consciousness, the intent to exist in perpetuity in this life or beyond, holding on to one's distinctions from others.

**Abhinivesha** supplies the fear of extinction. In this life it appears as the fear of death; across the continuity of several lives it is the fear of losing individuality of body, mind, and soul.

**Avidya** supplies the delusion that underwrites the entire mechanism — the mistaking of the temporary for the eternal, of the individual self for the Supreme.

Together these three kleshas create the force that keeps the jivaatma cycling through incarnation after incarnation, adding to its [[concepts/samskars|samskars]], perpetuating the circle.

## In Practice

> We must recognise the fact that asisa or intent to exist in perpetuity creates the fear of death or existence in us. But paradoxically, we need to work towards lesser lives to lead.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.141

The recognition of asisa as a mechanism — rather than a truth — is itself part of the gyan that can weaken its hold. The aspiration is to exhaust as much karmic data as possible in each life, so that fewer lives are needed. Mukti (freedom) is inversely proportional to the strength of asisa.
