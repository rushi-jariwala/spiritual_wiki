# Astral Travel

## Definition

**Astral travel** is the voluntary or induced exit of the [[concepts/sukshma-sharir|spirit body]] from the physical body, allowing it to travel to other locations and even other realms while the physical body remains at rest. Hingori grounds the chapter in direct, repeated personal experience — and in decades of close observation of Gurudev, who travelled astrally "at least thrice a week, for several hours at a stretch."

> Regarding out-of-body experiences, I have not only had several myself, but have also been the understudy of a man who travelled astrally, at least thrice a week, for several hours at a stretch.
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VI, p.67

## Preparation: The Floating Stage

The spirit body's first step toward astral exit is dissociation — learning to separate its wakefulness from the physical body's sleep. Hingori describes this preparatory phase:

> I found myself floating about 3-4 inches over my body, though restricted to my body's boundary or sphere. [...] The 'floating me' was awake whilst the 'physical me' was fast asleep.
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VI, p.67

This stage happened several times in the *sthan* room with Gurudev's guidance. Hingori understood it as preparation for the spirit body to eventually exit the physical body fully. During dream and vision states, he sometimes travelled with Gurudev to different locations — so vivid he could feel the breeze and touch things. See [[stories/hingori-lonavala-temple-vision]].

## Gati — The Speed of the Spirit Body

**Gati** is the capacity for superfast travel in the spirit body. Ordinary spirits travel at a default speed; gati enables the spirit body to move like a rocket — through barriers, across vast distances, into other realms.

> In order to travel faster, the spirit body requires extra spiritual energy and practice. This energy gives the power of motion to the spirit body, which means it can use that energy to travel exceptionally faster than an average spirit. This extra energy also enables the spirit body to pass through barriers and reach other realms, perceivably long distances away.
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VI, p.68

Gati is acquired through the same practices that deepen the [[concepts/aura|aura]]. Guru grace is the prerequisite:

> *"Guru bin gati nahi"* — it is not easy for a spirit body to acquire superfast travelling speed without the grace of the guru.
> — Hindi adage, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VI, p.68

Hingori attained gati when R.C. Malhotra — senior disciple of Gurudev, sent by Gurudev — appeared in a vision state and taught him the science of superfast travel. After attaining gati and several rehearsals, the spirit body moved "like a rocket, accompanied with a buzzing sound" — so fast that the only sensation was movement itself. See [[stories/hingori-gati-malhotra]].

## Spirit World Physics

In the out-of-body state, the physical world appears transformed:

- **Solid matter becomes penetrable**: walls, ceilings, and floors offer no resistance — "almost as if they were made of the thinnest paper."
- **Light is dim, not dark**: even at night, the outside has a post-dusk quality; interiors are a few shades darker.
- **Spirits appear solid**: when seen with open physical eyes, spirits appear ethereal; when seen from within the spirit state, they appear as solid as physical beings.

> Every time I saw a spirit with my eyes open, it appeared ethereal in form, but when I saw a spirit while out of my body (being in spirit form myself), it appeared as solid as a being in physical form.
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VI, p.69

> [!tip] Perception is body-relative. What appears ethereal from physical eyes appears solid from spirit eyes. The spirit world is real — only the vantage point shifts.

### The Fan Ankle — Energy Fields vs. Solid Matter

Solid matter is penetrable; *rotating energy fields* are not. During one astral travel, Hingori flew through a ceiling fan to the floor above. On his return, his ankles caught in the fan's rotating energy field:

> I heard a distinct cracking sound and felt pain in my ankle. On awaking the next morning, I realised that my ankle pained in the same spot and consequently, I had a limp for around 15 days. It is my belief that the motion of the fan blades created a rotating energy field which hurt the ankle of my spirit body.
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VI, p.70

The fan blades themselves do not impede the spirit body — the rotating *energy field* they generate does. See [[stories/hingori-fan-ankle]].

## Nirman Kaya in Action

This chapter provides the clearest live demonstrations of [[concepts/mukti|nirman kaya]] — the duplicate spirit body created by evolved spiritualists while still physically alive:

**Ashish at Lemon Tree Resort, Goa** — Ashish's spiritual guru appeared across from him at lunch, told him not to eat non-veg on Thursday, then disappeared. The guru was physically in another city and unaware of the incident. "His second body seemed to have a will of its own." (*Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VI, p.70)

**Virenderji's uninvited guests** — A retired judge in Haryana overslept and missed his morning walk. Three friends arrived saying he had invited them to the park at 6:30am. "Here was a clear example of how another version of you can exist whilst on a path of spiritual evolution." See [[stories/virenderji-uninvited-guests]].

**Gurudev at two places** — On more than one occasion, Gurudev was seen by people at two different locations at exactly the same time. (*Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VI, p.71)

## Posthumous Appearances

Evolved saints continue to appear in spirit form after physical death:

> Many saints, including Gurudev, appear in spirit form to help and guide others.
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VI, p.71

Twenty years after Gurudev's passing, Hingori's schoolmate (DLF, Gurgaon) had a midday waking vision of a stranger projected on his wall: "Don't worry I will solve your problems. Come to Guruji of Gurgaon in Sector 7." He Googled it, found the sthan, and his life now revolves around it. See [[stories/schoolmate-gurudev-gurgaon]].

## *The Guru of Gurus* — New Accounts (*The Mahaguru*, pp.98–100)

The Mahaguru chapter adds several first-hand witness accounts of Gurudev's astral capacity not present in *Aatma Sutra*:

### Sitaram Taki — Haridwar (1970s)

The earliest documented witness account. One night in Haridwar, Gurudev told Sitaram Taki ji to press his feet and accompany him on an astral journey. He wrapped himself in a sheet, began *paath*, and Sitaram ji watched an identical sheet-wrapped Gurudev emerge from his body while the physical form remained seated. Paralysed with fear, Sitaram ji wet his pants before the astral form returned and merged. See [[stories/sitaram-taki-haridwar-astral]].

### The Sarson Ka Saag Departure

On another night at Gurudev's home in Hariana, while disciples ate sarson ka saag with desi ghee, Gurudev's head fell back with a sudden jerk. Finding no pulse or breath, his disciples assumed he had died and, in panic, emptied the household's supply of desi ghee over his body hoping to generate warmth. Sitaram ji recalled that Gurudev had mentioned returning from astral meetings with Buddhe Baba by 3:30 am — so the group waited. Gurudev revived a few minutes after 3:30 am, walked to the bathroom, and returned saying: *"What have you people done to me? I'm stumbling all over the place because I've got desi ghee all over my body!"* He then laughed and "let loose a volley of his favourite Punjabi words." See [[stories/gurudev-sarson-saag-astral]].

### Control Over the Elements

*The Guru of Gurus* establishes a direct link between Gurudev's astral capacity and command over natural phenomena. Two documented instances:

**Parwanoo:** Rain disrupted a seva day. Gurudev looked up at the sky; within seconds the rain reduced to a trickle and stopped. Hingori notes that since that day, it has never rained in the area of that sthan on a designated seva day.

**Mungaoli, Madhya Pradesh:** Rain began while Gurudev and Hingori sat together. Gurudev stepped outside, returned to his seat with an agitated expression, then rose, opened the tent flap, and delivered harsh words skyward. The rain stopped instantly; dark clouds lifted.
