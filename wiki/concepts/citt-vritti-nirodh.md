# Citt, Vritti, Nirodh

Three interlocking Sanskrit concepts from Maharishi Patanjali's *Yogasutras*, taken up by Hingori in *Aatma Sutra* Ch.III to explain the nature of thoughts, emotions, and the practice of dispassionate observation.

## Citt

> "*Citt* is the mind in its totality â€” at the conscious, subconscious and unconscious levels; the individual mind; as well as the ethereal mind. *Citt*, is an infinite ocean, undivided, impossible to define by a single parameter."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.III, p.27

*Citt* is not the everyday thinking mind alone. It encompasses all levels of awareness and also the ethereal dimension of mind. It is reality itself, the whole truth. It cannot be grasped by the intellect, because the intellect is only a part of *citt*, not its master.

## Vritti

> "*Vrittis* are ripples or disturbances in the form of thoughts, perceptions or emotions in the ocean that is *citt*. Anything that can be expressed is *vritti*. A *vritti* is transient â€” one moment it is there, perceivable, and the next moment it vanishes, replaced by another ripple."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.III, p.27

*Vrittis* include perceptions, imagination, sleep, memories, acceptable knowledge, and misunderstanding. A *vritti* is not separate from *citt* â€” it is a temporary disturbance *in* it, like a wave is not separate from the ocean. The mistake is to give a *vritti* the status of a complete, separate reality.

**The critical karmic rule**: reacting emotionally to a *vritti* â€” taking ownership of it â€” leaves an imprint in the [[concepts/kaarna-sharir|karmashaya]], turning a fleeting thought into a new [[concepts/samskars|samskar]]:

> "You must understand that when you react emotionally to a *vritti* passing through the *citt*, it leaves an imprint of that *vritti* in your *karmashaya*, which stores memories of each lifetime, amounting to your karmic balance. In the bargain, a fleeting thought becomes yours due to taking ownership and becomes a credit or debit in your karmic record."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.III, p.29

*Vrittis* also cost energy: every act of perception, imagination, or memory consumes aura (*shakti*). "With more and more passing *vrittis* in the *citt*, you use up energy, thereby depleting the aura and losing energy." (Ch.III, p.28)

## Nirodh

> "*Nirodh* is the practice of observing the relation of *citt* and *vritti* and concluding that they are one and the same at the core. It is understanding that a *vritti* originates in the *citt* as a reaction (or hopefully an action) to the happenings in your surroundings. These happenings are like the wind that causes waves in the ocean. They fade away just as they come leaving behind the original state of *citt*."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.III, p.29

*Nirodh* is not suppression, elimination, or avoidance of thoughts. It is dispassionate witnessing:

> "A person having understood the concept of *nirodh* will begin to observe thoughts and emotions instead of reacting to them. He will observe the *vritti* entering his mind from the point of view of a third person, a dispassionate, detached spectator. He does not try to control, suppress, eliminate or encourage his *vrittis*. As waves enter and leave his *citt*, he watches, unfazed, unmoved, unaffected."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.III, p.29

*Nirodh* is not a meditation technique for a few minutes daily. It is a sustained state of being:

> "*Nirodh* is a state of being, one that must become a constant part of our conscious thinking, and not just practiced for a few minutes every day. Conditioning yourself to observe your thoughts from the outside is easier than detaching from emotions. Every time you think of a new string of thoughts, consciously remind yourself, these are just passing through your *citt*. They are not permanent, not yours."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.III, p.30

## The Ocean and Wave Analogy

> "*Citt* is reality and the whole truth, the vast ocean. *Vritti* is a disturbance or wave in this whole truth, but in its substance, it is still water... Sadness is only a ripple that passes through the *citt*, a passing thought."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.III, pp.27â€“28

When you are sad, the *citt* has not become sad. Sadness is a wave. The ocean remains the ocean. Intellect alone cannot grasp this â€” it can only produce *vritti*, not comprehend the whole.

## The Cell Phone Analogy

> "Imagine the cell phone as the *citt*, the usage and apps as *vrittis* and the battery charge as the energy or aura. With more and more usage of the apps, the cell phone battery charge is lost. In the same vein, with more and more passing *vrittis* in the *citt*, you use up energy, thereby depleting the aura and losing energy."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.III, p.28

A phone disconnected from the network is not depleted. *Nirodh* is not about switching off â€” it is about not over-trapping oneself in the apps. See [[analogies|Analogies]].

## Why This Matters for Karma

> "It is only in this physical life that a spirit can fructify its *karma*, both positive and negative, exhausting some or all that is stored in its *karmashaya*. To move towards the objectives of the spirit, *mukti*, and ultimately, *moksha*, one must ensure that while stored *samskars* from past lives fructify, no new *karma* is added to the *karmashaya*. And for this, the practice of *nirodh* needs to be developed and preferably mastered."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.III, p.30
