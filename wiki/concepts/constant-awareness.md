# Constant Awareness

## Definition

Constant awareness is the state in which the mind is simultaneously present to multiple frequencies of reality — physical, subtle, and spiritual — without effort or interruption. It is not about being gripped by thoughts; it is the mind telepathically tuning in to several frequencies at will. Gurudev lived in this state continuously.

> "Try as you might, you will never know me even though you think you do."
> — Gurudev, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.177

> "Constant awareness is a huge burden and not even remotely as glamorous as it sounds. Gurudev's ability to be perpetually aware was perhaps his life's acutest difficulty."
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.183

## Gurudev's Constant Awareness — Documented Instances

Gurudev's vigilant awareness is documented in numerous incidents:

**The flight to Hyderabad:** Sitting next to Hingori on a flight, Gurudev went into his trademark trance. When Hingori glanced quizzically, Gurudev smiled and said, *"Indu is putting the clothes out to dry at the sthan in Gurgaon"* — witnessing the daily life of his devotees from the air.

**Narrating a private argument:** Hingori took a detour to Gurgaon while driving his non-believer father from Chandigarh to Delhi. His father waited in the car during a massive argument along the way. The moment Hingori entered the sthan, Gurudev welcomed him and went into a trance for a few seconds — then narrated the entire conversation between Hingori and his father verbatim.

**Tracking Gaggu ji:** Five decades after a teen-runaway episode, Gaggu ji still cannot fathom how his uncle (Gurudev) found him at Shadipur bus depot — approximately forty kilometres from Gurudev's home, in a bustling Delhi hub, with no mobile phones and GPS only in aircraft, not telephony. Gurudev was waiting for him as he stepped off the bus.

**The family road accident:** A family staying at the Gurgaon sthan for treatment decided to visit relatives in Delhi past midnight. Gurudev was reluctant but relented. As soon as they left, he handed Bittu ji his car keys and told him to tail their vehicle. Minutes later, their vehicle rammed into a truck and skidded under it. Bittu ji pulled the family out; injuries were sustained, but death was averted. Gurudev later said:

> "I am a guru, and I can change the probability of an event's occurrence from 100% to lesser. Yet what is destined to happen will happen."
> — Gurudev to Bittu ji, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.179

See [[stories/family-road-accident-gurgaon]].

**The inland letter with a yantra:** An inland letter arrived addressed to Gurudev. Before opening it, he instructed Hingori to leave the room. When he summoned Hingori back five minutes later, the torn letter had been assembled like a jigsaw on the bed, visibly exposing a yantra meant to take his life. He had been aware of the letter's contents before opening it — and had shielded Hingori from potential danger. See [[stories/inland-letter-yantra]].

**The Bathri yogi:** At a State Electricity Board guest house in Bathri, Gurudev told Hingori that the spirit of an advanced yogi had sought refuge there for nearly a century, awaiting his arrival to grant it human birth. He allowed the spirit to choose the womb of Shobha Taneja ji, a disciple's wife. Their son, Abhay Taneja, was described as a product of immaculate conception. See [[stories/bathri-yogi-immaculate-birth]].

**Past-life knowledge:** Gurudev showed Ravi ji visions of not one but two previous lifetimes. He told Santlal ji that in this lifetime he had received tutelage two years before his destined time, owing to his mother's piety. He told Vashisht ji — who had seen Gurudev in visions for ten years — that they were supposed to meet at Renuka in 1970 but only made it in 1980.

**The 500-year wait:** Gurudev mentioned more than once that he had waited 500 years before gathering disciples who arrived around the same time as him, to accomplish a collective spiritual mission.

> "The world believes I sleep, but how would they know that I keep an eye on them and guide them?"
> — Gurudev to his wife, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.178

> "I am an ordinary man, not an ordinary guru."
> — Gurudev to childhood friend Subbhash ji, in a dream, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.180

## Practices for Developing Conscious Awareness

Hingori suggests two practices derived from Gurudev's instructions:

### One-Point Concentration

Constantly stare at a chosen **imaginary point a foot away from your forehead.** This reduces the cacophony of thoughts, vastly improving attentional and cognitive abilities over time. Every time the mind wanders, return it to the point. With constant practice, you develop single-pointed concentration and learn to focus and defocus at will.

### Visualisation of OM

> "OM is the sound that created everything in the universe. OM is the guru. When you chant OM, you chant my name, and when you think of OM, you think of me."
> — Gurudev to Surinder Kaushal ji (who runs a sthan in Chicago), *The Guru of Gurus*, p.181

Gurudev's directive: visualise the symbol of OM on every surface you see, as often as possible. Hingori could connect the dots between practice and result only years later, when he achieved *guru charan prapti* — the movement of the kundalini to the third eye chakra. By visualising the OM, you use the power of thought and intent to activate the OM within you. Similarly, visualising the trishul unleashes its power within yourself.

> [!tip] Gurudev's instruction was simply: *"Buddhu ban ja"* — stop filtering perception through the prism of perspectives. Intuition begins where logic ends.

## OM and the Ajna Chakra

Prana flows in the human body via energy pathways called **nadis**, which form a plexus at specific points — rotating energy vortices known as **chakras**. The seven dominant chakras run from the base of the spine (Mooladhara, lowest vibration) to the crown (Sahasrara, highest and subtlest vibration).

The **Ajna chakra** (third eye, between the eyebrows) governs the brain and is responsible for decoding perception and enhancing the sixth sense. When activated, it empowers the mind to command the flow of bodily energy and access latent abilities — telepathy, clairaudience, clairvoyance.

The Ajna is the only point where the **Ida** (left, lunar, feminine) and **Pingala** (right, solar, masculine) nadis converge into the **Sushumna** to form an energy triangle (triveni). This synchronises the two brain hemispheres. The five chakras below Ajna are each activated by one of the five elements (earth, water, fire, air, ether), linked to the five senses. OM — the activator of Ajna — is beyond the elements and senses.

> [!tip] The guru's feet are situated at the Ajna chakra; his head at the Sahasrara. The journey from awareness to enlightenment, gyan to divya gyan, runs from the sixth to the seventh chakra.

## Manifesting the Visualisation

The brain cannot differentiate between thought and the activity triggered by that thought — brain research confirms this. What you think of doing produces the same physiological changes as doing it.

> "What you can do physically, you can do mentally."
> — Gurudev, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.185

Tibetan monks raise body temperatures with their minds. Shaolin monks train their minds to withstand intense physical pain. Some monks can mentally command their bodies to levitate. Repeated visualisation is the most effective way of unlocking and manifesting potential — including the realisation of constant awareness itself.
