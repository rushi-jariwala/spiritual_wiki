# Destiny

## Definition

Destiny is the result of *karmas* waiting to fructify. They do so only in alignment with planetary movements â€” when planetary positions are adverse, negative *karmas* get fructified; when positive, positive *karmas* fructify. Even dreams, during positive planetary movements, can be happy and spiritual in nature.

> "Destiny is the result of *karmas* waiting to fructify. They do so only in alignment with the planetary movements."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.V, p.51

*Karmas* are fruits of stored *samskars* in the long-term memory of inner consciousness. They fructify when combined with emotions and ownership. Actions perceived as positive generate positive karma for the future; those perceived as negative generate suffering so they can be exhausted.

## Core Teaching

The mechanism is one of oscillation â€” good/bad, right/wrong â€” driven entirely by the karmic balance sheet surfacing through planetary alignment.

> "And so, oscillates the pendulum â€” good, bad, right, wrong. One moment you like your life, another moment you do not. Duality has its day. Delusion has its way!"
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.V, p.51

Astrology is Hingori's primary frame for understanding destiny. Founded by the ancient *rishis* through intuitive analysis, it is presented as an exact science â€” though its practitioners are frequently inexact:

> "As I have always maintained, astrology is an exact science practiced by inexact practitioners."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.V, p.57

The birth chart (*kundali*) is the frozen planetary configuration at the *mahurat* of birth. Planetary transits thereafter (planets continuing to move relative to the birth chart) determine the timing and quality of karmic fructification across a lifetime. See [[concepts/mahurat]] for the mechanics of how the birth moment operates as the karmic matching instrument.

## Nuances

**Forms of reading destiny**: Hingori presents multiple approaches as valid â€” Vedic astrology (*kundali* + transits), Bhrigu Nadi (prophecies by the sage Bhrigu made thousands of years ago), palmistry, tarot, and psychic reading. All are treated as different instruments pointing at the same underlying reality of a pre-mapped future.

**The future has already happened**: Hingori raises this explicitly:

> "If the trends of your life are predetermined and the future has already happened in a cross-section of time and space that lies ahead, how do you examine the concept of guilt?"
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.V, p.60

This directly undercuts the guilt mechanism that binds karma. If events unfold as destined, personal blame for failure or moral weakness is philosophically weakened. The very people who "become Gandhi or Martin Luther King or Guru Gobind Singh" â€” or Hitler â€” were destined to be so.

**Historical instances cited**: Nostradamus predicted Hitler's life 400 years before his birth. [[entities/bhrigu|Rishi Bhrigu]] predicted the futures of people living today, thousands of years ago. Gurudev predicted the Mumbai floods (2005) approximately 30 years before they occurred. See: [[stories/bhrigu-dr-chandra]], [[stories/gurudev-predictions-mumbai-politician]].

## In Practice

Believing in destiny provides the necessary shift from **expecting** to **accepting**. Failure to accept produces a cycle of "should have / could have / would have" â€” self-blame that feeds the inferiority complex and obstructs [[concepts/aatma|self-worship]].

Hingori illustrates the pervasiveness of this self-indictment:

> "I was viewing a video clip of a brilliant speaker who drew thousands of people to his lectures on life sciences. At the end of the lecture he asked, 'How many of you agree that the greatest obstacle in your life is you?' Around 5,000 people raised their hands."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.V, p.61

Destiny belief is offered as the philosophical counterweight â€” a way to release the self from the role of "greatest obstacle."

> "This is what I was meant to be and this is what I am. This is me â€” the created, the intended, the destined. The result of the karmic me. But not the 'guilty' me."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.V, p.61

> [!tip] The acceptance of destiny is not passivity â€” it is the removal of a false self-indictment. Effort still happens; it is also destined.

> [!warning] Statutory Warning (Hingori's own): "The predictions cited here are the ones which have been spot on. However, predictions have also gone wrong by hundreds of astrologers, including my friend." The framework endorses astrology as a science while acknowledging practitioner error.

## Destiny and Self-Blame (Ch.XI)

*Aatma Sutra* Ch.XI extends the argument to the dissolution of self-blame. Specific karmic configurations â€” planetary combinations â€” are known to astrologers to strongly predict late marriages, divorces, and separations. The self-blame that follows these outcomes is therefore a category error:

> "There are several combinations of planetary positions (understood by astrologers) that strongly predict late marriages, divorces or separation. Some other configurations show an abundance of material wealth, the goodness of character, while others indicate the habit of lying and deceit. Ironically, if our birth had been a couple of hours earlier or later, the script of our destiny might have read differently."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.XI, p.134

The practical implication: if the outcome was already encoded in the birth configuration, the person experiencing "failure" is not its cause. Self-blame under these conditions is an illusion of authorship over a pre-written script. See [[concepts/self-acceptance]] for the full treatment of how destiny acceptance dissolves guilt.
