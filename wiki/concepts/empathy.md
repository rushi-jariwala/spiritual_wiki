# Empathy

## Definition

Empathy, in Hingori's framework, is the ability to understand what another person is going through *without adding emotions to it*. It is the second of the five key formulas of evolution in *Aatma Sutra* Ch.IX â€” and is presented as both a spiritual practice and a prerequisite for self-acceptance.

> "The word 'empathy' is often used in conversations. It means to be able to understand what the other person is going through without adding emotions to it. However, truly empathising with someone is an extremely difficult task."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.IX, p.105

## Core Teaching

The surprising move in Hingori's treatment is the direction of causality: **self-acceptance requires empathy toward others first**. Constantly finding fault in others creates insecurity about one's own shortcomings, which in turn prevents one from seeing divinity within oneself. The rewiring must run in the other direction â€” accept others unconditionally, and the capacity to accept oneself follows.

> "It dawned on me that to be able to accept myself, I should first be able to accept others. Constantly finding faults in others made me insecure about my own shortcomings, thereby preventing me from seeing divinity within."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.IX, p.106

## Personal Account â€” The Saturn Lesson

Hingori describes how this teaching was not arrived at intellectually but forced upon him through adversity. The position of Saturn in his horoscope brought two months of being bedridden â€” enough stillness to examine and dismantle the pattern of finding fault in others, including fellow spiritualists.

> "I rewired myself to start looking at people from a different perspective. For example, if they behaved in a certain way to attract attention, instead of seeing this as their fault I started seeing it as a part of their nature. And in that lesson of empathy, I also learnt acceptance."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.IX, p.106

The shift from "fault" to "nature" is the operative reframe: what looks like a shortcoming is simply how that person's karma and samskars are currently manifesting. Judging them is as logical as judging a fruit for the season it ripens in.

## Sneh's Lesson

> Sneh, a 26-year-old acquaintance, confided that she learnt a lesson in empathy recently. In the past, when tending to patients, she secretly felt they fussed unnecessarily. But when she herself was bedridden for days with a back problem, she learnt the true value of empathy and can now relate better with the sick.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.IX, p.107

The pattern repeats: embodied experience â€” being in someone else's position â€” unlocks empathy. The theoretical understanding does not suffice.

## JRD Tata â€” Managing by Adjusted Expectation

Hingori cites an old newspaper interview with J.R.D. Tata:

> "Many times we are disappointed with people who work for us because we expect them to think and behave the way we want them to."
> â€” J.R.D. Tata, quoted in *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.IX, p.106

## Nuances

### Empathy vs. Emotional Entanglement

The key distinction is the *absence of added emotion*. [[concepts/vairagya|Vairagya]] (detachment) and empathy are not opposites â€” they are complements. Gurudev's example in the [[concepts/vairagya|Vairagya]] page makes this explicit: he was present to his disciples' pain and available to relieve it, but he did not *feel* it. This is empathy without merging.

### Different Journeys

> "Earlier, being at a social gathering would be sheer torture for me. I could not stand people being pompous and profound about trivialities... Today, I realise that it was not their fault but mine for not being empathetic enough to realise that my journey was different from theirs."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.IX, p.107

Each person is on a different rung of the same ladder. Judging others for being on a lower rung is as irrational as judging a first-year student for not knowing what a final-year student knows.

## In Practice

- When you notice fault-finding: flip the frame â€” what aspect of their *nature* or *karma* explains this behaviour?
- When a social situation frustrates you: ask whether the frustration is about them or about your expectation.
- When facing someone's limitation: use [[concepts/pratipakshbhavana|pratipakshbhavana]] to superimpose a compassionate interpretation.
- Empathy toward others is not a destination â€” it is the path toward [[concepts/maya|self-acceptance]] and self-worship (Chapters XIâ€“XII).
