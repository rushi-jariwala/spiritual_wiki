# Intent as Healing

## Definition

Intent as healing is the teaching that the guru's *intent* — not the specific technique, remedy, or ritual — is the operative agent in spiritual healing. All methods, mantras, and objects are incidental carriers; the consciousness that animates them is the actual curative force.

## The Source Passage

The teaching crystallises in a single scene. Hingori, as a young spiritualist, had accumulated a diary of ayurvedic remedies and began prescribing them to sthan visitors. Gurudev summoned him:

> "Tu doctor kyon ban raha hai. Maine tujhe guru banaya hai. Tu kaagaz bhi mathe se laga kar usko de dega, toh woh theek ho jayega."
> (I have made you a guru, not a doctor. Even if you touched a piece of paper to your forehead and gave it to him, it would have served as a cure.)
> — Gurudev to Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.97

Hingori's reflection:

> Gurudev's words made me realise that the guru's intent manifests as healing. Everything else is incidental.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.97

## Implications

### Method is a vehicle, not the cause

The same intent delivered through jal (sanctified water), a mantra, a touch, a glance, or a blank piece of paper produces the same healing — because none of these is the cause. The cause is the pure, focused consciousness of the guru flowing through whatever vehicle is at hand.

### Consistent with the jivaatma teaching

This is structurally identical to Gurudev's teaching on prayer:

> "When one goes to a temple to plead for the fulfilment of one's desire, it is not the temple's energy that provides a solution. That wish is fulfilled by the jivaatma, the spark of the consciousness supreme that resides within each being."
> — Gurudev, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.116

In both cases, the apparent external agent (temple, method) is not the cause. The consciousness — of the jivaatma in one case, of the guru's intent in the other — is what acts.

### Consequence for the disciple

Gurudev did not tell Hingori to discard all methods. He told Hingori to *identify himself as a guru*, not a doctor. The implication: a guru who knows the true source of healing does not need an elaborate toolkit. The doctor role is not wrong — it is insufficient. It stops at the method and misses the consciousness animating it.

## Related Teaching — Mantra Transfer

[[concepts/mantra-transfer|Mantra transfer]] operates by the same logic. When Gurudev declared Hingori *siddh* in a mantra before Hingori had chanted it, the siddhi resided in Gurudev's intent, not in the syllables. The mantra words were given *after*, as a formal structure to house what had already been transmitted.
