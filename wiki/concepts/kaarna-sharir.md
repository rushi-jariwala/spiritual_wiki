# Kaarna Sharir

## Definition

The **kaarna sharir** (causal body) is the body that carries the karmashaya — the storehouse of [[concepts/samskars|samskars]] — from one incarnation to the next. Hingori uses the metaphor of a USB stick plugged into a USB port: the karmashaya is the stick, the kaarna sharir is the port.

> The karmashaya or the USB stick is attached to the USB port called the kaarna sharir or causal body. This is our third body, below the astral body (sukshma sharir). The kaarna sharir and its karmashaya pass from one incarnation to another, and become the storehouse of our conditioning, our samskars.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.130

## Position in the Body Hierarchy

The kaarna sharir is the third body, situated below (subtler than) the astral body (sukshma sharir). It is distinct from the five [[concepts/koshas|koshas]], which are sheaths of consciousness around the jivaatma; the kaarna sharir is the inter-life vehicle that carries the karmic data.

## The Real Wealth

Hingori reframes the kaarna sharir's contents as the only thing a person truly *wills* to themselves across lives:

> Looking from a different perspective, the samskars we carry in our USB stick are the real wealth we 'will' to ourselves for our future lives.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.130

Material wealth stays behind. The kaarna sharir and its samskar load are what pass through the threshold of death.

## The Goal: Lightening the Stick

The aspiration is to exhaust samskars and reduce the karmashaya's load — not to fill it further:

> We need to try and exhaust our samskars and lighten the weight of our storage stick as much as we can, and cleanse this data to achieve a blank. Even a partial result is a great achievement. An ideal situation for sure, but hard to achieve on ground level! It would take several lives and many incarnations, but I think it's worth a try.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.130
