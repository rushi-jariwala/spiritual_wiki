# Koshas

> *"Perception at the level of the annamaya kosha is physically oriented. If one can think either from the level of vigyanamaya or anandamaya kosha, the perception undergoes a sea change."*
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.7, p.101

## Definition

The *koshas* are five sheaths or layers of existence that surround the [[concepts/jivaatma|jivaatma]]. Moving inward from one kosha to the next changes perception entirely. The journey through the koshas is the structural path out of ego-identification and toward [[concepts/moksha|moksha]].

## The Five Koshas

### 1. Annamaya Kosha (Food Body)

This sheath is sustained by food. It perceives through the senses and is the gross, physiological body — existing in the material plane. When consciousness rests here, people are influenced by the three [[concepts/gunas|gunas]] and oscillate between the three mindsets. Physical comforts and luxury are the aspirational values of this kosha: looks, beauty, art, social graces, brands, lifestyle, possessions, designations.

### 2. Pranamaya Kosha (Life-Force Body)

This sheath is formed of *prana* or life force. It looks and feels like it is made of ether or bio-magnetic energy. Being ethereal, it is not obsessed with physical aspirational values. Its aspirational values are spiritual power or *shakti* (electromagnetic energy). It is less capable of acquiring this shakti than its counterpart annamaya.

> After death, a shakti or person benefits greatly if their spirit is wealthy of electromagnetic energy. It is this shakti that forms the strength of a spirit. The more shakti a spirit has, the greater are its spiritual capabilities and the greater is its strength.
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.7, p.116

Each spirit aspires to attain *gati* or speed, which helps it traverse boundaries that act as barriers to lesser capable spirits.

### 3. Manomaya Kosha (Mind Body)

The sheath that creates a person's individuality or identity — also called the body of the mind. It deals with thoughts, emotions, attachments, cravings, and duality (positive/negative, good/bad, right/wrong).

> Unfortunately, this body keeps a person oblivious of his/her oneness with the Supreme Consciousness and creates a false identification of 'I am this' and 'I am that'. It is a kind of villain in the generation of happiness or unhappiness, both being individual perceptions.
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.7, p.116

Manomaya kosha keeps long-term memory stored, **including the [[concepts/samskars|samskars]] of several lifetimes**. Erasing these memories is a necessity for spiritual advancement. The *vritti* or whirlpool of thought happens at this level. Most decisions, creative or otherwise, happen here. The mind is the seed of action — all actions begin at this kosha. This is the body that creates a karmic delusion.

### 4. Vigyanamaya Kosha (Intellect Body)

When a person proceeds toward this kosha, partial delusion remains but relief begins. Wisdom is an attribute of this kosha; *buddhi* or intellect dominates existence.

> It makes a human being more focused towards keeping up the momentum of transcending to higher consciousness. It uses will power to eliminate or constrict identification with sensory pleasures. It inspires self-enlightenment instead of self-gratification and suppression of the senses. The person learns to transcend duality. It is a state of realisation, intimation and philanthropy.
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.7, p.118

The person remains within the purview of [[concepts/maya|maya]] and the loop of ego identification, but transcending the feeling of doer-ship becomes possible here.

### 5. Anandamaya Kosha (Bliss Body)

The final frontier, experienced only by a selected few.

> It is not about thrill or joy but more about a feeling of bliss and the realisation of not being an identity or an individual, of not being a doer, of having transcended feelings of guilt and pride. It is a state of enlightenment, which is achieved only by the most evolved beings.
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.7, p.119

Such a level of consciousness exists in saints who can heal people by intent and shield against destiny, neutralising the rays of the stars and planets. Gods and prophets have attained this state.

During this state, the physical body becomes lifeless but is fully conscious, surrounded by a capsule of bliss and thoughtlessness. A person here is capable of universal love, identifying with all forms of existence. This person ceases to be an individual and exists purely as a *sakshi* or witness — going beyond the effects of karmayoga and the doctrine of sins and good deeds.

People who die at this level have access to the highest realms of existence and become *ichhadhari* — those who can will their own destiny. At this level, a person transcends the three gunas of tamas, rajas, and sattva. When one transcends this body, the person ceases to exist and attains moksha — the ultimate goal.

## Structural Note

The five koshas map cleanly onto the progression from ordinary embodied life to liberation:

| Kosha | Primary pull | Status re: maya |
|---|---|---|
| Annamaya | Physical comfort, senses | Fully within maya |
| Pranamaya | Spiritual shakti | Between material and ethereal |
| Manomaya | Identity, memory, emotion | Karmic delusion; samskars stored here |
| Vigyanamaya | Intellect, wisdom, will | Partial delusion; doer-ship can be loosened |
| Anandamaya | Bliss, universal love, witness | Beyond maya; karma mukta; moksha |
