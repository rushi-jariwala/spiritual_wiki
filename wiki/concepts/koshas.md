# Koshas

> *The five sheaths of consciousness that surround the jivaatma. One must climb through them to surmount the ego.*

## Status

**Stub.** This concept is referenced in Chapter 1 of *Karma Sutra* but not unpacked there. The five koshas are named in the book's table of contents as part of a later section ("Circle of Life After Life"). Fuller treatment will be added when that section is ingested.

## What Chapter 1 Says

> The jivaatma is surrounded by five sheaths (levels) of consciousness called the koshas. Unless one learns to climb the ladder of consciousness from one kosha to the other, one cannot surmount ego.
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.1, p.12

This is the only mention in Chapter 1. What we can infer:
- There are **five** sheaths
- They surround the [[concepts/jivaatma|jivaatma]]
- They are hierarchical — a "ladder"
- Traversing them is the structural path out of ego-identification
- Without climbing them, the seeker cannot transcend the "I" principle, which means cannot transcend [[concepts/maya|maya]], which means cannot reach [[concepts/moksha|moksha]]
