# Loks

> *"Climbing the ladder needs effort and balance, sliding down the snake is gravitational."*
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VIII, p.88

## Definition

Loks are multiple levels of existence where beings dwell according to their qualifications. Each lok represents a particular range of consciousness — simultaneously the entry requirement and the defining quality of that realm. Beings exist there until those qualities alter and new qualifications lead to change.

Hingori presents the lok system using a 14-storey building analogy: our galaxy, the Milky Way, is the building. Earth — *Bhu lok* — is the seventh floor. Seven loks lie above Earth; seven lower loks (*tals*) lie below. The grossest form of matter in all the loks is the physical body of Earth's inhabitants; bodies become progressively subtler at higher levels. The upper loks are brighter; the lower tals are domains of limited light or partial darkness.

> [!tip] Bhu lok is the only lok through which beings from *all* other loks must pass. It is the karma lok — the only one that provides "the infrastructure for the relevant criss-cross visas." Evolution or devolution is only possible through rebirth on Earth.

## The Lower Loks (Tals)

### 6th Floor — Atal

The guna mix is a few shades lower than Earth's; *rajas* is predominant, combined with *tamas*. Fear and lust are the governing emotions. Residents include nobles with selfish attitudes, senior "I, me and mine" corporate executives, shrewd businessmen, corrupt doctors, those with multiple extramarital affairs, people indulging in sexual fantasising or masturbation. The corrective aim is to overcome lust and fear and develop self-confidence, self-discipline, and self-control — qualities that earn rebirth on Earth.

### 5th Floor — Vital

The level of ignorance rises; gratification of the senses is a greater aspiration. Hingori conjectures a guna mix of approximately 20% sattva, 40% rajas, 40% tamas. Residents: ambitious warriors, scientists with negative ambitions, dishonest businessmen, politically-minded corporate executives, self-centred and violent people — including those with riches, social standing, or Ivy League educations who used their advantages selfishly; corrupt priests; bribe-demanding government officers.

> "These loks are not ugly places, merely darker, in light, concept and consciousness."
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VIII, p.84

### 4th Floor — Sutal

Residents self-reflect as demonic and less positive; their *roops* (guna-forms) are harsh and their eyes lack kindness. Yet the desire for self-improvement is still present — a distinguishing feature from loks below. Vital and Sutal share a common denominator: lower self-opinions, negative samskars, and self-perception as guilt.

### 3rd / 2nd Floors — Talatal / Mahatal

Darkness prevails in greater measure, though not necessarily uncomfortable in the sense of active suffering. Residents include pseudo agnostics, self-realised sinners, and terrorists. Those who build sufficient resolve to change earn rebirth on Earth — but as lives of suffering: illness, financial problems, social dissatisfaction, a near-constant "Why me?" existence. Multiple corrective births are required to reverse trajectory.

### 1st Floor — Patal

The lowest level named in scripture. Tamas of the negative kind is dominant; self-reflection is not even a concept. Delusion and ignorance are at their peak.

> "Living in the pits is not a pun, it's literal. It's like living in the shadows with the power off."
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VIII, p.85

Plug-ins are hatred, jealousy, supreme guilt, envy, sorrow, basest emotions, and demonic nature — leading to demonic *roops*. Residents: Hitler, Napoleon, terrorists who murder freely, professional killers. Liberation becomes possible only when one has suffered enough to build a genuine resolve to change.

## Earth — Bhu Lok (7th Floor)

Earth is the karma lok — the crossroads of all existence. Its inhabitants reflect the gunas of various loks, because they arrive from everywhere and are heading everywhere. Earth is described as a cocktail of different qualities — and crucially, because it is the karma lok, it is possible here to go against one's inherited nature and qualify for better loks.

> *The playing field looks fair.*
> *The tools are intent.*
> *The obstacle is destiny and samskars.*
> *And time is short.*
> *Background music is maya or delusion.*
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VIII, p.86

Sliding down is gravitational; climbing requires effort and balance — "spiritual ballooning." Divine beings from higher loks descend to Earth to serve less evolved beings and to complete their own final attainments. Many gurus are obligated to take rebirth to uplift disciples.

> "The latest and one of the greatest was the Mahaguru whose karma bhumi was Gurgaon and afterlife headquarters is Najafgarh. While most prophets came with a small contingent of a few, he brought along hundreds of beings from higher loks to accomplish their joint mission."
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VIII, pp.86–87

See [[entities/mahaguru]].

## The Upper Loks

### 8th–9th Floors — Bhuvar Lok (Astral Plane)

The first lok above Earth. Moving from Bhu lok to Bhuvar lok requires shifting from predominantly tamas to more rajas and a dash of sattva.

### 10th Floor — Swa Lok / Swarg / Heaven / Jannat

Geographically located between the Sun and the Pole Star. The lok of intellect, memory, relationships, heroic attitudes, and emotions — and also of attainment. *Seva* leads here or beyond. Swarg is a place of immense pleasure and beauty where positive karmic currency is spent. The downside: good deeds get exhausted in pleasure, and when spiritual assets dwindle, residents return to Earth — reborn as kings, queens, administrators, or celebrities.

Gurudev's phrase: *"Tapo raj, rajo narak"* — after *tapasya*, one is born in kingship; after misusing kingship, one descends again. Those who are philanthropic and kind retain their lok status.

### 11th Floor — Mahar Lok

Lies beyond the *Dhruv Tara* (Pole Star). The land of the wise and balanced: selfless service, compassion, and self-love are the defining qualities. Karmas are no longer doable at this level — residents are *karm-mukt*. When they take birth on Earth they come as teachers, reformers, saintly people, and healers.

### 12th Floor — Jana Lok

Entry requires transcending devotion and beliefs and attaining the status of *gyan* (intuitive knowledge). The senses must be history at this point.

> "Till you harbour emotions and don't qualify for gyan or intuitive knowledge, you can only read about this lok, but not live here."
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VIII, p.91

### 13th Floor — Tapa Lok

The lok of *Divya Bhakti* and self-reflection of divinity. No judgements on others or self; the beginning of identifying as one with universal consciousness. Corresponds to the third eye in the body. When duality becomes an amalgam and wisdom hands the baton to *sakshibhav* (observership), one is catapulted here.

Hingori's personal testimony: Gurudev opened his third eye 35 years ago, then deliberately closed it — scanning from head to lower back — to prevent Hingori from becoming absorbed in third-eye capabilities and getting stuck at the Tapa Lok level. He did this for most disciples. See [[stories/hingori-third-eye-opened-closed]].

### 14th Floor — Satya Lok

Corresponds to the *Sahasrara chakra* — the gateway to final emancipation. Beings here do not recognise themselves as individuals but as divinity personified. They become *ichchaadhari* — able to manipulate planetary trends and neutralise them. They have an open visa for all loks and administer the spiritual attainments of others, assigning beings from higher loks with missions across the universe.

### Beyond Satya Lok

It is believed that loks such as *Vaikunth*, *Guru lok*, *Shiv lok*, and *Bhram lok* exist beyond Satya lok — parallel and variable according to the inherent consciousness of the beings (whether Shiva, Vishnu, or Brahma).

> [!warning] Hingori explicitly disagrees with scriptures that place *moksh* within the lok framework: "In disagreement to many scriptures I would say that moksh lies beyond these loks too." See [[concepts/moksha]].

## Gurudev's Revelation — The Dhruv Tara Crossroads

Gurudev shared with Hingori in confidence that in the afterlife, the soul must first pass through the *Dhruv Tara* (Pole Star) to reach its destination. At that point one encounters crossroads: one side bare-looking, the other aesthetically lovely.

> "He warned that the bare ones are the higher ones, and we should not get attracted to the lovely looking ones because of our sense of sight."
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VIII, p.93

The snare at death mirrors the snare on Earth: sense-perception mistaken for a guide to reality.

## Colour Codes of the Loks

From Hingori's personal visits (see [[stories/hingori-lok-visits]]):
- Residents of darker loks wear colour-coded togas of **dull green**.
- Evolved souls who descended to Earth with Gurudev wear a **slightly cream** toga.

> [!question] The colour-coding is offered tentatively ("I suspect") and derives from a single set of visits. It is an observation, not a doctrine.

## Method of Ascent — Spiritual Ballooning

One cannot climb to higher loks — one must float up. The process is lightening the load of samskars and karmic debts and moving from grosser to subtler mind-set. The guna mix is the primary instrument. See [[concepts/gunas]] for the self-assessment tool.

Gurudev's *ek vakya*: *"Apni mauth ko saath leker phirna"* — always be conscious and prepared for death, for that itself will enhance the value of life.

> "I conclude with the most significant line I can conjure and that is — Do not try to spend a pleasurable life, but instead a more invested one."
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VIII, p.94

## Statutory Warning

> [!tip] "'H' for Harvard is not necessarily 'H' for Heaven." Worldly achievement and education do not determine lok qualification — the guna mix does.

Some rulers of the lower loks are advanced beings with immense power. The system is corrective, not punitive beyond recovery. It is only when one has suffered enough and built a resolve to change that an opportunity for rebirth on Earth arises.
