# Mahurat

## Definition

A **mahurat** is a person's date and time of birth, calculated at a particular cross-section of time. In the Hingori framework, birth does not happen randomly — the mahurat is the karmic matching instrument. A soul requires a birth timing that aligns with its accumulated [[concepts/sanchit-karma|sanchit]] balance, so that the current life's [[concepts/prarabdh-karma|prarabdh]] can work out the stored karma.

> A person's destiny is the fructification of his/her karmic balance sheet. It stands to reason that a person will need a matching mahurat at birth. To have your karma balances reduced, a suitable birth time is a must. A perfect mahurat may not be possible, so I believe birth takes place to ensure that at an average, your karmas are worked out — of course with a little extra — plus or minus.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.5, p.88

## Core Teaching

The "Catch 22" of mahurat:

> The 'Catch 22' situation here is that every mahurat (a person's date and time of birth is calculated at a particular cross section of time), will have predictable luck in various spheres.
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.5, p.88

The horoscope derived from the mahurat reflects:

- Wealth · health · relationships · education · career · marriage · romance
- Nature · spirituality · criminal leanings · lifespan

These are not arbitrary planetary influences — they are the readable shape of a person's karmic balance sheet, projected into time.

## Nuances

- A person with more positive sanchit has a better destiny, with pockets of bad time to exhaust negative balances.
- A person with more negative sanchit generally has more bad luck, sorrow, poverty, strife, and very few segments of good luck.
- Being wealthy or famous does not guarantee an absence of suffering; simpler circumstances do not ensure unhappiness.
- Happiness quotient can be read in the mahurat "to a fairly accurate degree."

> [!question] What determines which mahurat a soul gets?
> Hingori implies the match is automatic — the soul's karmic balance requires a certain birth window. The mechanism by which this selection happens is not elaborated in Ch.5.
