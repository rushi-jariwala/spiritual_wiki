# Mahurat

## Definition

A **mahurat** is a person's date and time of birth, calculated at a particular cross-section of time. In the Hingori framework, birth does not happen randomly â€” the mahurat is the karmic matching instrument. A soul requires a birth timing that aligns with its accumulated [[concepts/sanchit-karma|sanchit]] balance, so that the current life's [[concepts/prarabdh-karma|prarabdh]] can work out the stored karma.

> A person's destiny is the fructification of his/her karmic balance sheet. It stands to reason that a person will need a matching mahurat at birth. To have your karma balances reduced, a suitable birth time is a must. ## Nuances

- A person with more positive sanchit has a better destiny, with pockets of bad time to exhaust negative balances.
- A person with more negative sanchit generally has more bad luck, sorrow, poverty, strife, and very few segments of good luck.
- Being wealthy or famous does not guarantee an absence of suffering; simpler circumstances do not ensure unhappiness.
- Happiness quotient can be read in the mahurat "to a fairly accurate degree."

## Nuances (Aatma Sutra Ch.V)

*Aatma Sutra* Ch.V develops the mechanical picture further. The frozen birth chart (*kundali*) records the position of all planets across 12 zodiac signs at the exact moment and place of birth. The interaction between a planet's transiting position and its birth-chart position produces the ever-changing texture of a life's karma fructifying.

> "Since the planets move at different paces, their chemistry does not remain the same as at the *mahurat* of birth. In fact, this chemistry is dynamic and displays a number of configurations leading to innumerable differing results."
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.V, p.54
