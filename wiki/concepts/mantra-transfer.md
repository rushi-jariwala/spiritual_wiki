# Mantra Transfer

## Definition

Mantra transfer is the mahaguru's practice of directly bestowing spiritual attainments on disciples — either by declaring them *siddh* in a mantra they have not yet chanted, or by allocating a portion of his own accumulated mantra count to their spiritual account. It is a one-directional gift of earned spiritual currency, flowing from guru to disciple, used to accelerate the disciple's evolution.

## Forms of Transfer

### Declaring Siddhi via a Laung

Hingori describes being summoned to Gurudev's room, made to swallow a clove (*laung*), and then told he was now *siddh* (accomplished) in a specific mantra — one he did not know a single syllable of:

> One day, he called me to his room, made me swallow a laung and announced that I was now siddh in a specific mantra. I was baffled since I didn't know a single syllable of that mantra! A few days later, when I asked him for the words of the mantra in which I was already siddh, he gave them to me over the phone.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.84

The sequence inverts the ordinary order: typically a practitioner chants a mantra for years to attain siddhi. Here, Gurudev transferred the accumulated attainment first; the words came after. The siddhi preceded the practice.

### Allocating Paath Hours

Gurudev also transferred the spiritual benefit of his own daily practice directly to disciples:

> "Gurudev instructed me to do my paath from 11 pm to 2 am every night and told me that he would do paath on my behalf from 2 am to 5 am every morning, allocating its benefit to me. His greatness and selflessness were beyond measure!"
> — Ravi ji, recalled in *The Guru of Gurus*, p.85

In this form, Gurudev performed the discipline on the disciple's behalf and credited the spiritual points to the disciple's account — while asking nothing in return except that the disciple make their own parallel effort.

### The Limit of Magnanimity

This generosity had a stated boundary. When Bittu ji and Pappu ji repeatedly neglected their own mantra practice while Gurudev continued covering for them, he said:

> "I cannot keep allocating a share of my mantra count to you if you don't make an effort to earn some spiritual points of your own!"
> — Gurudev, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.85

## Time-Specific Mantra Recitation — The Guru-Paher

Beyond transferring siddhi, Gurudev also taught *when* to recite mantras for maximum efficacy. He recommended disciples recite their mantras at specific times, and specified the **guru-paher** — the time-band between **1:15 am and 3:30 am** — as the optimal window for tasks requiring higher spiritual efficacy.

Gurudev himself used this band: he would go to bed around 1:30 am, do his paath for an hour or two, then catch a few winks before waking between 5 and 6 am. Mataji observed: *"I felt he had gained mastery over sleep because he hardly slept. When he did sleep, it was because he wished to and not because sleep had overcome him."*

His words on this practice: *"No one will ever know where I go. But I watch over people and guide them."* — indicating that his guru-paher paath was not simply recitation but astral work carried out while the world slept.

## What It Reveals

Mantra transfer makes explicit what the tradition implies throughout: spiritual attainment is a transferable currency, not merely a private achievement. The guru's tapasya creates a reserve that can be drawn upon by others. This is consistent with the broader teaching that [[concepts/intent-as-healing|the guru's intent is the operative healing agent]] — the mechanism is the same; only the target shifts from a single healing act to the disciple's long-term evolution.

## Mantra Practice — Gurudev's Instructions

*The Guru of Gurus* (Tools of Spiritual Transformation, pp.158–165) gives the fullest account of how Gurudev expected disciples to work with mantras. Transfer (above) is the guru's side; practice is the disciple's side.

### Origin of the Mahagayatri

At their first meeting in the forest, the elusive Buddhe Baba gave Gurudev the **eight missing words** in the mantra he was reciting — converting the Gayatri into the **Mahagayatri**. The extended mantra accelerated Gurudev's spiritual progress and became the precursor to all his subsequent attainments. See [[entities/buddhe-baba]].

### What a Mantra Is

Mantras are sound energy. Everything vibrates at a natural frequency; each frequency has a unique form. Repeated mantra recitation progressively aligns the practitioner's vibration with the vibration of the power the mantra represents. When alignment is complete, the practitioner is *siddh* in that mantra, and the power becomes accessible for self-protection, healing, and helping others.

Gurudev worked with approximately **eight mantras**, some of which were combinations of two or three. Their range: warding off negative energies · halting death and extending life · tapping into higher forms of consciousness.

### The Three Constants of Practice

Gurudev insisted on three unchanging elements during regular mantra practice:

**1. Asana (seat/place)**
Mantras should be recited while seated on a specific asana. Examples: Mahamritunjay mantra on a Kusha (Halfa) grass mat; Mahagayatri in a body of water (bathtub); Chamunda mantra in a forest or on deerskin. The asana must be used consistently — the same one for every session.

A Kusha grass mat reduces aura loss. Always use the same sheet or blanket wrapped around you so that your clothing retains your aura and dissipates very little into the environment.

**2. Fixed Time**
Gurudev advised setting aside a fixed time for meditation so that the body clock becomes subconsciously prepared. Science has validated that regular habits form in the basal ganglia — repeated behaviour patterns create new neuronal connections aligned to the habit. Mantra practice at a fixed time ensures the brain is pre-conditioned for meditation.

**3. Water (Jal) by the Asana**
A glass of water with a bowl on its lid should be kept on either or both sides of the asana during practice. During mantra recitation, sound waves are converted into electromagnetic waves via the membrane potential of body cells. The water magnetises any electromagnetic waves that escape from the auric layer, minimising the loss. Aura conservation is described as *"a critical aspect of spiritual hygiene — it takes a lifetime to increase your aura's potency."*

Heat produced by specific mantras is offset by water intake. Water can also wash away subtle negative energies — disciples prefer showering after seva for this reason.

### Ajapa Japa

Mantras should be recited **mentally** — never aloud — with sounds directed inward. Gurudev instructed disciples to continue silent internal recitations (*ajapa japa*) through the monotony of daily routines: standing, sitting, working, driving. Reciting a mantra before sleep causes it to run in the subconscious mind until waking — his named secret tip for multiplying mantra repetitions without additional time.

### Round Pillow

Gurudev advised using a round pillow for both mantra-based meditation and sleeping. According to physics, any force acting on a spherical object collects at its centre. The dissipated auric energy collects at the pillow's centre; when the crown chakra makes contact with the pillow, the accumulated energy is returned.

### Mahashivratri Jal

A day after Mahashivratri, a special jal is distributed across all sthans. It is made from water collected from **five rivers**, poured together, then enhanced with cloves, black peppercorns, green cardamoms, and other ingredients chosen for medicinal or purifying properties. Spiritually advanced disciples energise this jal by immersing their hands while reciting mantras.

The Mahashivratri jal:
- Can be stored for years without smelling bad
- Retains potency even after being diluted three times
- During Gurudev's time, people queued for hours for a bottle

### Water as a Healer

Sound travels through water five times faster than through air. Water can detect sounds directed at it and alter its molecular structure without changing its chemical composition — Hingori describes this as evidence that water *"has its own consciousness."*

Healing mantras are recited in ajapa mode; their sound is carried to the diseased part of the body by prana (breath). When two frequencies interact, one begins to resonate at the same frequency as the other — this process is called *entrainment*. Regularly reciting a healing mantra entrains the malfunctioning body part and affects a cure. Gurudev also predicted that people would one day place a glass of water at his samadhi at Neelkanth Dham, circumambulate it, and drink the energised water for relief.

### Physiological Effects

Mantras lower blood pressure, heart rate, and respiration rate; increase oxygen flow to the brain while decreasing its utilisation; slow brainwave activity; improve concentration; synchronise the left and right brain hemispheres. Greater brain-mind coordination raises the level of conscious awareness.

> "Greater the brain-mind coordination, the higher is the level of conscious awareness."
> — *The Guru of Gurus*, p.165
