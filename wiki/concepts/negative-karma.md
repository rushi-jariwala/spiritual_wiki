# Negative Karma

## Definition

Actions, thoughts, and attitudes that create a debit on the karmic balance sheet — obligations owed to others that must be repaid, in this life or across future lives.

> Karein burai sukh chahe, kaise pavey koye.
> Ropey ped babool ka, aam kahan te hoye.
>
> *Kabir questions how can one achieve happiness by performing bad deeds? Just like one cannot get mangoes from sowing a babool tree. Remember, you reap as you sow.*
> — Kabir, quoted in *Karma Sutra*, Ch.3, p.45

[[concepts/karma]]

## Core Teaching

Negative karma is not only about dramatic acts of harm. It accumulates through thought, attitude, and the quiet mechanics of social transaction — criticising, cheating, encashing praise. Hingori's framework treats every category as an **accounting entry**, not a moral verdict.

## The Five Categories

### 1. Criticising Others

Holding grudges, thinking negatively, or hurling silent curses at another person generates negative karma in two ways:

- It **nullifies** (in part or whole) whatever that person owes you for their own negative acts towards you — effectively cancelling a receivable.
- It **adds fresh debit** to your own balance sheet.

> Do not burden yourself with negative thoughts, actions and karmas in the mind.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.3, p.45

[[concepts/karma]] · [[concepts/doer-ship]]

> [!tip]
> The trap is invisible: a grudge feels like it belongs to the other person. Accountingly, it belongs entirely to you.

### 2. Acts of Unfairness

Cheating in business or trade, stealing money, ideas, or materials, lying, and deceiving all accumulate negative karma.

> Being less wealthy is better than resorting to deceitful means, because avoiding illusionary richness makes us wealthier in real terms. Monetary riches do not make a person wealthy.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.3, p.47

The counter-example offered is [[entities/mahaguru|Gurudev]] — a soil surveyor on a meagre salary in a 250-yard house in Gurgaon — who possessed immeasurable wealth in goodwill, human values, shakti, healing ability, and mastery of the mind.

> Bas ke dushwar hai har kaam ka aasaan hona, aadmi ko mayesar nahin insaan hona.
> *(While it is difficult for every task to become easy or achievable, even man does not always succeed in becoming human.)*
> — Mirza Ghalib, quoted in *Karma Sutra*, Ch.3, p.47

### 3. Criminal Acts That Harm Others

Physical harm, killing, and sexual assault generate debt that **cannot be settled in a single lifetime**.

> Taking someone's life is almost like owing one's own to that person. The payments of such debts occur over many lifetimes and put a heavy burden on the wrongdoer.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.3, p.49

Legal punishment and imprisonment count as **partial repayment only** — a fraction of the total karmic obligation. The remainder is carried forward in [[concepts/sanchit-karma|sanchit]] and drawn into future lives.

> [!warning]
> Sexual assault is cited as generating a debt "that cannot be a small debt" — the psychological damage inflicted on the victim is the measure, not the legal sentence served.

### 4. Mental Hurt and Trauma

Causing psychological suffering — infidelity, wrongful dismissal from employment, humiliation — is a karmic debt owed to the person who suffers.

Infidelity is noted specifically: *"It is therefore a debt of both involved in the act"* — both parties accrue karma, not only the one who acts.

**The soldier exception:**

> If you are a soldier and in a war to protect the country, you do not have to pay for the negative karma as you are not desirous of hurting the other person for your gain or satisfaction. You are defending your country, and yourself, from the enemy. There is no intent to harm in such an act. Nor is it an egoistic act.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.3, p.51

The operative variable is **intent** (see [[concepts/karma]] — intent as the primary karmic variable). Harm without personal desire or ego does not accrue karma.

> [!tip]
> However — guilt overrides the exception: *"if you feel guilty killing while protecting your country then the guilt, if not the act, will make you acquire negative karma."* The guilt is itself an acceptance of doer-ship. See [[concepts/doer-ship]].

### 5. Encashment of Good Deeds

Advertising or publicising a good deed — accepting fame, social positioning, political leverage, or public praise as a return — cashes out the positive karma that deed generated.

> This is negative karma, because the positivity earned is lost, as you have already derived the benefits from it.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.3, p.53

Forms of encashment include:
- Publicly announcing donations
- Naming funded rooms, buildings, or infrastructure after oneself or one's ancestors
- Sitting on a pedestal and being praised at a charity event
- Accepting any appreciation that converts the deed into social capital

> [!tip]
> Even partial praise dilutes partial positive karma. The rule is not absolute cancellation in every case — Hingori says it "can negate *some* of the positive effects."

This is the inverse of the counter-gift rule established in Ch.2 (see [[stories/sadhu-blanket-clove]]): accepting return on a good deed, in any form, depletes the karmic profit.

## Nuances

- **Thought counts.** Negative thoughts (grudges, curses) generate karma even without physical action. The mind is a karma-producing organ.
- **Intent is the primary variable** — but guilt is a secondary mechanism that can override even blameless acts by conferring doer-ship retroactively.
- **Multi-lifetime debt.** The most severe negative karma (killing, assault) cannot be repaid in a single life; it accumulates in sanchit and distributes across future prarabdh.
- **Partial legal repayment.** Human justice is real but insufficient — imprisonment reduces the karmic balance but does not zero it.

## In Practice

- Observe negative thought patterns (grudges, criticism) as ledger entries, not emotional states.
- Avoid any public acknowledgment of good deeds performed — no naming, no praise, no announcements.
- Apply the intent test before assigning guilt: *was this done for personal gain or ego?* If not, guilt itself may be the only karma generated.
