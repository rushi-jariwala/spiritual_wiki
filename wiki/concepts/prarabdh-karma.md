# Prarabdh Karma

> *The karmas drawn from sanchit into the present life — the shape of one's destiny as luck.*

## Definition

> The karmas that fructify during a person's lifetime in the form of his/her luck are prarabdh karmas.
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.1, p.7

Prarabdh is the slice of the [[concepts/sanchit-karma|sanchit]] warehouse that this incarnation has been allotted. It is what *plays out* in the present life as circumstance, fortune, and constraint — the script of Hingori's "movie of destiny."

## Core Teaching

Prarabdh is **predetermined but not without agency**:

> Though our luck is predetermined and so are our prarabdh karmas, how we accept that luck is a game to play. Today's fortune is yesterday's effort; so today, we have an opportunity to sow the seeds for the next crop, while we enjoy the present one.
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.1, p.7

The circumstances are fixed; the response is free. And the response itself generates fresh karma, seeding what comes next.

## Nuances

### The Mahurat

A person's prarabdh is locked in at the moment of birth — the **mahurat** — which has its own star chart and planetary configuration. Astrology, in Hingori's framing, is the science that reads this chart. Planetary movement through life is the *dynamic* chart, whose interplay of angles, conjunctions, and vibrations creates the specific conditions under which destined events fructify: events occur, feelings arise, coincidences happen, thought patterns shift, all leading to the fulfillment of prarabdh.

### Most Prarabdh Is Ordinary

> Most people's prarabdh karma involves hard work and humongous effort for either commercial sustenance or social upliftment. Everyone is not always lucky to have the luxury of doing good deeds or practicing charity.
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.1, p.7

Prarabdh is not usually glamorous. Most lives are a grind. The spiritual opportunity lies not in the circumstances themselves but in how one meets them.

### The Script Has a Mix

Hingori stresses that even strong positive prarabdh produces a mix of good fortune and difficulty, because a pure screenplay would not be a life. Suffering can be physical, mental, emotional, or spiritual. Where the balance is less positive, suffering dominates, but in every case the actor retains the ability to *act graciously, be kinder, think philosophically, and have a positive attitude* — improving the profit-and-loss account of this life regardless of the script.

## In Practice

- **Do not confuse luck with achievement.** Prarabdh karma often masquerades as the result of one's hard work, cleverness, or timing. Being grateful for luck preserves future positive karma; taking ownership of it erodes it (see [[concepts/doer-ship]]).
- **Work with the script, not against it.** Location, sequence, and setting are fixed. Delivery is not.
- **Use ingenuity in the ordinary.** Small gestures of helpfulness at work, kindness on the commute, attention in mundane moments — these are how new karma is sown while prarabdh is exhausted.
- **A guru can re-balance.** A sadguru may grant relief by activating positive prarabdh early, or by providing the "umbrella" of his energy bank to soften negative periods. See [[concepts/guru-disciple]], [[stories/gurus-umbrella-wife-delivery]], [[stories/scooter-accident-vision]].

> [!tip]
> "If you have to evolve in the future, the present will find the way. Your prarabdh karmas will manipulate your actions for their fulfillment."
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.1, p.25
