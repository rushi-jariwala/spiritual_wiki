# Samskars

## Definition

**Samskars** are the impressions or *engrams* left on the subconscious mind by karmas from current and previous lifetimes. When combined with emotions and ownership, karmas get stored as samskars and re-fructify at some point in the future. The samskar is the form of storage of [[concepts/sanchit-karma|sanchit karmas]].

> Samskars are the impressions or engrams left on the subconscious mind by karmas from current and previous lifetimes. When combined with emotions karmas get stored as samskars and re-fructify again at some point in the future. This process will continue for as long as we continue to attach emotions to our future actions and take ownership of them. Actions with a price tag attached is karmas with emotions and ownership.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.I, p.9

> When we do any karma, we observe our acts at different levels. If we expect it to bear fruit or result, (as reward or punishment) this desire (good or bad, happy or sad) gets stored in our deeper consciousness as an impression. This storage is meant to pass on from one life to another, one body to another and fructify in the future.
> â€” Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.128

The formation condition is precise: emotion + ownership. An act with neither leaves no samskar. "Actions with a price tag attached" is the Aatma Sutra's compact formulation.

## The USB Stick Analogy

Hingori's central metaphor: the karmashaya (the storehouse of samskars) is a USB stick; the [[concepts/kaarna-sharir|kaarna sharir]] (causal body) is the USB port. Short-term memory (smruti) and long-term impressions (samskars) are both stored on this stick. Once they fructify, they are removed â€” unless they get restored as new samskars.

> Your list of things to do for today are your samskars. The motivation to do them is your vasna and fulfilling the list is your karma. A dash of emotion or a sense of doer-ship converts them back into samskars.
> â€” Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.129

## Smruti vs Samskar

| | Smruti | Samskar |
|---|---|---|
| **Type** | Short-term memory | Long-term impression |
| **Scope** | This life's incidents | Across several lifetimes |
| **Fate** | Eventually merges into samskars | Fructifies and is removed â€” unless renewed |

Both are stored in the same location (the karmashaya / USB stick).

## The Vicious Circle

The samskars we carry are the fructifications of past karma. When they arise as [[concepts/vasna|vasnas]] (tendencies), they are meant only to exhaust the stored data. But the 'I'-sense intervenes:

1. A stored samskar fructifies as a vasna (inclination to act)
2. The prarabdh karma plays out â€” the act occurs
3. The person perceives the act as *their own* ("Who done it? I done it!")
4. Emotion and ownership colour the act
5. The act is re-coded as a new samskar â€” the USB stick is refilled

> Our ignorance leads to our expectation of a fruit or result from these compensatory karmas. So, we buy into ownership and get back into the vicious circle.
> â€” Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.131

## Gyan Breaks the Circle

Knowledge (gyan) neutralises the expectation of fruit. With gyan, one recognises that the karma playing out is a compensatory karma â€” its purpose is to exhaust old data, not to produce new results. Detachment prevents the emotional stamp that would re-encode it:

> Gyan would have neutralised such an expectation with the realisation that we should not expect our karmas to bear any fruit or result. We would be detached from its effects... These samskars would not get stored in the USB and therefore there would be no addition to the data in the USB. Our stored data would partially play out in the current karmas, which would lighten or reduce stored data.
> â€” Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.131

## The Golden Rule: Bhog, Rog, Yog

*Aatma Sutra* Ch.III adds a compact three-way schema for where samskars lead:

> "**Golden Rule:** The positive *samskars* stored will be converted in the future into *bhog* (pleasure and riches). The negative *samskars* into *rog* (debt, disease, and misfortune). The *samskars* related to spirituality and divinity will get converted into *yog* (spiritual evolution). Aim for *yog*!"
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.III, p.32

The three destinations:
- *Bhog* â€” the fruit of positive worldly samskars; enjoyable but it is expenditure, not investment
- *Rog* â€” the fruit of negative samskars; suffering that exhausts the debt
- *Yog* â€” the fruit of spiritual samskars; the only category that advances liberation

## Gurudev's Phrase

> He used to say 'Everybody's Paap ka Ghada has to get filled up'. (Your pot of sin needs to get filled.)
> â€” Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.131

Hingori explains this through Patanjali: negative samskars unfold faster than good ones. Negative thoughts arise faster than positive ones in consciousness â€” which is why negativity sells quicker than positivity.

## The Non-doership Solution

Non-acceptance of deeds is not "skin deep." The observer within cannot be fooled by the mind â€” it records the impression at the level of the citta regardless of what the mind claims. Therefore the concept of [[concepts/non-doership|non-doer-ship]] has to sink deep and be a part of the belief system of mind, intellect, and the observatories of the inner bodies.

> We have to try and avoid getting emotions attached to the karmas. Our passions and desires, likes and aversions, joys and sorrows, give karmas a touch of 'I'. This state of mind converts karmas to samskars and uploads these karmas into our USB.
> â€” Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.132

## Depreciation on the Asset

> The concept of samskars are your spiritual assets. It is only wise and acceptable that you claim depreciation on your assets. Around 30â€“40 per cent should be achievable, since 100 per cent success is a myth.
> â€” Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.133

The aspiration is not zero karmic data â€” that would be moksha, achievable only by the most evolved. The realistic goal is to exhaust more than one adds in each life: at least a partial lightening of the stick.

## The "Next-Time Syndrome"

A specific samskar formation pathway identified in the closing synthesis (p.141): when we fail at something and attach guilt or shame to the failure, the guilt itself forms a new samskar â€” an impression of incompleteness that demands resolution. This creates a "next time" obligation that pulls the jivaatma back into further incarnation to complete it.

> We must allow ourselves to fail and accept our failures gracefully otherwise it will too form samskars and add to the burden of the 'next time' syndrome.
> â€” Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, p.141

The corollary: graceful acceptance of failure exhausts the karma of the failure without generating new storage. The outcome is the same (the act failed), but no new samskar is created.

## The Guru's Role

Guru's guidance is a short-cut to erasing samskars at the unconscious level. Deep meditation or samadhi, and certain mantra siddhis under a guru's guidance, can free the USB stick of some of its baggage. A guru can reduce some of a disciple's karmas by helping them fructify in the dream state.

> With sadhana (dedicated spiritual practice) and cleansing, a multiplier effect can take place. The vasnas may weaken in intensity. The dilution of the I-sense can help reduce the kleshas, dilute the strength of the vasnas, and make it easier for a disciple to reach a higher level of consciousness. It's a chain reaction.
> â€” Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.134
