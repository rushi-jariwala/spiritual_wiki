# Sanchit Karma

> *The accumulated stored balance of all a soul's carried-forward karmas, assets and liabilities both.*

## Definition

> The sum total of the assets and liabilities of your personal karmic balance sheet is called sanchit karma.
> â€” *Karma Sutra*, Ch.1, p.4

Sanchit is the **lifetime-spanning ledger** of every karma that was not absorbed as [[concepts/kriyaman-karma|kriyaman]]. It is carried by the [[concepts/jivaatma|jivaatma]] across incarnations and forms the raw material from which future lives are scripted.

## Core Teaching

The defining mechanic of sanchit is that **assets and liabilities do not cancel each other**:

> The profits and losses are both carried forward to the balance sheet where you cannot deduct one from the other. The assets and liabilities cannot cancel out each other. You have to suffer the negative balances as well as enjoy the positive ones, as determined by the karmic law.
> â€” *Karma Sutra*, Ch.1, p.4

This is why life is a mixture of happy, sad, and neutral moments â€” and why even a person with obvious material blessings still encounters illness, emotional setback, relationship issues, or business loss. The positive balances are fructifying alongside the negative ones, neither offset by the other.

## Nuances

### Why "Sanchit"

*Sanchit* (Sanskrit, "accumulated" / "stored") is the warehouse. [[concepts/prarabdh-karma|Prarabdh]] is what is drawn from this warehouse into the current life. [[concepts/kriyaman-karma|Kriyaman]] is the foot traffic that never enters the warehouse at all.

### The Release Pattern

> The karmas are exhausted not according to the ratio in which they are stored but as allowed by the destiny of this life.
> â€” *Karma Sutra*, Ch.1, p.5

Which sanchit karmas get drawn down in any given lifetime is determined not by proportion but by the [[concepts/prarabdh-karma|prarabdh]] assigned to that life's *mahurat* â€” the moment of birth, with its unique star chart.

### The Movie Metaphor

Hingori's extended metaphor: **life is a movie made long ago, witnessed in the present.** The content amalgamated from sanchit is the screenplay. The script includes the interactions in which old debts are squared off. A strong positive sanchit produces a birth into material, mental, or spiritual wealth, positive horoscope, strength, agility, and â€” at the very highest end â€” philanthropic, moral, intuitive lives like those of Krishna, Sita, Mother Teresa, Buddha, Raja Janak, or Ram.

> [!tip]
> "If the balance sheet is extremely strong, the lead actor will be of high morality, philanthropic, and be spiritually inclined and often intuitive."
> â€” *Karma Sutra*, Ch.1, p.5

## In Practice

- **You cannot deduct suffering from success.** Do not expect your blessings to compensate for your afflictions; they are drawn from separate lines of the same ledger.
- **Focus on building the warehouse.** Every positive carry-forward â€” seva, tapasya, generosity â€” deepens the asset side for future lives, even when it does not alter the script of this one.
- **Use the positive side well.** If you are karmically wealthy, the choice to share (philanthropy, seva) is itself an investment in future prarabdh. Hingori cites Bill Gates, Warren Buffett, the Birlas, and the Tatas as examples of balancing acts.
