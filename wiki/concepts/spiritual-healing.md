# Spiritual Healing

## Definition

Spiritual healing is the clearing of negativity — in any of its forms — from a person's aura, body, or life-thread, by a spiritually advanced being operating through dispassion, divine authority, and pure intent. Gurudev was described as among the most remarkable healers that ever walked the planet.

> Earth's history bears testimony to the presence of spiritually advanced beings endowed with magnetic energy and enriched auras. They could magnetise the negative energies manifesting in others and were advanced spiritual healers. Jesus, Shirdi Sai and Guru Nanak can be counted on this list of a few. Gurudev was among the most remarkable healers that ever walked this planet.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.219

## Sources of Negativity

Negativity manifests through six main channels:

1. **Negative samskars** — factored into the life by destiny
2. **Planetary influences** — physical and mental ailments resulting from specific planetary configurations
3. **Ageing body parts** — degeneration causing suffering
4. **Black magic** — performed on a person by another entity
5. **Spirit possession** — possessing spirits causing bodily damage; complicated for victims to identify
6. **Ancestral curses (pitra peeda)** — curses on a family line (kula) that ground a person's good luck; not targeted at any individual but borne heavily by some
7. **Crossover pickup** — unknowingly absorbing diseases removed from others and left on pavements, road crossings, etc.

> Therefore, sometimes you may be born with negativity, sometimes it can be thrown at you, and sometimes you may pick it up.
> — *The Guru of Gurus*, p.220

## Approach to Healing

Spiritual healing must be done with **dispassion and without nepotism**. Gurudev never used his healing powers on his own kith and kin — medicine, not spiritual intervention, came to their rescue. His children were told to learn to heal themselves. On some occasions, family members became instruments of seva (e.g., a young girl's typhoid was transferred to Gurudev's own daughter so the girl would be rid of the disease).

Despite being advanced healers themselves, Santosh ji is still troubled by kidney stones, Ravi ji by a chronic skin allergy, and Puran ji by orthopaedic problems. Gurudev explained this to Ravi ji:

> "You take away the pain of so many people. Sometimes a fraction of that pain has to be borne by you too."
> — Gurudev to Ravi ji, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.222

Being a healer comes with its own responsibilities.

### Faith is not a prerequisite

> While faith does appear to be a common denominator in the lives of the spiritually healed, the mahaguru did not believe in binding people to faith. The sthan was obligated to serve those who came for help and healing.
> — *The Guru of Gurus*, p.223

Those who stopped coming after their needs were met were released without obligation. Those who stayed became Gurudev's long-term responsibility for spiritual development.

## Non-Doership as the Frame

Gurudev consistently refused personal credit for healing:

> "I don't cure anyone who comes for help. They cure themselves. I am not the doer."
> — Gurudev, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.227

He recommended and practised the philosophy of [[concepts/non-doership|non-doership]] in all healing — only those *meant* to get cured came to him. He was merely the facilitator.

When Rajpal ji asked at London's Heathrow airport whether Bhagwan was upset by Gurudev's ability to change, erase, and rewrite people's destinies:

> "Bhagwan is within each being. Bhagwan has given me the stature of a guru and the authority that comes with it. I exercise this authority so that the sick can be healed."
> — Gurudev to Rajpal ji, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.225

## Handling Spirit Possession

Most practitioners capable of relieving spirits attack, torture, or overpower them. Gurudev's method was different: he treated spirits as extensions of himself and offered them a **reward** for freeing their victims.

> He would tell us that spirits and humans are similar except for their composition and capabilities. And since spirits are also a part of the Shiv-parivaar, it was our job to serve them too.
> — *The Guru of Gurus*, p.224

Spirits were offered the gift of human birth or passage to higher dimensions — the greatest possible reward, since spiritual transformation is faster in the human form than as a spirit. Gurudev also allowed certain animal spirits to be born as humans.

See [[stories/om-singh-thirteen-chains]] — the most striking documented possession case, where a man shackled to thirteen chains was freed with complete ease.

## Life Extension

> Just as he had the power to will birth for the spirits, he could extend human life by subtracting a few years from that person's future lifetime and adding it to his present. He could gift years in multiples of two or five.
> — *The Guru of Gurus*, p.225

His son Parvesh ji confirmed a case where a cancer-stricken woman was granted twenty additional years by Gurudev and lived to tell the tale.

## Modes of Healing

### Physical
- Touch or stare — the most direct mode; pain reduced and eventually disappeared when his hand was placed on a sick person
- Specific spices as carriers of healing energy: cloves, cardamoms, peppercorns, yellow mustard, areca nuts, and mantra-energised water, in different measures per ailment. Gurudev compiled approximately 100 natural prescriptions over time.

### Remote
His healing abilities extended beyond physical proximity:

> He could heal a person telephonically or mentally from afar. He could also heal someone he had never met or spoken to.
> — *The Guru of Gurus*, p.226

### Dreams and Visions
Dreams and visions served as conduits for healing rays — not just while he was alive but especially after his passing. See [[stories/bittu-burns-healing-dream]].

### Through Objects
Jal (sanctified water) retained its healing potency for years:

> Be it water sipped by the guru or energised with mantras, the jal retains its freshness and does not smell for years. It is truly an elixir of life.
> — *The Guru of Gurus*, p.229

### Unconventional Methods
He sometimes healed by doing precisely the opposite of what any logical mind would prescribe:
- Lady with a chronic sore throat told to eat spicy gol-gappas — voice restored. See [[stories/sore-throat-gol-gappas]].
- Rajpal ji's singing-damaged voice cured by eating a handful of snow from the ground. See [[stories/rajpal-hoarse-voice-snow]].
- Fungal infection on Giri ji's sister's hands cured by applying ghee on rotis at the sthan for eleven days. See [[stories/giri-sister-fungal-ghee]].

### The Power of Intention Over Method

> "Even mud from the sthan would work as effectively as any other spiritual treatment."
> — Gurudev, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.228

This is the practical expression of [[concepts/intent-as-healing]]. When Hingori accidentally gave a piles remedy to a hernia patient, the patient reported feeling better the following week. See [[stories/hingori-wrong-remedy-intention]].

## Laxmi Bandh — Energy Lock

Aside from physical and spiritual ailments, Gurudev could heal **Laxmi Bandh** — an energy block that causes persistent poverty or tight-fisted living, typically the result of a curse. Unblocking it ensured that "a poor man's buck would fetch more for any amount spent." See [[concepts/aura]].

## Diseases Healable

The documented range of conditions that Gurudev partly or wholly cured includes:
- Acute arthritis (Hingori's own case — cured in under a minute after ten years of suffering)
- Orthopaedic problems and body pains
- Abdominal and digestive tract issues
- Diabetes, jaundice
- Kidney and gall bladder stones
- Cancer (including blood cancer in children)
- Venereal diseases, burns, infections
- Multiple sclerosis, multiple personality disorders
- Depression, neurosis, psychosis
- Spirit possession
- Third-degree burns (Bittu ji regained sight and mobility)
- Fungal growths and skin conditions

## Healing as Seva

Spiritual healing was a significant part of Gurudev's seva. He did it without accepting a dime in return. Those he empowered with healing abilities received a threefold legacy:

1. To spiritually transform themselves through the medium of seva
2. To grant relief to others
3. To inspire others to walk in their shoes

> The world knew Gurudev as a miraculous healer who could heal at will. He cured people of incurable diseases within minutes.
> — *The Guru of Gurus*, p.228
