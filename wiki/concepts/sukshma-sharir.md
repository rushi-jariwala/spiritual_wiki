# Sukshma Sharir

## Definition

The **sukshma sharir** is the spirit, ethereal, or astral body. It is everlasting â€” it existed through past lives, exists now within the physical body, and will continue after this life.

> The *sukshma sharir* may be understood as the spirit, ethereal or astral body.
> Our spirit is everlasting and has existed through our many past lives, exists in our present life, and will continue to exist in our many future lives as well. It lies within the physical body and consists of the mind and the *kaarna sharir*.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.II, p.15

The [[concepts/isthul-sharir|Isthul Sharir]] (physical body) is temporary â€” assigned for a single lifetime. The sukshma sharir persists across all of them.

## Physical Nature of the Spirit Body

Chapter IV extends the description of the spirit body's material nature:

> The spirit body is made of ether, almost like a body made of smoke (one-tenth as dense as smoke) slightly lit from within. Because of its ultra-light weight and subtle vibration, it does not need to move slowly like its physical counterpart and it can fly at will.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.IV, p.42

This body barely receives thoughts; its thinking is substituted by observation. The evolved spirit body observes dispassionately; the unevolved remain stuck in attachments.

The spirit body requires *shakti* not only for sustenance but for travel between realms and for protection:

> The power or energy accumulated by the body also helps to protect itself from being bullied or captured by other spirit bodies. *Shakti* is a measure of the spirit's strength.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.IV, p.42

See also [[concepts/aura]] â€” the aura is the external radiation of this accumulated *shakti*.

## Goal: The Accumulation of Shakti

The goal of the spirit body is the accumulation of spiritual energy (*shakti*), which manifests as the aura:

> The goal of the spirit body or *sukshma sharir*, is the attainment of energy that can be used when it exists purely in its non-physical incarnation. This energy is called *shakti* and is manifested as the 'aura'.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.II, p.15

Since the spirit body is made of ethereal material (a far subtler vibration than the physical), it requires *shakti* for movement and sustenance in the non-physical state. The physical body's value lies precisely in its ability to generate this energy:

> **Golden rule:** Wealth across lifetimes is measured in the currency of *shakti*. You are only as rich as your aura and its potential.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.II, p.15

> [!tip] The physical body is the *instrument* by which the spirit body earns its wealth. This is the deeper purpose of human existence â€” not worldly accomplishment, but the accumulation of shakti.

## Duration of the Spirit Body

The spirit body can exist far longer between incarnations than the physical body does during a lifetime:

> You can exist in your spirit body probably 5 to 10 times longer than you will in your physical one.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.II, p.16

Between incarnations, the waiting period can be 100 years or more, depending on spiritual status and the realms previously inhabited. Rebirth is deferred until a *mahurat* (an auspicious planetary moment) can be found that matches the spirit's samskars for fructification. See [[concepts/mahurat]].

## The Loks: Realms of the Spirit Body

Spirit bodies dwell in *loks* â€” higher or lower earthly realms â€” between incarnations. The realm a spirit inhabits reflects its karmic and spiritual status:

> Spirit bodies that live in *loks* (higher earthly realms) for extended periods, are the fortunate ones. The unfortunate ones are stuck in lower realms, as they do not have the capacity or the *karma* to take themselves to different or higher realms.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.II, p.15â€“16

The loks are treated in full in Ch.VIII.

## Stuck Spirits: Bhoots and Jinns

Spirits without the karma to travel to higher realms get stuck â€” in their homes, on neighbouring trees, or at the place of their death:

> They get stuck in their homes, or on a neighbouring tree or even at the place of their death. These are colloquially referred to as haunting spirits or *bhoots* in Hindi.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.II, p.16

Some haunt and trouble others; some are helpful; others exist in suspension, waiting for their descendants to perform good deeds in their name so they can accumulate karma points and move on. Science has confirmed their existence and recorded their presence.

The more unfortunate spirits are captured by practitioners of black magic, or by *jinns* â€” powerful spirits that function as spirit goons, forcing captured spirits to serve them.

## Rebirth and the Matching of Samskars

Rebirth is not automatic â€” it requires a matching *mahurat* and the right family:

> To get rebirth that provides a destiny matching the fructification of your *samskars*, or at least a large part of it, is not an easy task. It is for this reason that sometimes a rebirth is deferred till a matching *mahurat* (an auspicious moment decided by the juxtaposition of the planets) is found. Sometimes, the spirit is born in its own family or *kul*, but for that to happen, it has to wait for the right time and opportunity.
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.II, p.16

The Tibetan Buddhist system of rebirth â€” where senior lamas locate reincarnated teachers by specific signs â€” is noted as a unique variant where the spirit is born outside its own family line. Hingori cites the case of [[stories/thuksey-rinpoche-reincarnation|Thuksey Rinpoche]] as evidence.

## Seh Sharir: Dematerialisation

A rare accomplishment of the most evolved saints is *seh sharir* â€” the ability to dematerialise the physical body and re-materialise it in a realm of their choice, effectively leaving no physical corpse behind. Known examples cited: Guru Nanak Dev, Guru Gorakhnath, Sant Kabir, Jesus, and â€” from the Gurudev lineage â€” Sitaramji (mentioned by Ramesh Chand Malhotra, a senior disciple of Gurudev).

> There are accounts of several great saints who left their human form *seh sharir* (moving on to the afterlife by de-materialising the physical body and having the capabilities to re-materialise it).
> â€” Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.II, p.18

## Astral Travel

An advanced application of the spirit body's subtle nature is **astral travel** â€” the voluntary exit of the sukshma sharir from the physical body to travel to other locations and realms. Gurudev practised this "at least thrice a week, for several hours at a stretch." Hingori himself experienced progressive stages: floating 3â€“4 inches above his physical body; dream-state travel with Gurudev to locations later verified in physical reality; and eventually the attainment of *gati* (superfast travel) taught by senior disciple R.C.
