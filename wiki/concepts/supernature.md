# Supernature

## Definition

**Supernature** is the transcendent nature of an advanced spiritual being — the sum of capacities that exceed all human and natural limits. In *The Guru of Gurus*, the term is used exclusively in relation to the mahaguru, and it is distinguished from *siddhis* (acquired powers that he renounced). Supernature is not an accumulation of powers; it is what remains — and indeed what *emerges* — after all powers are surrendered.

> He rose to the status of a mahaguru when he renounced his siddhis in the Ganges and became the manifestation of the powers he represented.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.248

The mahaguru's supernature is described as the full expression of the Shiv-parivaar: the OM, trishul, shivling-gileri, Ganpati, Nandi, and jyot manifested as symbols on his body — not decorative, but functional certifications of the powers they represent. See [[concepts/power-symbols]].

## The Central Paradox

The defining formulation of this concept is the inversion Hingori arrives at in the closing section of the chapter:

> The mahaguru's supernature was not limited by his destiny, but he chose to limit his destiny to express his supernature.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.248

This is the answer to the chapter's opening question: *"Why did this spiritual superman design for himself the destiny of a commoner?"* The answer is that the choice of ordinary circumstances was itself an expression of extraordinary nature — not a constraint upon it. Limitlessness choosing limitation is itself the proof of limitlessness.

The Brighu Samhita offers the only available external description:

> "He is like Shiv while in meditation and like Vishnu in his speech. In seva and protection, he is like Brahma, and in relieving people of their pain, he is like Jagdamba. His aura is like the sun, warm on the outside, soft inside, and filled with hidden knowledge. People will not be able to know the real entity behind this human form."
> — Brighu Samhita, cited in *The Guru of Gurus*, p.232

And Gurudev's own claim, confirmed by every account in the chapter: *"We would never know who he really is."*

## Dimensions of Supernature

### Multiple Identities / Form-Changing

The mahaguru was witnessed appearing in different forms by several disciples. He appeared in a five-headed form (with a body bathed in golden light) to Shambhu ji on Mahashivratri 1974 — one of the few times he allowed such a glimpse, and even then, at great cost to the viewer. Other disciples caught partial glimpses:

- Kwatra ji saw his guru's form turn into pure light as he prostrated at his feet
- Rajpal ji saw his head double in size
- Gupta ji of Parwanoo saw a dwarfed form
- Pappu ji (son of Shambhu ji) saw the long-bearded form within minutes of the clean-shaven avatar

See [[stories/shambhuji-five-headed-form]] and [[stories/uddhav-ji-black-triangles]] for the most detailed accounts.

> At best, we saw him as a human being with an advanced spirit rather than an advanced spiritual being in human form.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.235

### Elemental Synergies

Gurudev's connectivity to the elements endowed him with influence over them:

- **Water**: He located the exact spot on his Khandsa farm that would yield potable water when an earlier borewell had failed; converted arid land at Renuka into greenery by digging a water channel. Waves at Juhu Beach and Mahabalipuram moved selectively to caress his feet while others standing nearby were not touched.
- **Rain**: He could will rain to fall or stop. On one Guru Purnima, rain fell to exactly the extent of the queue of visitors — and stopped precisely at the queue's end.
- **Fire**: The Mahagayatri mantra confers the ability to contain fire. Most Mahashivratri nights, the mahaguru dipped his hands in boiling lemon tea to bless it — without a flinch, wince, or blister.

Disciples empowered by him also demonstrated elemental command: Pappu ji of Jwalamukhi was protected from tantrik fireballs by his deceased father Shambhu ji. See [[stories/pappu-shambhuji-flames]].

### Body-Mind Capacities

- **Food**: Could live without food for days. On Mahashivratri, queues lasted two days; he did not eat until he had met them all.
- **Temperature**: Could withstand extreme temperatures; continued in a light cardigan when others shivered in layers.
- **Sleep**: Could put one hemisphere of his body to sleep while the other remained awake and operational. Pehalwan ji observed him riding a scooter with eyes closed — he was doing *paath* at the time.
- **Speech**: A man of few words, yet communicated far more than he spoke. His words had the power to come true. He never said 'NO'. As Ashok Bhalla ji recalls: *"The word NO does not exist in my dictionary."*

### Omniscience of Time

> "When people come to meet me, their past, present and future flashes on a screen in my mind. I can also tell if they have come with faith or just for fun."
> — Gurudev to Das Saheb, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.245

He lived in constant awareness of past, present, and future. He could exhaust karmas, change samskars, and subtract years from a person's future life to add to their present one. See [[concepts/constant-awareness]] and [[concepts/spiritual-healing]].

### Bilocation and Invisibility

He was physically present and absent at the same time. On at least three documented occasions he was seen in two places simultaneously:
- Visiting Sunil ji at his shop in London while seated with Bakshi ji at the Gurgaon sthan
- Conversing at his office and his farm at about the same time
- Attending to a phone call in a locked room in Nagpur while seated with disciples moments earlier

He could also walk through crowds unrecognised at will. In Srinagar, he purchased spare parts from a shop whose owner worshipped his photograph — without being recognised. See [[stories/gurudev-invisible-srinagar-shop]].

If he did not wish to be photographed, shutters froze or reels came out blank. A German photographer who insisted on shooting him two days before Mahashivratri — despite being told "Today, I am not present here" — found all his attempts blank; only the Mahashivratri photographs turned out.

For full treatment of astral dimensions, see [[concepts/astral-travel]].

### Spiritual Mastery

He had command over the world of spirits — helping them move from lower realms to higher ones, granting them human birth when appropriate. He travelled astrally four to five days a week for periods of two to seven hours. He took disciples on astral voyages to Paris, to a raft on an open sea, and across the planets of the solar system. See [[concepts/astral-travel]] for the full account.

He deployed his astral form in service — appearing in disciples' dreams to heal, reveal futures and pasts, transmit mantras, and exhaust karmas in the dream state rather than the physical one. He developed multiple *nirman kayas* (subtle energy bodies) to simultaneously attend to many people.

He allied with advanced spiritual entities to jointly serve life forms, including Shirdi Sai, Guru Nanak, Guru Gobind Singh, Guru Vashisht, Parshuram ji, Lord Krishna, Dattatreya, Ganpati, Hanuman, several Muslim saints, Shiv manifestations, and Devis such as Mahakali, Laxmi, Saraswati, and Renuka.

## Kept Under Wrap

Hingori notes that the chapter deliberately underplays the supernature accounts so the reader does not get distracted from the core theme: the activation and enablement of spiritual transformation.

> He used every power he had in that service but depending on the angle of view, you either saw the ordinary man or the extraordinary mahaguru.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.248

> [!tip] The choice of ordinariness was not modesty — it was precision. The mahaguru calibrated exactly how much of his supernature to express in each moment, and that calibration was itself a supernatual act.

## Nuances

> [!question] Supernature versus siddhi — what is the distinction?
> Siddhis are *acquired* powers that the mahaguru renounced at Har ki Pauri in 1970. Supernature is what emerged *after* that renunciation — it is what he *is*, not what he *has*. The power symbols that appeared on his body are certifications of this nature, not trophies of accumulation. This distinction is made explicit in the chapter's closing lines.
