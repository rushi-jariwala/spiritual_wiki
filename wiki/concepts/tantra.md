# Tantra

## Definition

Tantra is a **spiritual science** — inherently neither negative nor positive. It is a set of practices and methods that work with subtle energy, spirit forces, and consciousness. Hingori clarifies this in *Guru Sutra* Ch.2:

> "Tantra is a spiritual science, and hence, is inherently neither negative nor positive. Infact many tantric rituals are used for self-protection, relieving physical pain and curing ailments. Unfortunately, the word has been badly tainted due to the negative use of its powers by certain practitioners."
> — Hingori, *Guru Sutra*, Ch.2, p.18–19

## Positive Uses

Many tantric rituals are used for:
- **Self-protection** from negative vibrations, spirits, and black magic
- **Physical healing** — relief from pain and ailments
- **Energy work** at crossings and charged locations

Village practitioners across India use *jhaad phook* (dusting-away of disease energy) with lemons, coconuts, and other materials. These are placed at road crossings, which concentrate energy due to the frictional intersection of people's auras.

## Negative Uses

Some practitioners use tantric knowledge to **harm others** — casting spells, blocking good fortune, or spiritually attacking people. This negative use is primarily associated with [[concepts/gunas|tamasic]] practitioners and teachers, who also teach their disciples to monetise such services.

Road crossings can hold negative energy discharged by such practitioners; spiritually weak individuals passing by may inadvertently pick it up.

## Tamasic Double-Edge

Tantra operates like a double-edged sword:
- Wielded with sattvic intent: healing, protection, self-evolution
- Wielded with tamasic intent: harm, control, exploitation

This mirrors the general pattern in Hingori's framework: the same instrument (karma, mantra, intent, aura) can compound positive or negative depending on the doer's guna mix and intent.

## Mantra Vidya — The Science of Mantras

**Mantra vidya** is the branch of tantra concerned with the use of sacred sound. It is one of the three main disciplines of the gurukul curriculum (alongside tantra proper and yantra), and among the most extensively documented in the Hingori corpus.

### How Mantras Work

Mantras generate sound vibrations that add to the aura or energy levels of those who recite them. This applies not only to Vedic mantras but to the mantric traditions of Buddhism, Christianity, Islam, Jainism, and others.

> Golden Principle: "The multiplicity of mantra recitation enables one to connect to the relevant power at the macrocosmic level and awaken the representation of that power in the microcosmic self. Thereby, connecting the outer and inner sources of the same power, within one's self."
> — Hingori, *Guru Sutra*, Ch.3, p.22

Mantras operate on three levels of connection:
- **Deity or concept**: the mantra homes in on the collective power of the being or force to which it is attributed
- **Planet**: mantras attributed to planets connect to their rays — the guru mantra, for example, connects to Jupiter
- **Deceased saint**: the mantra homes in on the saint's energy field, which persists in an ethereal dimension

### Positive and Negative Uses

Positively: heal, defend against spiritual attacks, strengthen the aura.
Negatively: harm and hurt others.

This dual-use is the same double-edge that characterises all of tantra — the polarity is in the practitioner's intent and guna mix, not the method itself.

### Asana Requirements

Each mantra is most effective when recited in a specific asana (seat/posture). Examples from *Guru Sutra* Ch.3:

| Mantra | Required asana |
|---|---|
| Gayatri Mantra | Kusha grass mat or deerskin |
| Mahagayatri | Immersed in water |
| Mahamritunjay Mantra | Animal skin or kusha grass mat |
| Chamunda Mantra | In a forest or on deerskin |
| Others | Cowsheds, during eclipses, etc. |

The logic: asanas are **bad conductors of electricity** — they neither absorb nor discharge mantra energy quickly, so the energy vibrations are retained by the practitioner's body rather than dissipated into the atmosphere. Any dissipation that does occur adds to the aura of the location, or is absorbed by the asana itself.

> In his formative years, Gurudev would wrap himself in a blanket or sheet while sitting to recite his mantras.
> — Hingori, *Guru Sutra*, Ch.3, p.22

(Where animal skin is not available, pure cotton blankets or cotton mattresses serve the same purpose.)

### Fixed Time for Recitation

Gurudev advised keeping a fixed time for mantra recitation. The body clock becomes subconsciously prepared — just as it initiates digestive secretions at regular meal times, it becomes preconditioned to concentrate at the designated mantra hour. See [[concepts/mantra-transfer]] for Gurudev's specific teachings on timing, including the guru-paher (1:15–3:30 am) and the three constants of practice.

### Yantra

**Yantra** — occult mystical diagrams — was the third major discipline alongside mantra and tantra in the gurukul mentorship stage. Like mantra, it is a system of concentrated subtle force; where mantra works through sound vibration, yantra works through geometric form.

### Diminished Efficacy in the Current Age

A significant note from Ch.3: human capacity to mobilise subtle energy has declined over millennia. Ancients could propel weapons and move objects with mantras; in the present age, mantras operate only at the aura level. The macro effects (mind over matter, materialisation) are now "virtually theoretical rather than practical." Hingori attributes this to possible changes in the Earth's axis or gravitational pull — but names no definitive cause.
