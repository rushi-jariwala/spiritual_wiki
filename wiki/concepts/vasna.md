# Vasna

## Definition

**Vasna** are the tendencies or inclinations that arise within consciousness to perform a particular act or karma. They are the mechanism by which stored [[concepts/samskars|samskars]] fructify — the energy that brings a coded impression to the surface at the right moment so that it can be exhausted through action.

> For our past samskars to fructify, when the time comes, there are vasnas or tendencies that arise within our consciousness to perform a particular act or karma. This tendency to do something is purely a mechanism to exhaust the stored karma (in the form of coded impressions or samskar). They are a form of energy in our consciousness, which brings to fore the tendency to perform an act or karma from our prarabdh karmas (designated to fructify in this life) at the right time.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.128

## Nature

If most of a person's samskars lean towards negative impressions, the vasnas arising will be more negative, and vice versa. The person is largely pulled in the direction the data dictates.

> Desires to do acts are fuelled by their own energy. 'I should not have done that, but I just could not help myself' is a common phrase used by one whose vasnas have got the better of him/her.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.128

## Vasna → Desire → Samskar: The Loop

The critical juncture is what happens after a vasna arises:

1. Vasna arises — a tendency to perform a particular act
2. If we **take ownership** of the vasna and **spice it with emotion**, the tendency is perceived as *desire*
3. Acting from that coloured desire generates guilt and pride — both of which re-impress the act as a new samskar

This is the mechanism by which a karma that was merely a result (a fructification of old data) becomes a cause for a future effect. The USB stick is refilled.

> When we take ownership of these vasnas and spice them with emotion that's when a tendency is perceived by us as desire and finally ends up making us feel guilty for having succumbed to those desires.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.128

## Will Power and the Boundary

The teaching does not imply that vasnas are irresistible. Will power — an attribute of the [[concepts/koshas|vigyanamaya kosha]] — can allow the tendency to arise, even overwhelm, without one succumbing to it. One can bear the vasna without acting, or act without emotionally owning the act.

> We can use our will power to avoid things that are inappropriate. We can bear the tendency, allow it to overwhelm us, but not succumb to it.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.133

The use of will power to resist — or of non-doership to act without emotional ownership — both prevent the vasna from converting into a freshly coded samskar.
