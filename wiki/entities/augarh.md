# Augarh

> *"A phantom in the garb of a human / manifesting and multiplying at will. / Eyes bright as rubies, an intoxicating swagger, / he appears wherever he pleases still."*
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.51

## Who / What

**Augarh** is Gurudev's mysterious disciple — the head of the **Aghor sect** — whose spiritual domain spans **Haridwar and its adjoining areas**. He is described as a **realised manifestation of the singular form of Shiv (Shiv minus Shakti)** with the following capacities:

- Manifesting in various human forms at will
- Appearing in multiple locations simultaneously
- Communicating telepathically
- Disappearing instantaneously and without trace

While Augarh was a disciple of Gurudev, **his own followers are not Gurudev's disciples** — a distinct boundary noted explicitly by Hingori.

## Role in the Lineage

Gurudev referred to Augarh as disciples' **elder brother (gurubhai)**, to whom they could turn for help in spiritual matters:

> Gurudev referred to Augarh as his disciples' elder brother, to whom they could turn for help in spiritual matters.
> — paraphrased from Ravi Trehan ji's account, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.52

Many of Gurudev's disciples received his support when they requested it. **Shobha ji** (Surender Taneja ji's wife) used Augarh as a "personal genie" to grant mundane wishes. However, some disciples could not connect with the abstractness of his unmanifested form and thus could not benefit from his spiritual might.

## Physical Manifestation

When Augarh appeared in human form, Hingori describes him as:
- Dressed in white (kurta-pyjama and turban, or covered in a white sheet)
- Grey-haired
- With **large red eyes** and a penetrating gaze
- A **confident, drunken swagger** in his gait
- Capable of chanting mantras — including the exact mantra being recited mentally by another person

He met Gurudev in human form on visits to Haridwar. Encounters at other locations were often unannounced and of short duration — he would appear, fulfil the spiritual purpose, and vanish.

## Notable Encounters

- **Har ki Pauri (Haridwar)** — bent to touch Gurudev's feet at aarti; disappeared with Gurudev into the crowd; touched Mataji's feet as a mark of respect without her awareness. See [[stories/augarh-har-ki-pauri]].
- **Laxman Jhoola (Rishikesh)** — appeared as a white-sheet-clad man on a bench at a ghat; stared at Shobha ji with red eyes; simultaneously placed hands on Surender ji's son's feet as Gurudev placed hands on the boy's head; vanished before Surender ji emerged from the Ganges. See [[stories/augarh-laxman-jhoola]].
- **Mataji's hometown** — appeared to Hingori as a drunk and dishevelled man with a bicycle, chanting the exact mantra Hingori was reciting mentally; circled the house all night guarding where Mataji was staying. See [[stories/hingori-augarh-mataji-town]].
- **Asthal, Dehradun** — appeared in response to Hingori's mental invitation; drank no refreshments; stated he was "there to honour an invitation"; younger disciples followed him out and found he had vanished. See [[stories/augarh-laxman-jhoola]] and [[stories/hingori-augarh-tea]].
- **Telepathic tea guidance** — guided Hingori step by step via thought-waves to brew perfect tea for a late-night guest, while Hingori was alone in Gurgaon and Augarh was physically absent. See [[stories/hingori-augarh-tea]].
