# Buddhe Baba

> *Gurudev's mysterious spiritual mentor. Omnipresent, identity undisclosed. "The teacher had a teacher, someone old, wise and true. He called him Buddhe Baba and despite countless excavations, that was all we ever knew!"*

## Who / What

**Buddhe Baba** is the enigmatic spiritual guide whom Gurudev described as "an omnipresent part of his and our lives." Gurudev was always quick to attribute his work entirely to Buddhe Baba:

> He was not the doer and all credit for his seva belonged entirely to his spiritual mentor.
> — paraphrased from Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, pp.47–48

When disciples asked intensely profound questions, Gurudev would sometimes say he would need to "confirm with Buddhe Baba" before answering — treating the mentor as a living, accessible oracle even after his own advent as mahaguru.

Gurudev also refused more garlands than his guru wore:

> "I don't want more garlands on my neck than my guru."
> — Gurudev, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.47

He never revealed Buddhe Baba's identity, leaving room for conjecture. **That conjecture was never settled.**

## First Meeting

Sometime before Gurudev gave up his siddhis in 1970, he set up camp in a remote hamlet in **Madhya Pradesh**. While chanting mantras in the forest near the camp, he came across an old sage at a deserted temple. The exchange of a pinch of tobacco began a chain of events that changed everything. See [[stories/buddhe-baba-deserted-temple]].

## Appearances

Buddhe Baba appeared not only to Gurudev but to several members of his family:

- **Gurudev's younger sister** — appeared as a reddish-light-encased form of an elderly sage in white, with a radiant face, long grey hair, and a thin beard; reassured her about the meaning of Gurudev's words and then vanished. See [[stories/buddhe-baba-sister-vision]].
- **Mataji** — Gurudev was overheard in the middle of the night conversing with "someone he addressed as Baba"; he ended the conversation abruptly when Buddhe Baba informed him that Mataji was overhearing them.
- **Renu ji (Gurudev's daughter)** — home sick and alone, saw a grey-haired man in white emerge from the storeroom opposite her room and vanish; covered herself in fear; Mataji later suggested it may have been Buddhe Baba.
- **Puran ji** (in dream state) — saw Buddhe Baba distributing food grains to the needy.
- **Roopal ji** (in dream state) — saw his photo on a wall of a sthan; described the same physical characteristics as Puran ji, despite the two never having met or spoken.

## Identity — Unresolved

Two theories circulate:

1. **A realised manifestation of Shiv** — consistent with Gurudev's own hints and with the high calibre of the guidance provided.
2. **Sitaram ji of Benaras** — the guru of Gurudev's initial spiritual mentor, Sitaram ji of Dasua. Sitaram ji of Benaras was an accomplished saint who — like Jesus and Guru Nanak Dev — *went seh sharir after death:* he dematerialised his physical body by reconfiguring its atomic structure, a feat accomplished by only a few spiritualists in history. Given this elevated stature, he qualifies to be called Shiv, which compounds rather than resolves the confusion.

> Gurudev's hints at Buddhe Baba being Shiv only compound the confusion. Since the mahaguru never disclosed Buddhe Baba's identity, we can only guess but never be sure.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.50

> [!question] The identity of Buddhe Baba is an intentional mystery left by Gurudev. Speculation is invited but no answer is authorised.
