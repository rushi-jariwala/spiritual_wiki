# Hingori

> *The author of the Hingori Sutras. A disciple of Gurudev whose personal transformation began with being cured of rheumatoid arthritis in a single minute.*

## Who

Hingori is the author (often with co-authors, e.g. Hannah on *Dream Sutra*) of the **Hingori Sutras** — a series of books distilling the teachings of his guru, **[[entities/mahaguru|Gurudev]]**, into practical frameworks on karma, the soul, dreams, and the guru-disciple relationship. The books are published by Pali Hills Tourist Hotel Pvt Ltd (Le Sutra) and distributed in India through Bennett, Coleman & Co. (Times Group).

## Biographical Notes (from Ch.1 of Karma Sutra)

### The Arthritis Cure

Hingori suffered from **rheumatoid arthritis for 10 years** before meeting Gurudev. Gurudev cured him in a minute. This single event is the hinge of his spiritual life:

> I thank my stars for my suffering from arthritis for 10 years, as it became the reason for my turn around from a one-way ticket to hell (metaphorically speaking) to a flexi-pass that could take me everywhere.
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.1, p.13

He describes the experience as his **initiation into the spiritual realm** and into a "karmically wealthier existence." See [[stories/hingori-arthritis-cure]].

### Before Gurudev — A Self-Described "Disbeliever"

> Seeing philosophy come to life, made it easier for a disbeliever like me, to subscribe to him as a master and become enthralled by the subject.
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.1, p.16

Hingori does not present himself as a natural devotee. He calls himself a disbeliever whose direct experience of healing — and of watching 30-year-olds heal people from various ailments after only a short association with Gurudev — was what made the philosophy real to him.

### Role in the Lineage

Hingori is a **second-generation disciple** and part of the network Gurudev built — over 500 spiritual healers across three generations. He notes that it "came naturally to me to pass on his gift of power to many younger people who showed both devotion and spiritual potential" — implying his own role in transmitting abilities onward. He looks forward to a fourth generation.

### Family and Personal Context

From the stories in Chapter 1, we know:
- **Married.** His wife's difficult childbirth features in one of the guru's-umbrella stories (see [[stories/gurus-umbrella-wife-delivery]]).
- **Has a sister** — in the late 80s / early 90s, living in New Jersey, USA, where Moses is said to have appeared to her in vision form several times.

## Personal Spiritual Practice

Hingori describes a distinctive practice around [[concepts/doer-ship|doer-ship]]:

> Personally, I seem to get almost instant results and reactions for the deeds I do. (I may not claim doer-ship.) Whenever I have too much enjoyment or pleasure, the pain is not far behind. In such a case I go through a balancing act... I have opted to not allow joy to be a dominating emotion in my mind and thus bought an insurance against the emotion of sorrow.
> — *Karma Sutra*, Ch.1, p.25

This is not pessimism — it is a disciplined refusal to *own* either pleasure or pain, so that neither compounds.

## Voice and Method

- **Plain, practical prose** — Hingori writes like a layman explaining his own working model, not like a scholar citing scripture
- **Stories as evidence** — his method of argument leans heavily on first-person narratives of grace and miracle
- **Business metaphors** — karma as "balance sheet," "profit and loss," "assets and liabilities" — reflecting a practitioner grounded in commercial life rather than monastic seclusion
- **Ecumenical** — names gurus from every major tradition (Indian, Greek, Chinese, Islamic, Jewish, Christian) as legitimate teachers of the same truth

## Appeal to the Reader

From the book's author's appeal:

> I feel blessed with spiritual luck beyond expectation; maybe beyond justification, and certainly beyond my aspirations. One of the outcomes of possessing extreme wealth is the desire to share.
> — *Karma Sutra*, preface

The Sutras are framed as this sharing.
