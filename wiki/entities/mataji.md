# Mataji (Sudesh Sharma)

> *Gurudev's wife. Called Mataji — "mother" — by his disciples. The Shakti to his Shiv.*

## Status

**Partial.** Key biographical content comes from "The Family Man" (Ch.2, pp.26–45), not yet ingested. This page covers what is established in Ch.1 and the source page overview.

## Who

Gurudev's wife, born Sudesh Sharma, became known reverently as **Mataji** (mother) among his disciples. She married Gurudev in 1960 — he was in his early twenties, she was twenty years old, from a well-respected family in Ludhiana, Punjab. She worked as a schoolteacher and helped run the household while Gurudev devoted his free time to seva.

## In Chapter 1

Her name appears in Ch.1 in one context: Hingori records that Mataji believed the miraculous recovery from illness at Baba Balak Nath — when Gurudev was around ten years old — "left an indelible mark on him and may have triggered his quest for spiritual knowledge." See [[stories/gurudev-illness-baba-balak-nath]].
