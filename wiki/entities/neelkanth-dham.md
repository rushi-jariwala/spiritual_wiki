# Neelkanth Dham

## Who / What

Neelkanth Dham is Gurudev's *samadhi* — the site of his physical interment — located in **Najafgarh**, on the northern outskirts of Hastinapur as defined in the larger geographic boundary. Gurudev chose to leave his physical body on **28 July 1991** at the age of 53.

> Gurudev decided to leave his body in 1991, at the age of 53, to continue his spiritual pursuits in the afterlife. He chose July 28th to depart from this earthly realm. His mortal remains were interred at Neelkanth Dham, his samadhi in Najafgarh.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.111

## Significance

Gurudev himself oversaw the construction of his final resting place and **prophesied** before his death that those who visited the Dham would be relieved of their suffering. Hingori attests that this has been borne out: Neelkanth Dham is described as a spiritually charged location where people continue to be helped and healed, and many claim to have sighted Gurudev there decades after his passing.

## The Brighu Samhita Corroboration

In August 1991 — shortly after Gurudev's death — three of his devotees (Narinder ji, Virender ji, and Saroj ji) visited the custodians of the Brighu Samhita in Saharanpur for a reading on Gurudev. The ancient sage Brighu had predicted their arrival for this very purpose thousands of years prior.

The Samhita stated:

> "On the snow-capped elevation of Mount Kailash, in a spiritual dimension called Shiv Lok, live hundreds of highly evolved beings who are ganns of Shiv. These ganns descend to the earthly realm to carry out covert missions. They keep their identity and purpose hidden during their time on earth. They return to their spiritual abode when their earthly mission is completed."
> — Brighu Samhita, cited in *The Guru of Gurus*, p.111

The reading described Gurudev as a *divya aatma* (enlightened spirit) who lived an ordinary life, never revealing his true identity — which was *Shiv swaroop* (manifestation of Shiv). It further states that in his metaphysical form, he **returns to his Dham in Hastinapur's northern outskirts daily to serve**. The larger boundary of Hastinapur referenced by the Samhita includes modern-day Najafgarh. See [[stories/gurudev-brighu-samhita-after-death]].

## *Aatma Sutra* Corroboration

[[sources/aatma-sutra]] Ch.VIII identifies Gurudev's "afterlife headquarters" as Najafgarh — consistent with the Dham's location — and notes that he "brought along hundreds of beings from higher loks to accomplish their joint mission." See [[entities/mahaguru]] and [[concepts/loks]].
