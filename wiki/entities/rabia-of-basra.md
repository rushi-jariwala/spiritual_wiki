# Rabia of Basra

## Who

Rabia of Basra (c. 714–801 CE) is one of the most revered saints in the Sufi tradition. Hingori introduces her in *Aatma Sutra* Ch.XI as the exemplar of self-acceptance under impossible conditions: sold into slavery, forced into prostitution during a famine, yet refusing to blame herself. Her sustained prayer drove her into *dhyaan*; her master freed her after witnessing an ethereal lantern of light suspended above her head. She went on to become a great Sufi saint.

## Role in the Hingori Sutras

Hingori uses Rabia to make the case that self-acceptance is not contingent on circumstances being fair or favourable. She represents the highest form of the teaching: the confidence in oneself and faith in the divine even when every external condition points to shame and worthlessness.

> "Despite the odds, she had confidence in herself and faith in the power she worshipped. Can we not emulate her self-confidence, or attempt to better it?"
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.XI, p.134
