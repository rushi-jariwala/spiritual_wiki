# Sitaram Taki Ji

## Who / What

Sitaram Taki ji is an imposing personality and one of Gurudev's devoted disciples. He worked for the **Bank of America** but, as Hingori puts it, personally banked on Gurudev. He was always willing to help, serve, and contribute. He is notable in *The Guru of Gurus* as the most frequent witness to Gurudev's astral departures — and as a healer who assisted at the Renuka seva camp.

## Notable Stories

### Two Astral Departures — Eyewitness

**Haridwar (1970s).** On Gurudev's instruction, Sitaram ji pressed his feet and was told to accompany the mahaguru on an astral journey. Gurudev wrapped himself in a sheet, began *paath*, and Sitaram ji watched as a sheet-wrapped astral form emerged from Gurudev's physical body, while the seated physical form remained. Sitaram ji was so frightened he wet his pants. The astral form eventually returned and merged. See [[stories/sitaram-taki-haridwar-astral]].

**Hariana (date unstated).** During a night meal of sarson ka saag and makki ki roti, Gurudev's head fell back with a sudden jerk. Feeling no pulse or breath, the disciples in the room panicked and poured desi ghee over his body. Sitaram ji, remembering that Gurudev had mentioned returning from astral meetings by 3:30 am, persuaded the group to wait. Gurudev revived shortly after 3:30 am, stood, walked to the bathroom, and returned: *"What have you people done to me? I'm stumbling all over the place because I've got desi ghee all over my body!"* He then roared with laughter. See [[stories/gurudev-sarson-saag-astral]].

### Renuka Healing

At the Renuka camp (1980), Sitaram ji assisted in healing a young girl who could not stand. On Gurudev's advice, he stood on the girl's toes while another disciple lifted her by her hands. The girl stood and walked for the first time. Her parents wept with gratitude. See [[stories/renuka-disabled-girl]].
