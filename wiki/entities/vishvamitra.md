# Vishvamitra

## Who/What

Vishvamitra is one of the most celebrated *rishis* (sages) in Indian scripture, given the rare title of *brahmarishi* — the highest level of spiritual accomplishment. He is a central figure in the *Ramayan*, serving as the guru who brings Ram and Lakshman to face their first great challenges.

## In Aatma Sutra

Hingori cites Vishvamitra in Ch.XII as a companion example to [[entities/valmiki]] in the **Phase A-to-Z** teaching:

> "The *Brahmarishi* Vishvamitra, once an arrogant king, felt slighted by Guru Vashisht. In order to seek revenge, he did penance to achieve the same attainments as Guru Vashisht. He failed several times but finally made it to the title of *brahmarishi* (the most advanced in spiritual practices). So, which one was he — the arrogant king or the accomplished guru?"
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.XII, p.153

The tension between Vishvamitra and [[entities/guru-vashisht]] is particularly rich: the very man Vishvamitra sought to defeat or equal became the measure against which his own transformation was calibrated. The rivalry, initially driven by pride and a desire for revenge, became the engine of one of the greatest spiritual ascents in Indian mythology.
