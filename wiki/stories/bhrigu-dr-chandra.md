# Bhrigu's Prophecy — Dr. Chandra's Family

## Full Narrative

> Rishi Bhrigu, one of the seven great sages created by Brahma, who lived thousands of years ago, predicted the future of people living today. Dr. Chandra, a close friend of my father's, once visited Bhrigu Nadi at Hoshiarpur in Punjab, to know the future of his family. At the time of the reading, his son was studying at a film institute. The Bhrigu Nadi predicted that his son's career would be a failure, with no material future at all. When his son graduated from the film institute, he bagged a lead role in a film. The film was produced by a well-known producer, who had several successes to his credit. Dharmendra (a famous leading Bollywood star at the time), also had a prominent role in the film. Despite high expectations, the film bombed at the box office. The young man was far more good looking than many actors of his time but was not destined to be a star. He was only offered a few roles in small plays thereafter. He now teaches song and drama in a school in South India. A film star he did not make, and Bhrigu predicted that thousands of years ago.
>
> Dr. Chandra's elder daughter was incredibly shy of men and refused to meet potential suitors for marriage, despite her parent's desperate attempts. I persuaded her to meet a handsome acquaintance, but after reluctantly meeting him, she turned down his proposal. Bhrigu had however predicted that she would have two children. Eventually, at the age of 39, she married a man who already had a son from his first wife. They had a child of their own, and Bhrigu had hit the bulls eye again.
>
> Dr. Chandra called my mother one year and told her he was going to die in August. Since he was physically fit and suffered from no major ailment, my mother refused to believe him. But Bhrigu was right again; he died of natural causes in August that same year.

— Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.V, pp.52–53
