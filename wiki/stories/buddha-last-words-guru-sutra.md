# The Buddha's Last Words — On Teaching and Proximity

Verbatim passage cited by Hingori in *Guru Sutra* Ch.6, pp.61–62, in the section on judging one's spiritual progress. Hingori frames it as the Buddha's way of directing his disciples toward self-enquiry — assessing whether they have lived by the teachings — rather than by external markers or the guru's physical presence.

## Text

> "The teachings I have given you, I gained by following the path myself. You should follow the teachings and conform to their spirit on every occasion. If you neglect them, it means you have never really met me. It means that you are far from me even though you are actually with me, but if you accept and practice my teachings then you are very close to me, even though you are far away. It is the nature of all formed things to dissolve. Strive with diligence. The true Buddha is not a human body: it is Enlightenment."
> — The Buddha, last words to his disciples, cited in Hingori, *Guru Sutra*, Ch.6, pp.61–62

## Source

*Guru Sutra*, Chapter 6 — Journey Towards Disciplehood, pp.61–62.
