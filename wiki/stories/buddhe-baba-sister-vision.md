# Buddhe Baba Appears to Gurudev's Sister

**Source:** Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, pp.49–50
**Related entities:** [[entities/buddhe-baba]], [[entities/mahaguru]]

---

> His younger sister was one of the few people who claimed to have met his enigmatic spiritual guide. Once, she was overcome with emotion when she overheard Gurudev telling his disciples that they would only understand his teachings after he had 'gone away'. Interpreting his words as a portent, she was disturbed at the prospect of a life without him. As she lay sobbing on his bedroom floor, she noticed a reddish-light stream through an opening in the wall. Encased in light, the form of an elderly sage robed in white with a radiant face, long grey hair, and a thin beard was discernible. Gurudev's sister sat up, startled! The elderly sage asked her why she was upset. Sobbing, she quoted her brother's words to his disciples. The sage told her that Gurudev was probably talking about the official camp he would leave for in a few weeks. Hearing this, she felt a wave of relief wash over her. Shortly after reassuring her, the sage vanished. When she shared this experience with her brother, he told her she had seen Buddhe Baba.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, pp.49–50

---

## Significance

This account establishes several details that complement the [[stories/buddhe-baba-deserted-temple|deserted temple encounter]]:

- Buddhe Baba appears unsolicited, in response to emotional distress — not at Gurudev's behest but apparently out of independent concern for Gurudev's family.
- His physical description — white robes, radiant face, long grey hair, thin beard, encased in reddish light — would later be independently matched by Puran ji and Roopal ji in their dream-state encounters, despite none of them having met each other.
- His reassurance is gentle and practically worded: he reframes "gone away" as a reference to an upcoming work camp. Whether this is literally true or a kindness is left open.
- Gurudev's confirmation ("you have seen Buddhe Baba") is the clearest identification of the figure by Gurudev himself — without revealing any further detail about who Buddhe Baba actually is.
