# Guru Sutra — Chapter 1 Closing Poem

**Source:** Hingori, *Guru Sutra*, Ch.1, p.8 (closing verse)

---

> We are blind with seeing eyes
> believing we're what we are not.
> He lifts the veils of untruths
> and frees us from the rot.

— Hingori

---

The verse closes Chapter 1 ("Concepts of Guruism"). "Blind with seeing eyes" is avidya in compressed form — the senses are functional but their output is maya, not absolute reality. "Believing we're what we are not" names the false identity: the jivaatma mistaking itself for the body, the role, the name. The guru's action is described as lifting veils, not installing knowledge — removing obstruction rather than adding information. "Frees us from the rot" echoes the decay that comes from sustained delusion.

Closely related to the Chapter 1 definition: "a guru is the dispeller of avidya and unveiler of maya." See [[concepts/guru-disciple]], [[concepts/maya]].
