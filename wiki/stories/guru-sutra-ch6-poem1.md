# Guru Sutra Ch.6 — The Pride Verse

Verse appearing mid-chapter in *Guru Sutra* Ch.6 (p.50), in the section on spiritual vanity and self-importance as obstacles to disciplehood.

## Text

> By blowing the trumpet to satiate pride,
> By allowing the littleness to grow tall,
> By befriending the chicanery of self-importance & praise,
> you've set the soul up for a mighty fall.
> — Hingori, *Guru Sutra*, Ch.6, p.50

## Related Concepts

- [[concepts/guru-disciple]] — the second and first obstacles to disciplehood: spiritual vanity and desire for self-importance; the verse names the mechanism (littleness allowed to grow tall) and the consequence (a mighty fall)
- [[concepts/kleshas]] — asmita ('I-ness') as the root; the verse is Hingori's poetic rendering of Patanjali's asmita klesha applied to the spiritual path
- [[concepts/samskars]] — pride and self-importance, once entrenched, deepen into samskar grooves that slow spiritual progress
- [[concepts/aura]] — boasting depletes the aura's energy; shakti spent on self-aggrandisement cannot be accumulated for spiritual service
