# Guru Sutra Ch.6 — The Self-Judgement Verse

Closing verse of the *Judging Oneself* section in *Guru Sutra* Ch.6 (p.58), cautioning against harsh self-assessment on the spiritual path.

## Text

> The scales will tip one way or another.
> The sea will surge and the tide will ebb.
> Harsh self-judgement is a trap
> so steer clear of its treacherous web.
> — Hingori, *Guru Sutra*, Ch.6, p.58

## Related Concepts

- [[concepts/guru-disciple]] — Judging Oneself section: growth is non-linear; the disciple must not evaluate themselves by a single cross-section in time
- [[concepts/self-acceptance]] — the verse is a poetic expression of the self-acceptance teaching: oscillation (scales, sea, tide) is the nature of growth, not evidence of failure
- [[concepts/destiny]] — the tipping scales and surging sea are images of destiny's oscillation — the same pendulum logic that makes harsh self-judgement a category error
- [[concepts/prarabdh-karma]] — the ebb and flow imagery maps directly onto the fructification of prarabdh: positive and negative karmas alternate, neither state is permanent
