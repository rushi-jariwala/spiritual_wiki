# Gurudev's Birth and the Cobra

## Full Narrative

> One spring morning, Gurudev's mother placed her infant son in the crib on the terrace of their house, only to return a few minutes later to find a hooded cobra seated on him. As she stood paralysed with fear, the cobra slid away and disappeared through a crevice in the wall. Unsure of what the cobra sighting meant, Gurudev's parents consulted a temple priest who told them that it was an excellent omen, indicative of the boy's exalted future.
>
> A similar prophecy was made by a sadhu (ascetic) who visited Gurudev's house a few years later. He informed Gurudev's mother that her son would become a powerful saint, 'someone like Shiv', when he turned thirty-five.

— Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.8
