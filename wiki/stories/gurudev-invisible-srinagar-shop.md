# Gurudev Goes Unrecognised in the Devotee's Shop — Srinagar

**Source:** *The Guru of Gurus*, Supernature — Body, Mind & Beyond, pp.243–244

## Full Narrative

> Becoming invisible was a yogic kriya the mahaguru had mastered. He could walk through crowds and not be recognised if he did not will it. Once while in Srinagar, Rajpal ji and Gurudev went spare part hunting for their jeep, which had broken down. They entered a shop only to notice the shop owner lighting a dhoop to the mahaguru's photograph, paying his respects. Seeing this, Rajpal ji whispered to his guru that it could be difficult to buy from the shop since it belonged to a follower. The owner would refuse to accept a fee for the products, and the guru would not take anything for free. Gurudev looked at Rajpal ji and said, "He will only refuse to accept payment if he recognises us." Sure enough, the great guru purchased the products without being recognised by the very man who worshipped him. It was an unhurried, no-fuss transaction!
>
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, pp.243–244

## Context

This story is offered as one of several demonstrations of the mahaguru's mastery over invisibility — described in the chapter as a *yogic kriya* (conscious spiritual action), not a trick or sleight of hand. The invisibility is not physical disappearance; it is the withdrawal of the quality that makes him recognisable. The shopkeeper could see him, interact with him, and complete a transaction — but could not connect the face before him with the face in the photograph above the counter.

The episode also illuminates Gurudev's consistent refusal to receive anything for free. His wish not to burden a devotee was precise enough that he solved it by adjusting his own perceptibility rather than by avoiding the shop. This is characteristic of his approach: no drama, no drama avoidance — just a quiet, internal adjustment.
