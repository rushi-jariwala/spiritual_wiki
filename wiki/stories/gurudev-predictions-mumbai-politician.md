# Gurudev's Predictions â€” Mumbai Floods and the Politician

## Full Narrative

> Gurudev's predictions were the most unique. Around 30 years ago, he predicted that Mumbai would be flooded, with water levels rising alarmingly high, resulting in many deaths. We all know about the Mumbai floods in 2005, around two decades after he passed on.
>
> One of my college friends and I, often met Gurudev together. He was a chartered accountant with a small practice, but with a large and magnanimous heart. Gurudev would often tell him, "I will make you a world-famous leader and one of India's admired politicians". We would be thrilled to hear these words but since he was not a member of any political party then, the chances of this prediction coming true seemed faint. Years later, he rose to become the chairman of a public limited company. Three months before the general elections, he was offered a seat by a political party. He went on to be appointed a cabinet minister for several years, holding many important portfolios. He is now invited internationally to speak on various platforms and is admired for his humility and honesty. He acknowledges Gurudev's grace, lives simply, and never takes advantage of his high position. He knows what he is today was exactly what he was meant to be, hence the humility. He does his utmost to fulfil his duty and goes out of his way to be kind to his junior workers. He considers his job an opportunity to serve people.
