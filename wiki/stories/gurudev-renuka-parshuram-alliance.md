# Gurudev and Parshuram Ji Form a Spiritual Alliance at Renuka

## Full Narrative

> Renuka, a small town in the Sirmaur District of Himachal Pradesh, is believed to be part of the spiritual domain of Parshuram ji, the immortal son of Renuka Devi and her husband, Saptarishi Jamadagni.
>
> In 1980, Gurudev set up camp in Renuka on a plot owned by a local politician, Shri Chandramani Vashisht. After settling in, Gurudev asked Bittu ji to clean and refurbish an old shivling situated on the campsite.
>
> Vashisht ji posed the question to the mahaguru when he returned a few days later: he had been seeing images of various deities flash sequentially in meditation, always ending with the image of a bespectacled man in pants and a shirt. Gurudev showed him the OM on his hand after serving him a cup of tea. On seeing the luminous symbol, he prostrated at Gurudev's feet. "Son, I was supposed to come here in 1970, but I arrived in 1980," Gurudev said with a smile. "You've been waiting for me for ten years!"
>
> Gurudev is said to have had spiritual meetings with Parshuram ji before beginning seva at Renuka. In 1980, they formed an alliance that resulted in the establishment of a sthan. Vashisht ji, a disciple of Gurudev and Parshuram ji, took over the reins. Before his death, he appointed his disciple, Dinesh ji, to carry the baton of seva. It is believed that Parshuram ji still visits the sthan to keep an eye on things.
>
> In addition to Parshuram ji and Renuka Devi, Gurudev set up camps in many places and formed alliances with the deities who ruled there. These include Khawaja Moinuddin Chishti, Shri Markandeshwar, Baba Balak Nath, Ganpati, the Sikh Gurus (Guru Nanak Dev, Guru Gobind Singh, and Guru Angad Dev), Hanuman ji, the Ashvin Kumaras, Dattatreya, Sai Baba of Shirdi, Indra, and possibly many others.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, pp.77–81
