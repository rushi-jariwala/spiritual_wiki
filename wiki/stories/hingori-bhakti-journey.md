# Hingori's Four-Year Bhakti Journey

**Source:** *The Guru of Gurus*, pp.151–152

> My transformation was aided by tenuous efforts at mind-control and letting go of vices. At the end of this period, Gurudev gave me the responsibility of opening and running a sthan in Mumbai, saying, "Now you are ready to share gyan and make others do seva."

**Context:** Gurudev's instruction — *"To conquer happiness, you must conquer sorrow"* — set off Hingori's four-year tryst with bhakti. His quest to conquer duality soon demolished his personality. He went from being a flamboyant, proud, and popular man with a gift of the gab to becoming reticent and serious, rarely allowing himself to have fun, behaving more like a social recluse than the showman he had once been. As he started losing his identity, applause gave way to under-confidence.

He recited mantras for almost six hours every day and became *siddh* in a few. The powers of the Shiv-parivaar appeared as symbols on his hands — attainments bestowed by Gurudev that qualified those four years as years of fulfilment. At the stage of *bhakti*, the mind of the practitioner enters sensory objectivity and emotional unaffectedness, freeing itself of intellectual interrogation — and enlightenment begins to express itself.

At the end of the period, Gurudev gave Hingori the responsibility of opening and running a sthan in Mumbai.
