# Hingori's Closing Poem — "I Am the God to Whom We All Pray"

**Source:** *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.XIV, p.188
**Author:** Hingori

## Full Poem (verbatim)

> *I am the God to whom we all pray,*
> *Though I may not know of it today.*
>
> *I am the creator of this universe immense,*
> *Though of it I can't really make sense.*
>
> *I am above the truth, beyond the lies,*
> *Though I don't know how to feel it despite several tries.*
>
> *I am all, tat twam asi they say,*
> *Though I can't sense it, there must be a way.*
> *I believe faith will take me to the other shore,*
> *Universal love and empathy will make me self-adore.*
>
> *When finally maya gets a real pounding,*
> *My intuitive awareness will find grounding.*
> *The truth I will see and reality feel,*
> *My God in the mirror will finally unpeel.*
> *Now worship the me or worship thee,*
> *Let enlightenment happen and showcase the we.*
> *Let enlightenment happen and showcase the we.*
>
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, p.188

## Context

This poem closes the body of *Aatma Sutra*, appearing after the three self-worship exercises and the Life Scorecard introduction. It is Hingori's personal *tat twam asi* — spoken in the first person, with full acknowledgement of the gap between knowing the truth intellectually and sensing it experientially. The refrain "Though I may not know of it today" / "Though I can't sense it" is characteristic of Hingori's honesty: he does not claim to have arrived; he claims to be on the path.

**Structure of the argument in verse:**
- I *am* the divine — but cannot sense it yet
- Faith, love, and empathy are the bridge
- When maya is overcome, the God in the mirror is revealed
- The endpoint is not "I" but "we" — enlightenment as collective

The final line repeated — *"Let enlightenment happen and showcase the we"* — is the book's last word on the ego/self question: the goal of self-worship is not self-aggrandisement but dissolution into the we.
