# The Crow as Morning Alarm — Hingori's Premature Son

**Source:** *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.XIII, pp.165–166

## Full Narrative

> When my son was born premature, he had to be kept in an incubator for several days. My wife stayed in the hospital to breast-feed him at 6:15 every morning. She often got late, and the nurses felt it was not good for the baby. Then, out of providence came a strange morning alarm. A crow would arrive at 5:45 every morning and knock with its beak on the windowpane. It would keep knocking until my wife awoke.
>
> My late mother-in-law, a light sleeper, accompanied her in the room so that waking up was easier. Once the ladies had woken up, the crow would stop knocking and fly away. But it never missed a single day of being the morning alarm. How do you explain that? How does a crow know when it is time to knock? And that too at sharp 5:45 am?
>
> As it was a miraculous birth, where the nurses and doctors did not have much hope for the baby's survival, I realised there were a few spiritual forces at play. The crow was probably one of them. Some power may have heightened the level of consciousness of the crow. Or taken over the body of the crow for its purpose.

## Related Concepts

Hingori uses this story — alongside the dog-temple story — to argue that the ceiling of consciousness is not fixed for any creature. If a crow's awareness can be heightened by a spiritual force to serve a precise function (knock at 5:45am, stop once the task is done), the same ascent is possible for humans. The question becomes: if animals can surpass their nature, what is stopping us?

- [[concepts/self-worship]] — animals as faith-builders; if a crow can ascend, so can we
- [[concepts/aura]] — spiritual forces can inhabit or elevate animal consciousness momentarily
