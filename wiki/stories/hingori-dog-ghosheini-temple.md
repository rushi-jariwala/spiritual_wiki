# The Dog Leads Hingori to the Temple — Ghosheini

**Source:** *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.XIII, pp.166–168

## Full Narrative

> We had gone for a family trip to Himachal Pradesh with my brothers-in-law and their kids. We stayed at a unique hotel by the river in a hamlet called Ghosheini, two hours away from Kullu. We had to cross the river in an apple cart to get to the hotel. On the third or fourth day of our stay there, I felt heaviness around my head, as if there was some external energy present in my hotel room. It made me constantly uncomfortable. In the vicinity, there was a dog that lazed all day and night except when it was hungry. It was like a stuffed toy that moved occasionally. One evening, when everyone had gone for a jeep ride, I decided to stay back and investigate the reason behind the heaviness. I was standing in the garden, when the dog suddenly came to life, exhibiting a surge of energy. It stood in front of me, as if asking me to follow him. I wondered if my mind was playing games or if the animal was actually communicating with me. There was only one way to find out and so I followed the dog.
>
> He led me to the path where we used to go for a walk every evening. We reached a fork in the road where the left path went around the hill, and the other, towards a village. I wanted to take the left path, but the dog would not follow. He waited by the other path and stared at me. So, I gave up and followed him. When we nearly reached the top, from where the village was visible, the dog jumped over a bush and disappeared. I spotted a couple of village girls and asked them if there was a temple nearby. They said there was one about 20 steps to the left. I followed their directions and came to a huge banyan tree with a temple built around it. Surprisingly, the same dog was sitting there, as if waiting for me. On seeing me, it disappeared again, leaving me alone at the temple.
>
> I sensed the temple was trying to communicate with me, so I listened with my mind. It asked if I would collaborate with it and serve people in the neighbourhood and in return, it would help as well. I agreed, provided the temple would facilitate my service by helping me procure some land in that location. A few minutes later I left. The dog was waiting down the road to escort me back, minus the hyper energy. The heaviness around my head had vanished too.
>
> We were checking out of the hotel the next day and the owner refused to accept the bill. After a lot of coercing, he accepted only half the amount. No explanations why. Perhaps he liked our faces!
>
> We went back to our homes and forgot about the incident, but the temple did not forget us. Two years later, while in office, my partner randomly asked me, "Would you like to buy land in Ghosheini?" He further explained that an army colonel wanted to sell his land in the quaint village. The tree certainly knew how to reach me.

## Related Concepts

Hingori's conclusion: "Can a lazy dog suddenly become a messenger between one spiritual energy and another? It must have had to reach a certain level of consciousness, far beyond the level of a lazy dog." The story is told in the chapter on self-worship as an illustration of how faith is built — through unmistakeable, observable evidence that spiritual intelligence operates through ordinary channels (animals, chance encounters, hotel bills, business partners).

The temple's follow-through two years later — sending the land offer through a business partner — demonstrates what Hingori calls an "exchange of services" with a spiritual intelligence: the tree knew how to reach him.
