# Hingori's Visits to the Loks

## Source

*Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VIII, pp.93–94

## Full Narrative

> Unfortunately I have not visited all these loks in my present state of consciousness. I have visited only three or four at the present level of my awareness and consciousness. As I recall, on one lok, I was not walking, but in Padmasana, floating above the ground, and it seemed to be a very bright, but a very rustic place, and not much aesthetically speaking. On another occasion, I remember visiting another lok, which had a similar topography and was similarly bright, where I witnessed many saints walk across from one place to the other. I assume these are experiences of higher loks. I reflect upon an experience of visiting a darker lok, where I met my grandmother, who saw me, recognised me but decided not to acknowledge me, showcasing to me the significance of role-play. During the life we shared on Earth, she was the most loving grandmother one could hope for. But in the afterlife she treated me as just a number and disregarded recognition and relationship. Strange but true the people she was sitting with in that lok all wore a colour code toga of dull green. To take this point further I saw a lot of evolved souls who descended to earth with Gurudev wearing a slightly cream colour toga. This makes me suspect that people who live in the different loks probably have colour coded clothes created by their minds.
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VIII, pp.93–94
