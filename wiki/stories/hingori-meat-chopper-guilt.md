## Full Narrative

> Thirty years ago, I was sitting in a locked room with a meat chopper in my left hand, planning to chop off my right hand. I had slapped a domestic worker for being rude and insulting. Having chosen the spiritual path, I felt guilty for my action. I was convinced that chopping off my hand was a fair and well-deserved punishment for my behaviour. I was emotional and, in a daze, thinking of the possible outcome, when all of a sudden, I heard a voice in my ear. The voice said, "Who are you to blame me? You are my painting. I am the artist. By blaming yourself, you are blaming me." I was shocked. I felt guilty about being guilty. I realised I am what I am because this is what my destiny meant me to be. If I am guilty, then my destiny is guilty.
>
> Does the acceptance of destiny mean that this book advocates, or even encourages, you to do whatever you like, and thereafter conveniently blame it on the consciousness supreme? Well, people who want to take the path of convenience would certainly take it as such. But, definitely not those who follow the path of acceptance.
>
> The advocacy here is acceptance. I accept that my destiny created my fit of rage, which resulted in me slapping the domestic worker.
>
> Acceptance of destiny means acceptance of the fact that destiny is the sole creative force for our actions. Therefore, we ought not to take ownership or doership of our actions. Sometimes, we forget that there is also the destiny of the other person at play. The domestic worker referred to above, may have been fated to go through the misery of being ill-treated. I may have only been the pawn for the fulfilment of his destiny, but because I took ownership of the act, guilt slid in through that crevice.
