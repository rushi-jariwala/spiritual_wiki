# Hingori Misses the Astral Tour — Puranji's Call

## Full Narrative

> My astral travel started with Gurudev telling me casually that he would take me for a long out-of-body tour. Gurudev asked me to go to sleep while he lay down doing *paath* (focused *mantra* practice), that was when he would travel astrally. While I was asleep, his emissary, Puranji, appeared in my dream state and said, "Gurudev is calling you". Limited by my physical orientation, I did not realise that I should take the help of the emissary, walk out of the body, and follow him. Instead, I forced myself to physically wake up and go to his room. There I found Puranji massaging Gurudev's feet whilst he had left his body. I missed my chance of astral travel that night.
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.VI, p.67

## Source

*Aatma Sutra*, Chapter VI: Astral Travel, p.67

## The Brahmaand Offer — *The Guru of Gurus* Version

The SUPERNATURE chapter (*The Guru of Gurus*, p.246) provides a more specific version of the same night. Before going for *paath*, Gurudev told Hingori: *"Let me take you on a journey of the Brahmaand."* Hingori notes: "Brahmaand didn't mean going to the moon and back. It was the whole milky way." Hingori calls the missed opportunity a failure of recognition — not understanding that Puranji appeared in astral form and that he was meant to exit his own body to follow. The *Aatma Sutra* version describes the same mechanics without the Brahmaand destination; the *Guru of Gurus* adds the intended scope of the journey.
