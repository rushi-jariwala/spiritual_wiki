# Hingori at Renuka — Attacked by the Son, Saved by the Mother

## Full Narrative

> During my days of spiritual immaturity and adventurism, I remember once trying to absorb power from a temple owned by one of Gurudev's disciples in Renuka. While praying at the altar I sensed the power attack me. I felt uneasy and could not get rid of it. I sat in the car and decided to leave. I felt something destabilising my mind and body and a heaviness around my head. I knew from past experiences that I was under attack by some powerful force.
>
> Unable to sit in the car any longer, I got out and walked towards Renuka Lake. The uneasiness had subsided since my feet were on the ground and the negative energy attacking me may have been partially transmitted to it. Nonetheless, it persisted. Not being able to think of an alternative, I prayed to Devi Renuka (the mother of sage Parshuram) and asked her to protect me from the attack. What happened next was unbelievable. It was like watching a television screen on my forehead. I saw a beautiful woman (I assumed it was Devi Renuka), dressed in white, adorned with ornaments and with a crown on her head. She looked glorious. She touched my head and within a moment the heaviness disappeared. The attack was averted. I felt normal again. I have preserved the holy water of Renuka Lake in my place of worship as a connection with the charming and magnanimous Devi. I learned the golden rule that energy was better earned than stolen.
>
> Attacked by the son, saved by the mother!
>
> When I returned to Gurudev's camp, I sat like a mouse at the rear. He walked upto me and confirmed that I had been punished by Parshuramji because I had erred.
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.X, pp.119–120
