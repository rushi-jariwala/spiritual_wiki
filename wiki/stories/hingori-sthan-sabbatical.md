# Hingori's Forced Sabbatical — Declining the Sthan

## Full Narrative

> I was one of the sevadaars in the main sthan in my city. Gurudev asked me to start a sthan in my house. I felt awkward about starting a sthan of my own, wanting to avoid the significance. I humbly requested him to allow me to continue to do seva at the main sthan. Being a spiritual bowler, he bowled another googly! And masterminded the circumstances that led to my forced sabbatical for three long years. Three years later when he repeated his request, I jumped to it with alacrity.
> — Hingori, *Guru Sutra*, Ch.6, pp.49–50

This episode was preceded by a parallel incident involving Tonyji:

> Tonyji shared a story with Darius in which Gurudev had asked him to start a sthan in Kashmir three decades ago. For reasons best known to him, he had declined. Today his sthan is where his boots are.
> — Hingori, *Guru Sutra*, Ch.6, p.49

## Source

*Guru Sutra*, Ch.6 — "Journey Towards Disciplehood," pp.49–50
