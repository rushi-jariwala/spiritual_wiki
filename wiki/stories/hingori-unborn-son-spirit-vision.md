# Hingori's Vision of His Unborn Son's Spirit

## Full Narrative

> A few months before my wife got pregnant, I encountered my yet-to-be-born son's spirit body. Judging from his clothes, I gauged that he belonged to the 19th century. On awakening from a state of *dhyaan* or meditation, I saw my unborn son's spirit form, sitting on the floor, gazing at my wife's feet. It was a scary vision, as it was my first experience of seeing a ghost with my eyes open.
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.II, p.17

## Source

*Aatma Sutra*, Ch.II — "Different Kinds of Bodies," p.17

## Related Concepts / Entities

- [[concepts/sukshma-sharir]] — the spirit body is visible in its pre-birth waiting state; the clothes suggest the spirit carries identity markers from its previous incarnation
- [[concepts/mahurat]] — the spirit is waiting before birth, consistent with the model of deferred rebirth until the right planetary moment
- [[entities/hingori]] — Hingori's first experience of seeing a spirit with open eyes, outside the dream state; occurs during *dhyaan*

---

*Guru Sutra* Ch.6 adds a different angle to this same encounter. Where *Aatma Sutra* focuses on the sight itself (the spirit in 19th-century clothes at his wife's feet), Ch.6 focuses on the consequence for the father-son role-play:

> I saw my son's spirit a year before he was born. His spirit was that of an old man, and hence, I find it hard to mollycoddle him and see him as who he is in physical form. I can clearly see the role-play that is required of me but I find it difficult to play it convincingly.
> — Hingori, *Guru Sutra*, Ch.6, p.57

See [[concepts/role-play]] — the Ch.6 section on emotional neutrality. Knowing the soul's true nature makes the parental performance harder to sustain, not easier.
