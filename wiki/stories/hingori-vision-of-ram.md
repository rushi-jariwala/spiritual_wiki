# Hingori's Vision of Ram — Offering Good Wishes

**Source:** *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.XIII, pp.160–161

## Full Narrative

> A few years later, I got a vision in the early morning hours that I was at a gathering where around hundred people were waiting to pay their respects to Lord Ram, Sita, and Lakshman. When it was finally my turn, Lord Ram asked me what I desired. I surprised myself by saying I wanted nothing and had come to offer them my good wishes instead. Sita thanked me with a broad smile. Lord Ram and Lakshman acknowledged me too, but with wry grins.
>
> I woke up shocked. What was I, 'Mr. Ordinary', doing offering good wishes to someone who is a god to millions? Thereafter, I started understanding that I was a part of the divine, which in reality, existed within me. I started identifying more with that part of me, than with the one that had been making an idiot of itself for most of its life.
>
> If you could identify with the divinity in you, you would not only identify with your physical being, which is but the outer layer of the whole configuration of you, but you in the entirety.

## Related Concepts

Hingori categorises this as a **Category 1 dream** — where the interaction shows that the dreamer's unconscious considers itself qualified and significant enough to stand as an equal before Ram, Sita, and Lakshman. The offering of good wishes (rather than asking for a boon) reflects a self-perception of equality, not supplication.

The wry grins of Ram and Lakshman suggest gentle approval — they recognise the shift in Hingori before he does. Only after waking does the shock arrive: "What was *I*, 'Mr. Ordinary', doing offering good wishes to someone who is a god to millions?"
