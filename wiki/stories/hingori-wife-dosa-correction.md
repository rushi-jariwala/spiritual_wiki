# Hingori's Wife — Three Years of Dosas

## Full Narrative

> When my wife complained about my frequent, late-night spiritual gossip sessions with my gurubhais, he ensured that for three years from that day, I came to Gurgaon for Bada Guruvar and other Thursdays, but instead of seva, I took my wife and kids for a dosa snack. Seva of the wife instead of seva at the sthan! With one googly, he had me for a golden duck.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.128

## Source

*The Guru of Gurus*, Entrepreneur Extraordinaire — People Management, p.128

## Context

Gurudev received Hingori's wife's complaint — presumably astrally or through the disciple network — and responded not with sympathy for Hingori's spiritual enthusiasm but with a redirection: the wife's well-being was itself a form of seva. For three years, Hingori served his family instead of serving at the sthan, without being told the reasons explicitly. The correction was delivered through a structure, not a lecture. Hingori reflects on it with the cricket metaphors he deploys throughout this chapter: a "googly" he never saw coming, ending in a "golden duck" — out first ball, no runs.
