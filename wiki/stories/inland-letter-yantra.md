# The Inland Letter with a Death Yantra

**Source:** *The Guru of Gurus*, p.180

> Once I was alone with him at the sthan when an inland letter arrived for him. His expression grew stern as he looked at it, and he lay the letter on the bed, instructing me to leave his room and wait outside. When he summoned me after about five minutes, the torn letter was lying on the bed, assembled into a jigsaw puzzle in a manner that visibly exposed a yantra meant to take his life. He was aware of the letter's contents but did not want me to see it before he opened it because he believed it would endanger me. A practitioner of negative tantra had tried to harm or kill the mahaguru with his black art, but by failing, the tantrik gave us an example to learn from. Gurudev's vigilance helped him thwart deadly attempts.

**Context:** The incident occurred when Hingori was alone with Gurudev at the sthan. Gurudev knew the letter's intent before opening it — his expression changed as he merely looked at it. He shielded Hingori from potential danger by sending him out of the room. The torn letter, reassembled as a jigsaw on the bed, revealed the yantra-based intent to take his life. The attempt failed because Gurudev's awareness neutralised it before it could act.
