# Kapill's Vision of Shiv in Himself

**Source:** *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.XIII, pp.158â€“159

## Full Narrative

> Much before Kapill started coming to the *sthan*, the general manager at his father's office asked an astrologer the characteristics of his boss and the boss' son (Kapill). On being told the astrologer's views, Kapill agreed with the assessment on his father. However, the astrologer had also predicted that in his thirties, Kapill would have a dream of Lord Shiva. Kapill soon forgot this.
>
> A few years later, Kapill, by then a *sevadaar* (one who serves other beings) at the Mumbai *sthan*, while meditating, saw a vision of Shankar (the manifested form of Shiv). Awestruck, as he continued to gaze at Shankar, he realised he was actually looking at himself. He was bewildered and speechless.
>
> Later, on deciphering Kapill's vision, his guru told him that there was no difference between Shiv and him since the same consciousness supreme existed in everyone.
