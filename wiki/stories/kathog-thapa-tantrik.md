# Thapa the Tantrik Tests Gurudev at Kathog

## Full Narrative

> Thapa, a tantrik who lived in a small shack across the road from the dorm, was sceptical and suspicious of the events. He was convinced that the man who dazzled the people of Kathog by displaying an OM and trishul on his hand was an illusionist. Thapa decided one night that it was time to call the illusionist's bluff. He dispatched a powerful spirit under his command to instil the fear of god in Gurudev. Then he waited for the spirit to return and grew agitated as seconds turned into minutes and minutes into hours.
>
> When the sun rose, he went to the dorm to figure out what had happened. Gurudev invited him inside when he noticed him peering through a slightly ajar door. When Thapa cautiously entered the room, the mahaguru laughed and said, "You need to stop taking these silly tests!". Thapa realised right then and there that Gurudev was no illusionist. He begged his pardon, replacing doubt with devotion.
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, pp.73–75
