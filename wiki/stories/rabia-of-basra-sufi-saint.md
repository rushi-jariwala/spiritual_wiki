## Source

## Full Narrative

> Rabia of Basra was sold as a slave when she was young. When a famine hit Basra, her master forced her into prostitution to make extra money. She, however, had a great love for the divine and prayed so long and hard that she entered into a state of *dhyaan*. One day, Rabia's master saw her with an ethereal lantern suspended over her head, radiating light. Awestruck, he freed the young girl. Rabia went on to become a great Sufi saint. Could she have reached such an evolved state by blaming herself for being an enslaved prostitute? I doubt it! Despite the odds, she had confidence in herself and faith in the power she worshipped. Can we not emulate her self-confidence, or attempt to better it?
