# Rajpal Ji's Hoarse Voice — Ego in Seva

## Full Narrative

> To teach Rajpal ji a lesson for showing off his singing skills during seva, Gurudev made his voice so hoarse that he could hardly utter an understandable word. A few months later, when he again sought forgiveness from his guru, Gurudev asked him to eat a ball of snow to reclaim his vocals. Rajpal picked up some snow from the ground he was standing on and ate it. The cure was almost instantaneous.
> — *The Guru of Gurus*, p.228

## Source

*The Guru of Gurus*, Healing — Modes of Healing, p.228.

## Notes

The story is paired in the text with other examples of Gurudev giving "anything he felt like to cure another" — in this case, snow from the ground. The cure was as unconventional as the correction. Together, the two actions form a single arc: the correction was precise and targeted; the cure was equally precise once genuine contrition was established.

The months-long duration of the hoarse voice indicates this was not a momentary reprimand but a sustained correction — Rajpal had to live with the consequence until he sought forgiveness with genuine intent.
