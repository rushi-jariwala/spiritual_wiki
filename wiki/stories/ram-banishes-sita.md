## Full Narrative

> Another popular story from the Ramayan is one where a washerman questioned Ram's decision of accepting his wife Sita after she had been held captive by Ravan, king of Lanka. When her chastity was questioned, Ram asked her to leave the kingdom. She lived in a forest under the patronage of the Sage Valmiki (the author of the epic Ramayan). Ram's decision did not make him look wise as a king or sensitive as a husband, did it?
>
> Ram was beyond *maya* and hence beyond guilt. He realised if he did not do what was expected out of him, then the people in his kingdom would resort to immorality. He punished Ravan for abducting his wife but had no malice towards him. Nor did he have any guilt for banishing his wife to the forest.
>
> Everyone is entitled to their own take on Ram's decisions. Personally, I never had much respect for him. I found him unworthy of worship. But after reading the teachings of Guru Vashisht, I understood Ram's unique perspective and why he did what he did. And I learned to respect him (I have not forgotten that the divine spark in Ram is no different from you or me; the only difference being the stages of refinement, timing, level, and destiny).
