# The Disabled Girl Who Walked at Renuka

## Full Narrative

> Among the many people who came to Renuka was a young girl who couldn't stand without support. Her parents, who lived in a remote village, walked the steep path to Renuka while carrying their disabled daughter on their backs. They pleaded with Gurudev's disciple, Sitaram Taki ji, who was attending to the queue in which they stood, to heal their child. Sitaram ji sought his guru's guidance. Following the mahaguru's advice, Sitaram ji stood on the girl's toes while another disciple, RP Sharma ji, held her hands and lifted her forcefully. The girl stood up and walked gingerly for the first time, leaving everyone speechless. Her parents sobbed joyfully and thanked the healers, saying, "Aap ka bhala ho!" (May destiny smile on you!).
> — Hingori, *The Guru of Gurus*, p.80
