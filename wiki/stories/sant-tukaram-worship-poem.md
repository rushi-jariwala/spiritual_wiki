# Sant Tukaram — Worship Reaches Through Any Image

**Source:** *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.XIV, pp.173–174
**Original Author:** Sant Tukaram (Marathi saint-poet, 17th century)

## Full Verse (verbatim)

> "I made an earthen image of Shiva, but the Earth is not Shiva;
> My worship reaches Shiva, the Earth remains the Earth it was.
> I made a stone image of Vishnu, but the stone is not Vishnu;
> My worship reaches Vishnu, the stone remains what the stone was.
> I made a pewter image of Amba, but the pewter is not Amba;
> Amba receives my worship,
> Though a pewter that pewter remains.
> Even so are saints worshipped, the worship reaches the Lord,
> the saint is but His servant, an instrument, a conduit pipe."
> — Sant Tukaram, quoted in *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.XIV, pp.173–174

## Context and Use

Hingori quotes Tukaram immediately after prescribing Exercise 1 (photograph worship) and the "nine billion doorways" teaching. The verse provides the classical Marathi devotional grounding for Hingori's argument: **the image is a conduit, not the destination**. The worship passes through the earthen/stone/pewter medium and reaches the divine; the medium remains unchanged. Similarly, the photograph of yourself is not the object of worship — it is the conduit pipe between the physical appearance and the consciousness supreme at the other end.

> "Through this photograph (of yourself) you have done what software engineers do. You have outlined a path between the physical appearance in your photograph and the consciousness supreme at the other end."
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.XIV, p.174

The closing line — "the saint is but His servant, an instrument, a conduit pipe" — extends the analogy: just as the saint is a conduit for divine worship, the self (in its physical form) is a conduit for the inner divine to receive reverence.
