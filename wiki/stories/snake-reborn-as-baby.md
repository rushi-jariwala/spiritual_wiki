# The Snake Reborn as a Baby

**Source:** *The Guru of Gurus*, p.199 (Social Hygiene)

> Gurudev acceded to a snake's request for freedom from the clutches of a tantrik who had enslaved it. The mahaguru had the snake killed and ceremoniously buried before allowing it to take human birth. When a couple who had visited Gurudev in Renuka to request a child returned a year later with their newborn, the mahaguru summoned Bakshi ji, a disciple, to see the child. Bakshi ji recognised the baby as the former snake because of the hood marks on her back. Reminiscing, he says that she was the most beautiful baby he had ever seen!

**Context:** A tantrik had enslaved a snake — presumably via black art binding. The snake's spirit, aware enough of its situation to communicate a request, appealed to Gurudev for freedom. Gurudev's method was to kill and ceremoniously bury the snake, releasing its spirit from the enslaved body and allowing it to take human birth. The couple from Renuka who had been granted a child by Gurudev became the vehicle for this birth. The hood marks on the baby's back were the physical identifier that allowed Bakshi ji to recognise the continuity.
