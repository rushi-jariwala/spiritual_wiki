# The Sore Throat and the Gol-Gappas

## Full Narrative

> For example, when a lady with a chronic sore throat approached him for help, he asked her to snack on spicy gol-gappas. While she did so hesitatingly, the sheer act of following his advice restored her voice to its original decibels and made her a staunch believer. Awe was a sentiment he inspired in millions.
> — *The Guru of Gurus*, pp.227–228

## Source

*The Guru of Gurus*, Healing — Modes of Healing, pp.227–228.

## Notes

The prescription of spicy gol-gappas for a chronic sore throat is the textbook counterintuitive cure. Medical convention would call spicy food an aggravant for a sore throat. The cure demonstrates that Gurudev sometimes healed by doing "precisely the opposite of what any logical mind would think should be done." The lady's hesitation — she did so *hesitatingly* — makes the outcome more remarkable: the act of trust, however tentative, completed the circuit.
