# The Reincarnation of Thuksey Rinpoche

## Full Narrative

> The public ceremony to formally acknowledge the 5-year old Thuksey Rinpoche in Ladakh, as a reincarnation of his past life as Lama Thuksey Rinpoche, was akin to a king's coronation with all its grandeur and protocol. This is hard to believe, but at the age of 33, Thuksey Rinpoche, managed to get back the property that belonged to him in his previous life! He got it transferred to himself through a legal process.
> — Hingori, *Aatma Sutra*, Ch.II, p.16–17

## *Guru Sutra* Ch.5 — Extended Account (pp.39–40)

Meera, a dedicated devotee of a Buddhist order, narrated the fuller version to Hingori. In his previous life, Thuksey Rinpoche had founded a school in Leh. His disciple in that life was **Gyalwang Dhukpa**. After Thuksey Rinpoche's death and reincarnation as a child, he was mentored by Gyalwang Dhukpa in this life — the former master is now a student of his former student. The courts also transferred the school's land to Thuksey Rinpoche in this life, accepting the legally filed reincarnation claim.

> Strangely the disciple, is a disciple of his own disciple of the past! Fabulous mind twister, isn't it? To shock you further, would you find it easy to believe that even the courts have transferred the school land to Thuksey Rinpoche, accepting the fact that he is the reincarnation, of his former self, and therefore, the former landowner!
> — Hingori, *Guru Sutra*, Ch.5, pp.39–40

Hingori uses this story to illustrate that **chronological seniority in a spiritual order means nothing** — the reincarnated Rinpoche had more spiritual attainment than his current-life teacher by virtue of what was carried forward. See [[concepts/guru-disciple]] (Spiritual Hierarchy vs. Chronological Hierarchy).
