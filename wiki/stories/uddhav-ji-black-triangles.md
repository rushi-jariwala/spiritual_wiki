# Uddhav Ji and the Black Triangles

## Full Narrative

*First account* — *The Guru of Gurus*, Entrepreneur Extraordinaire — People Management, pp.127–128 (brief):

> When Uddhav ji saw two black triangles with yellow eyes float into his room, he grabbed his nunchaku to lash out at them. Gurudev later informed him that he had to appease the accompanying triangular deity so that Uddhav ji would not have to pay the price for the nunchaku aggression with his life.

*Second account* — *The Guru of Gurus*, Supernature — Multiple Identities, p.235 (expanded):

> When Uddhav ji attempted to use his nunchaku on two black triangles with yellow eyes that entered his room through the window, his hand froze, and he passed out. When he awoke the next morning, he decided to inform the mahaguru. "First you invite me to visit you, and then when I do, you try to attack me," his guru commented. The mahaguru implied that he was one of the triangles and went on to tell Uddhav ji that he had to negotiate with the accompanying triangular form for his devotee's life since the nunchaku aggression had not been well received!

## Source

*The Guru of Gurus*, two passages: Entrepreneur Extraordinaire, pp.127–128 and Supernature — Multiple Identities, p.235.

## Context

The SUPERNATURE version adds three details absent from the earlier account: Uddhav ji's hand froze (he could not complete the attack), he passed out, and he woke the next morning to tell Gurudev. Gurudev's comment — *"First you invite me…then try to attack me"* — is the fullest form of his reply, revealing that he was himself one of the two triangular forms. He then had to negotiate with the accompanying entity (the second triangle) for Uddhav ji's life, since the nunchaku aggression had been taken as an act of hostility.

This story documents Gurudev appearing in a non-human geometrical form to visit a disciple — one of several instances in the SUPERNATURE chapter of the mahaguru manifesting beyond his ordinary human form. See [[concepts/supernature]].
