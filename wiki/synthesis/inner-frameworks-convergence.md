# The Convergence of Inner Frameworks

*Available sources: Karma Sutra (fully ingested). Pattern identified after Ch.7.*

---

After Ch.7, four separate frameworks in the Karma Sutra each describe a three-stage inward progression — from body-identified, ego-attached existence toward witness-consciousness and liberation. The alignment across them is precise enough to suggest these are not separate practices or doctrines, but different lenses on a single underlying movement.

| Framework | Stage 1 (Lowest) | Stage 2 (Middle) | Stage 3 (Highest) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gunas** | Tamas | Rajas | Sattva → beyond gunas |
| **Koshas** | Annamaya (food/body) | Manomaya (mind) | Anandamaya → moksha |
| **Attitude to Deeds** | Self-centred | Satisfaction | Detachment (*karma mukta*) |
| **Non-Doership** | Full doer-ship | Partial witness | Full witness (citta-level) |

## What the pattern shows

Each framework maps the same axis: **the degree to which the 'I'-sense owns experience**.

- **Stage 1** — identity fused with the body and its appetites; karma binds freely; samskar loop runs at full speed
- **Stage 2** — partial disentanglement; the mind and intellect are engaged but still claim outcomes; some karmic binding continues
- **Stage 3** — the 'I' recedes to the witness position; karma cannot attach; the samskar loop breaks

The upper bound in each framework — beyond gunas, beyond the koshas into moksha, *karma mukta*, full non-doership — converges on the same state: the jivaatma no longer registering ownership of experience.

## Implications

This convergence has a practical consequence: elevation in any one framework should produce movement in the others. A person working on their guna mix (more sattva, less tamas) is simultaneously refining their kosha engagement and loosening doer-ship, even if they do not frame it that way. The frameworks are overdetermined descriptions of one process, not parallel tracks.

> [!tip] Sattvic is not the destination. It is penultimate. All three frameworks point beyond their own highest stage — the gunas must be transcended entirely, not merely optimised toward sattva.
