# The Samskar Loop — Mechanism of Karmic Perpetuation

*Available sources: Karma Sutra (fully ingested). Pattern drawn from Ch.8, pp.127–134.*

---

Ch.8 of the Karma Sutra provides the mechanistic underpinning for why the karmic cycle perpetuates across lives. It is not merely a moral accounting system — there is a specific loop by which each act generates the conditions for its own repetition.

## The Four-Link Loop

```
[Samskar] → [Vasna] → [Act plays out] → [Doer-ship re-encodes act] → [New Samskar]
```

1. **Samskar** — a coded impression stored in the [[concepts/kaarna-sharir|kaarna sharir]] (the USB stick analogy) fructifies as a tendency
2. **Vasna** — the tendency surfaces; the [[concepts/prarabdh-karma|prarabdh karma]] plays out; the act was *only ever designed to exhaust old data*
3. **Doer-ship** — the 'I'-sense claims the act, colours it with ownership, pride, or guilt
4. **Re-encoding** — the act is logged as a new [[concepts/samskars|samskar]]; the USB stick is refilled

The critical insight: the act itself is neutral — it is the exhaustion of prior karma. The *loop closes* only because of step 3. Without doer-ship, the act would exhaust the samskar and leave no residue.

## The Background Force: Asisa

Running beneath the loop is [[concepts/asisa|asisa]] — the jivaatma's composite will to perpetuate. Assembled from:

- **Asmita** — the 'I am the body/mind' identification
- **Abhinivesha** — the clinging to continued existence
- **Avidya** — ignorance of the soul's true nature

Asisa is not the loop itself; it is the voltage that keeps the loop running. It is why the loop reasserts even when the practitioner intellectually understands non-doership.

## The Break

> Non-acceptance of our deeds is not 'skin deep'. The observer within us cannot be fooled by the mind and record the impression at the level of the citta.
> — Hingori, *Karma Sutra*, Ch.8, p.132

The break must occur at the level of **citta** (the totality of conscious, subconscious, and unconscious mind) — not merely the surface intellect. Surface intellectual agreement with non-doership does not prevent the citta from re-encoding the act.

Four tools that operate at this depth, per Hingori:

| Tool | How it breaks the loop |
|---|---|
| **Gyan** (knowledge/realisation) | Dissolves avidya; achieves aham brahmasmi; burns accumulated sanchit |
| **Seva** under a guru | Builds intuition; creates channel for gyan to descend |
| **Samadhi** | Operates directly on the citta; erases samskars |
| **Mantra siddhi** | Samskar-erasure tool under guru direction |

> [!tip] The loop is not broken by good behaviour — it is broken by removing the 'I' from behaviour. Karmic plastering (accumulating positive karma) is the service road; non-doership at the citta level is the highway.

## Implications for Practice

The karmic worksheet (daily audit, CCTV principle) targets step 3 — it trains the practitioner to *notice* the moment of doer-ship rather than letting it run automatically. It does not break the loop at the citta level, but it weakens the habit of claiming ownership, which over time may create conditions for deeper non-doership.
